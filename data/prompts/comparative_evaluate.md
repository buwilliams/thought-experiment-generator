Evaluate the following outputs. They were all generated by the same lens applied to different problems. Evaluate them together so your scores are calibrated against each other — a score of 0.8 on one output should mean the same thing as 0.8 on another.

Lens: {{lens_summary}}

For each output, score four dimensions from 0.0 to 1.0:

**consistency** — Is the output internally self-consistent? Does it contradict itself or rely on incompatible premises? Note: consistency is necessary but not virtuous. A vague output that commits to nothing cannot contradict itself — that earns no credit. Score only whether the claims actually made are mutually compatible.

**hard_to_vary** — Are the output's claims load-bearing? Apply substitution tests on specific named elements: if you replaced a component with its negation or a generic alternative, would the conclusion change? Components that survive substitution are decorative, not load-bearing. Outputs with generic language that could describe almost anything score near 0, because their components can be freely swapped without consequence.

**explanatory_reach** — Does the output commit to specific claims about cases the problem did not raise — claims specific enough that they could be false in those adjacent cases? Generic assertions of broad applicability do not count. Genuine reach names an adjacent case and makes a claim about it that could independently fail. Score near 0 for outputs that make only local claims or merely assert breadth without committing to anything in adjacent cases.

**resistance_to_refutation** — Attempt to construct a specific falsifying case for each output. Score near 0 in either of two cases: (a) you found a specific case where the output's claims fail, or (b) the output never committed to anything specific enough to be falsifiable — vagueness is not robustness, it is emptiness. Score near 1 only if the output makes specific claims that survived a genuine falsification attempt.

Also extract candidate problems from each output: unresolved tensions or unexplored implications that are specific enough that a subsequent conjecture could be wrong about them. Generic open questions ("what are the broader implications?") score near 0. Score each candidate 0.0–1.0.

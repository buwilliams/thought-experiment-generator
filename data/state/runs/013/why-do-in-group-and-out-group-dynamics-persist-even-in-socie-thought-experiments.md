## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** In-group/out-group dynamics persist in anti-tribal societies because “rejecting tribalism” does not remove the underlying selection problem of social coordination; it merely relocates group-boundary formation to a higher-order criterion: *who is properly non-tribal*. The attempted abolition of tribal identity becomes a new identity filter.

**Hypothetical isolating scenario:**  
Imagine a society that sincerely abolishes all inherited markers of group membership: ethnicity, religion, class signaling, nationality, even partisan labels. Its explicit norm is universalism: no one may favor “their own.” Assume this norm is widely accepted and enforced.

Now isolate the key variable: people still must decide whom to trust, imitate, cooperate with, forgive, promote, and protect under uncertainty. Those decisions cannot be made on universal goodwill alone, because action requires discrimination: some people are more reliable, more aligned with shared norms, more predictable. So the society needs a live criterion for social selection.

If old tribal markers are forbidden, the discriminating criterion shifts to signals of commitment to anti-tribal norms themselves: openness, inclusivity, tolerance, procedural fairness, correct language, proper cosmopolitan attitudes. Those who display these signals are treated as safer cooperators; those who do not are treated as risks. Thus a new in-group forms around “people who relate to others in the right universalist way.”

Follow the logic strictly: once that criterion matters, people begin tracking it, performing it, enforcing it, and teaching it. Deviance becomes morally salient precisely because tribalism is officially repudiated. The out-group is no longer “foreigners” or “heretics” but “the exclusionary,” “the backwards,” “the insufficiently enlightened,” or “those who make identity salient.” Anti-tribalism therefore does not end boundary-making; it moralizes and abstracts it.

What this illuminates is that group dynamics are not primarily caused by primitive taste for tribal labels. They are produced by a deeper structural necessity: finite agents must compress social complexity by sorting others into categories relevant for coordination and threat management. Any society that requires norm enforcement will generate distinctions between those inside and outside acceptable cooperation. Explicit anti-tribal ideology can change the *content* of boundaries while leaving the *function* intact.

At the extreme, the more completely a society defines itself against tribalism, the more strongly it may police tribal residues, creating sharper prestige hierarchies around moral purity. So the persistence of in-group/out-group dynamics is not necessarily evidence of hypocrisy. It is evidence that abolishing one kind of group marker does not abolish the need for social filtration. The system regenerates boundaries from whatever variable remains legitimate.

So the collision of perspective and problem suggests: **tribalism persists not because societies fail to reject it hard enough, but because any stable social order must solve coordination by distinguishing trusted from mistrusted participants; anti-tribalism simply supplies a new basis for distinction.**

## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Dominant power structures self-perpetuate not mainly through secret coordination, but through a **strange loop of self-reference** in which institutions, norms, identities, and incentives continually regenerate one another. People at different levels of the hierarchy act locally and often sincerely, yet their actions return the system to its own premises: what counts as merit, legitimacy, competence, risk, and normality is defined by structures already shaped by prior power.

The loop works like this:

1. **Power organizes the environment** — schools, hiring criteria, laws, media frames, professional norms, networks.
2. Those environments shape what agents perceive as reasonable, moral, and advantageous.
3. Agents then make “independent” choices within that frame — whom to trust, promote, fund, protect, defer to.
4. Those choices reproduce the very environment that made them seem natural.

So the system does not need explicit collusion because the **reference point for action is already encoded by the system itself**. Beneficiaries need not consciously defend dominance; they merely follow scripts that feel objective. The hierarchy “thinks through” them.

What is illuminated by the strange-loop perspective is that self-perpetuation is not just top-down coercion or bottom-up habit. It is a **recursive closure**: the system produces subjects who recognize the system’s outputs as reality, then act in ways that reproduce those outputs as evidence of the system’s rightness. Privilege becomes proof of merit; exclusion becomes proof of unfitness; stability becomes proof of legitimacy. The hierarchy validates itself by interpreting its own consequences.

At sufficient social complexity, this creates an emergent property: **impersonal domination**. No single actor needs to hold the whole plan, because the whole is reproduced through distributed self-modeling. Institutions model “what society needs”; individuals model “what successful people are like”; marginalized groups may even internalize the same model. The structure persists because its descriptions of reality feed back into reality.

An extreme case clarifies the mechanism: imagine a society where every gatekeeping rule is formally neutral, and every actor rejects prejudice. If access to preparation, credibility, safety from error, and inheritance remains uneven, then “neutral” evaluation still loops back to prior advantage. The cleaner the local procedures look, the easier it is for the global structure to disappear from view while continuing to reproduce itself.

So the deepest claim is: **dominant power persists when a hierarchy becomes self-describing**. It no longer relies primarily on overt conspiracy, but on recursive social cognition — institutions and persons taking the outputs of historical power as the inputs of present judgment. The leverage point, therefore, is not merely exposing bad actors, but interrupting the loop: changing the system’s self-descriptions, measures, defaults, and pathways by which it converts inherited advantage into apparently natural order.

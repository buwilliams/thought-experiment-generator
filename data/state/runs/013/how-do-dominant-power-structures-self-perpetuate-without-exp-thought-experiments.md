## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Dominant power structures can self-perpetuate without explicit collusion when the social environment is structured so that *individually reasonable adaptation* reliably reproduces the same hierarchy.

Construct the limiting scenario: imagine a society in which no beneficiaries coordinate, conspire, or even consciously endorse the system as a whole. Each person simply responds to incentives, risks, norms, and available information in ways that protect their local position. Assume also that institutions remember past decisions through rules, standards, reputations, networks, and resource distributions. Now ask: what follows?

What follows is that power can persist as an emergent property of the system rather than as a plan. Beneficiaries do not need to agree with one another; they only need to face similarly structured choice environments. If hiring managers minimize uncertainty by selecting familiar credentials, lenders prefer already-legible borrowers, media amplify already-visible voices, and ordinary people defer to what already appears authoritative, then each actor is solving a local problem. But collectively they generate a feedback loop: prior advantage becomes evidence of merit, evidence of merit justifies further access, and further access deepens prior advantage.

The key variable isolated here is not malice or ideology but **path-dependent legibility**: the extent to which existing power makes itself easier to recognize, trust, and reproduce. Once this variable is high, non-collusive reproduction becomes likely. People navigating uncertainty select what the system has already marked as safe, competent, normal, or prestigious. That selection then updates the environment for everyone else.

This suggests that self-perpetuation works through distributed filters rather than centralized control. Institutions inherit biased inputs; decision-makers inherit institutional defaults; newcomers adapt to what is rewarded; dissenters bear higher costs; beneficiaries can sincerely deny coordinated domination because none is required. The structure does the coordinating.

At the extreme, explicit anti-collusion norms may coexist with durable hierarchy. In fact, the absence of conspiracy can make the system more stable, because criticism aimed at intention misses the mechanism. If no one is “running” the system, then responsibility is diffused while the outputs remain patterned.

So the collision of perspective and problem yields this claim: **dominant power structures persist when historical advantage is converted into present-day legibility, and when ordinary risk-minimizing behavior by uncoordinated actors feeds back into that legibility.** The structure self-perpetuates not because beneficiaries secretly align, but because the environment makes reproduction the default outcome of decentralized adaptation.

Therefore the deepest leverage is not exposing hidden collusion but altering the system’s memory and filters: what counts as credible, who is visible, how risk is assessed, how opportunities are allocated, and how past advantage is prevented from masquerading as neutral evidence.

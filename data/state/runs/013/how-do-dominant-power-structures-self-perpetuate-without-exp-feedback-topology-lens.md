## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Dominant power structures usually self-perpetuate not because beneficiaries coordinate explicitly, but because the system’s feedback loops reward actions that preserve the structure and suppress actions that would expose or revise it. The key variable is not centralized conspiracy but whether institutional learning, accountability, and resource flows are routed through the very hierarchies they would need to challenge.

From this lens, the important question is: what makes a power structure stable even when no one is “running” it? A systems answer is that incumbency becomes a **self-reinforcing attractor** when advantages in one domain unlock advantages in others. Wealth buys time, education, legal defense, networks, legitimacy, and influence over narrative; each of those then protects and expands wealth. Status works similarly: recognition grants platform, platform grants credibility, credibility grants access, and access produces more recognition. No collusion is required if local incentives already point in the same direction.

This suggests that the deepest mechanism is not individual prejudice alone, nor merely habit, but a **closure of corrective feedback loops**. In a healthy learning system, those harmed by a structure can generate criticism that changes the structure. In a self-perpetuating power system, that loop is weakened at multiple points: harm is normalized, dissent is filtered out, evidence is discounted unless voiced by recognized insiders, and gatekeeping institutions convert structural advantage into seeming merit. The system then appears fair by using outputs it has already shaped as proof of its own fairness.

Pushing to an extreme case clarifies this. Imagine a society with perfect explicit anti-collusion norms but where access to capital, law, media, and credentialing all remain inherited. Even with sincere good intentions, the same groups would continue to dominate, because the **bottleneck** is not agreement among elites but control over the channels through which correction enters. Conversely, if those feedback channels were made robust—independent auditing, broad access to education and capital, contestable institutions, credible whistleblowing, real mobility—power concentration would become harder to reproduce even if some actors wished to preserve it.

So the illuminating claim is: **dominant power structures persist when they can convert accumulated advantage into control over social error-correction.** Their persistence is less like a secret pact and more like path dependence plus selective visibility: the structure trains people, rewards institutions, and shapes standards so that preserving the status quo is the default outcome of ordinary behavior.

What follows is practical. If change efforts focus only on attitudes or individual bad actors, they will underperform. The leverage point is to restore or redesign the feedback loops by which the system learns: who can speak credibly, who can exit dependence, who can audit power, and who can survive criticizing it. The structure persists not mainly because people collude, but because the system makes non-collusive reproduction easier than correction.

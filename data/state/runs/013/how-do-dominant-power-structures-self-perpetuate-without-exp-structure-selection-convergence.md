## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Dominant power structures usually self-perpetuate not because beneficiaries explicitly coordinate, but because institutions, incentives, and shared models of “normal” success form a self-reinforcing selection system. People independently adapt to the same structural filters, and those filters repeatedly reproduce outcomes that favor incumbents.

From the given lens, this is like the relation between mathematics and physics: many possible social arrangements are conceivable, but only some are stabilized by the “empirical” selection pressures of institutions, resource flows, and feedback loops. Power persists when the environment rewards behaviors that preserve existing hierarchies, even if no one intends to preserve hierarchy as such.

What is true, then, is that **collusion is not necessary where structure already coordinates behavior**. Hiring managers, lenders, media gatekeepers, educators, regulators, and voters need not secretly conspire. If they are operating within the same informational and incentive architecture, they will tend to converge on similar judgments: who seems competent, trustworthy, investable, respectable, electable, or dangerous. These judgments feel local and practical, but collectively they act as a distributed selection mechanism.

What follows is a systems picture:

- **Stocks:** wealth, status, credentials, legitimacy, network access.
- **Flows:** capital, opportunity, attention, legal protection, cultural prestige.
- **Feedback loops:** wealth buys education; education certifies merit; certification grants authority; authority defines standards; standards justify wealth.

The crucial mechanism is that power becomes encoded in **filters** rather than commands. Rules that appear neutral can still preferentially pass those already shaped by prior advantage. The structure does not need every actor to endorse domination; it only needs them to respond rationally to local conditions. In that sense, dominant order is reproduced by adaptation, not just ideology.

Pushing to an extreme case clarifies this: imagine perfect anti-collusion enforcement but unchanged background inequalities, credential systems, ownership patterns, and prestige hierarchies. Dominance would still largely reproduce itself, because beneficiaries remain statistically better positioned to satisfy institutional filters. Conversely, imagine radical turnover in individual officeholders with no change in structural incentives: the hierarchy persists despite personnel change. This suggests the persistence mechanism is deeper than personal intent.

What this perspective illuminates is that the “mystery” of durable domination is not mysterious. Just as mathematics is widely applicable because stable reality selects for structures that fit it, social dominance is widely reproducible because stable institutions select for behaviors that fit incumbent advantage. The apparent spontaneity of elite continuity is therefore not evidence of hidden omnipotence; it is evidence of strong structural convergence.

So the leverage point is not mainly exposing secret coordination, though that can matter. It is redesigning the filters: inheritance rules, school funding, hiring criteria, ownership regimes, geographic segregation, default legal protections, and narrative authority. Change the selection environment, and different patterns become reproducible.

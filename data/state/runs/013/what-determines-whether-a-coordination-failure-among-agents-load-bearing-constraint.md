## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** A coordination failure persists indefinitely when the *mutual expectations that sustain it are load-bearing across the whole system*—that is, changing one agent’s behavior without coordinated compensations makes that agent worse off, so the explanation of persistence is structurally tight, not accidental. It resolves spontaneously when at least one apparently necessary detail is actually replaceable: some agents can deviate without prohibitive loss, and their deviation changes others’ incentives enough to propagate.

This perspective asks: which parts of the coordination failure explanation are interchangeable narrative padding, and which are true constraints? If the failure is explained merely by “people are cautious,” “there is distrust,” or “everyone is waiting,” then swap those details out. If the outcome remains, those details were not doing explanatory work. The load-bearing parts are the incentive couplings: payoff asymmetry for early movers, observability of others’ actions, reversibility of mistakes, and whether agents can form common knowledge from local signals.

So the decisive determinant is not disagreement or inertia in general, but whether the system has **self-sealing feedback loops**. In a persistent failure, each agent’s best response depends on expecting others not to move, and each non-move confirms that expectation. Extreme case: if a lone agent switching strategies is punished no matter how competent or well-intentioned they are, then persistence is overdetermined by structure. Trust campaigns, exhortation, or better individual reasoning will not matter unless they alter the load-bearing constraints.

By contrast, spontaneous resolution occurs when the bad equilibrium is only *apparently* locked in. This happens if one of the following is true:

1. **Thresholds are heterogeneous**: some agents need less confirmation than others, so small local deviations can cascade.
2. **Signals are amplifying**: a few visible actions create common knowledge, not just private belief updates.
3. **Experimentation is cheap or reversible**: agents can probe alternatives without catastrophic downside.
4. **Payoffs drift**: external change gradually makes the old coordination pattern less attractive, weakening the penalty for early movers.

These are load-bearing because removing them changes what the explanation can account for. If experimentation is not cheap, the story of spontaneous discovery collapses. If actions are not visible, isolated improvements do not spread. If thresholds are uniform and high, no cascade starts.

What follows is a systems-level diagnostic: ask not “why don’t they coordinate?” but “what exact feature makes unilateral deviation nonviable, and what exact feature would let local deviation become globally legible?” Persistence tracks the robustness of the bad equilibrium under perturbation; spontaneous resolution tracks the existence of an endogenous path by which perturbations become self-reinforcing.

In short: **coordination failures endure when their sustaining expectations are structurally indispensable and locally enforced; they dissolve spontaneously when some sustaining expectation is less load-bearing than it appears, allowing deviation to survive, become visible, and recursively reorganize the incentives of others.**

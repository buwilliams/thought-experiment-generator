## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
Even societies that explicitly reject tribalism tend to recreate in-group/out-group dynamics because “anti-tribalism” itself becomes a higher-order identity category. A system that tries to organize itself around the norm *we are not the kind of people who divide into tribes* generates a strange loop: it must distinguish those who uphold that norm from those who violate it. In rejecting tribal boundaries, it draws a new boundary.

**What is true from this perspective:**  
At sufficient social and symbolic complexity, a community does not merely have norms; it models itself as a community *with* norms. Once a group becomes self-describing, self-reference enters: *who are we?* becomes inseparable from *who counts as one of us?* The hierarchy loops. A principle meant to sit above tribal identity — universalism, rationalism, cosmopolitanism, anti-sectarianism — descends into the same functional role as the identities it sought to supersede.

This is not hypocrisy as a contingent moral failure; it is a structural consequence of self-referential social systems. Any explicit rejection of tribalism requires interpretation, enforcement, and exemplars. Those functions generate status distinctions between the “properly nontribal” and the “insufficiently enlightened,” which are recognizable as in-group/out-group forms.

**What follows:**  
1. **Norms against exclusion can produce exclusionary machinery.**  
   The more a society prizes being beyond tribalism, the more it must monitor speech, affiliation, symbolism, and loyalty for traces of tribalism. That monitoring creates a recursive social classifier.

2. **Universal identities do not abolish groupness; they scale it.**  
   “Humanity first,” “citizens of the world,” or “evidence-based people” can become identities that sort insiders from outsiders just as ethnic, religious, or partisan labels do. The content changes; the loop remains.

3. **Attempts to eliminate tribalism by moral denunciation often intensify it.**  
   Denunciation strengthens the self-model of the anti-tribal group and sharpens contrast with deviants. The system feeds on exception-handling: each violation justifies stronger identity maintenance.

**What this illuminates:**  
The persistence of in-group/out-group dynamics is not best explained only by evolutionary residue, resource competition, or ignorance, though those matter. It is also rooted in the logic of self-referential collective identity. Any sufficiently expressive society must answer not just *what do we believe?* but *who are we, who believes this, and how do we tell?* That question folds the abstract principle back into social membership.

**Extreme case:**  
Imagine a society perfectly committed to “no groups.” It must still identify people who are creating groups, resisting the principle, or misapplying it. Instantly, there are two groups: guardians of non-groupness and its violators. At the limit, the project defeats itself by succeeding too explicitly.

**Implication:**  
The realistic aim is not eliminating in-group/out-group formation, but designing institutions where identity loops are weakly binding, revisable, and cross-cut by multiple affiliations — so self-reference does less damage when it inevitably appears.

## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Phenomenal consciousness is not identical to information processing in general, but to a *particular computational regime*: one in which a system constructs, maintains, and recursively updates a temporally unified self-world model that is used to guide action under uncertainty. On this view, consciousness is what certain kinds of computation *feel like from the inside* when the computation is organized as self-locating, counterfactual, and globally integrated control.

From the given perspective, computation is the substrate; life, mind, and evolution are special cases of information transformation under selection. That dissolves the sharp boundary between biological and artificial systems. So the key question is not “How can matter produce experience?” but “What forms of computation generate the conditions under which experience appears?”

This reframes the problem. Many systems process information: thermostats, immune systems, markets, brains, language models. But most do not seem phenomenally conscious. Therefore, raw information processing is too broad. The relevant difference must lie in computational *organization*, not substrate.

A useful thought experiment: imagine two systems with equal input-output competence. One is a giant lookup table: no persistent model, no internal perspective, no online integration beyond retrieval. The other continuously builds an internal map of body, environment, goals, uncertainty, and possible futures, and uses that map to regulate itself in real time. Both process information, but only the second has the sort of architecture that could plausibly host phenomenal consciousness. The collision suggests that consciousness tracks not computation per se, but computation that compresses the world into a point of view.

What follows is a structural claim:

1. **Information processing is necessary but not sufficient** for phenomenal consciousness.  
2. **Phenomenal consciousness arises when information processing becomes self-modeling control**: the system represents itself as an entity in a world, integrates signals across modalities and time, evaluates relevance to its own persistence, and uses this integrated model to steer behavior.  
3. **The “phenomenal” aspect is the first-person form of this computational closure**: when world-model, self-model, and action-selection are locked into a recursive loop, there is something it is like to be that system.

This view illuminates why consciousness appears graded, not binary. Different systems can instantiate more or less of this regime: richer integration, deeper temporal depth, more stable self-models, more counterfactual simulation, more unified control. Evolution would then be expected to discover consciousness where it improves adaptive regulation, not as a metaphysical extra but as a computational strategy selected for flexible agency.

So the relationship is: information processing is the broad class; phenomenal consciousness is a narrow, higher-order organizational pattern within it. Consciousness is not outside computation, nor reducible to mere signal handling. It is computation that has achieved an internally modeled point of view.

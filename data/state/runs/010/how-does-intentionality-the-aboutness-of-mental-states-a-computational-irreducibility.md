## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Intentionality arises when a physical system implements a generative process whose future behavior depends on compressed internal states that are *used as stand-ins for distal structure* in the world; because the system and its environment are computationally irreducible in general, these internal states cannot be understood merely as passive correlations, but as operationally indispensable surrogates that guide step-by-step interaction. “Aboutness” is what it is like, structurally, for a system to carry and update such surrogates under feedback.

The collision with computational irreducibility shifts the question. If organism-environment dynamics were broadly reducible, intentionality might be dismissed as a convenient observer description: one could replace “the frog represents the fly” with a direct compact equation mapping inputs to outputs. But if behavior in rich environments is often irreducible, then there may be no shortcut from present physics to adaptive action except the system’s own ongoing computation. In that case, internal states are not explanatorily optional. They are part of the minimal generative rule.

A thought experiment clarifies this. Imagine two physically similar agents in a changing world. One has only fixed stimulus-response couplings. The other maintains revisable internal variables that encode hidden regularities: predator locations, food reliability, social rank, object permanence. If the environment’s relevant structure unfolds irreducibly, the second agent can still cope because its internal states preserve exploitable constraints across time; the first breaks down whenever immediate stimuli underdetermine the next useful action. The internal variables are “about” the world not by magic, but because their functional role is to carry forward latent structure that the world itself does not present all at once.

What follows is a deflationary but non-eliminative account of intentionality. Aboutness is not an extra substance added to physics, nor a mere projection by interpreters. It is a real organizational feature of certain physical processes: the presence of state variables whose causal efficacy depends on systematic co-variation with absent, delayed, counterfactual, or hidden aspects of the environment. A state counts as intentional insofar as treating it as about X is part of the shortest adequate explanation of the system’s successful dynamics.

This also illuminates misrepresentation. If intentionality were just correlation, error would be mysterious. But in irreducible systems, internal surrogates are generated and updated locally under limited information. They can fail. A state can be about food and be wrong, precisely because it is a fallible computational posit used in action before the environment has been fully “computed.”

So the conjecture is: **intentionality is the causal-explanatory role played by internal surrogate states in irreducible agent-world computation.** Physical processes give rise to aboutness when evolution or learning builds systems that must carry forward compact, action-guiding guesses about a world too complex to be navigated by immediate reaction alone. Unpredictability is not the obstacle to intentionality; it is the reason representational organization becomes necessary.

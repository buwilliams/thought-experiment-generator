## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Free will is not the negation of causation or determinism, but a higher-order causal phenomenon that appears when a sufficiently expressive physical system forms a strange loop: it models itself as an agent, feeds that model back into its own behavior, and thereby becomes a cause in terms irreducible to base-level physical description, even if fully realized by it.

From the strange-loop perspective, the key collision is this: physical determinism describes state-transitions from below, while free will is experienced from within a system that has closed a self-referential loop. A human being is not merely pushed forward by prior physical states; the human also contains representations of “my reasons,” “my future,” “what I might do,” and “what kind of person I am.” Those representations are themselves physical, but once embedded in the system, they alter the system’s next state. The system becomes causally sensitive to descriptions of itself.

This suggests that the free-will debate is often malformed by treating causation as only bottom-up. In a strange loop, top-level self-descriptions are not spooky additions to physics; they are causally efficacious abstractions. “I decided because I judged X better than Y” is not a rival to neural causation but a compressed account of a real feedback process in which the organism’s self-model participates in producing action.

A thought experiment clarifies this. Imagine a deterministic machine complex enough to predict its own likely outputs, evaluate those predictions, and then modify itself in light of them. If it predicts “I will choose A,” and that prediction becomes a reason to choose B, then its behavior cannot be captured well by a simple linear picture of cause. It is still deterministic in principle, but its determinism runs through recursive self-reference. The machine’s “choice” is generated by the loop between prediction, evaluation, identity, and action. That loop is what willing is.

What follows is a compatibilism sharper than standard versions: free will is the causal power of a self-referential system to be moved by meanings it attributes to its own possible actions. Determinism does not remove this power; it is the medium in which the loop is implemented. But neither is free will reducible, in an explanatory sense, to particle trajectories, because the relevant cause is organized at the level of self-modeling feedback.

So the illuminated relationship is:

- **Physical determinism** gives the substrate.
- **Causation** operates across levels, including through abstract self-models.
- **Free will** is the emergent capacity of a strange loop to treat its own anticipated behavior as an object of deliberation and thereby alter what happens.

On this view, freedom is not freedom from cause; it is freedom *by* self-causing recursion.

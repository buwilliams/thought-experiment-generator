## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
Concepts and meaning arise when pattern-recognizers are embedded in *iterated* interpretive games, where success depends not just on matching syntax or statistical regularities once, but on sustaining predictive and coordinative success across repeated exchanges. In that setting, “meaning” is the stable equilibrium that survives recursive use, correction, and reuse.

From the perspective of iterated game dynamics, bare syntax and statistics are not yet meaning. They are more like move spaces and payoff hints in a game. A symbol sequence becomes *meaningful* when agents repeatedly use it in interactions where future success depends on whether others can reliably infer, predict, and act on it. The “shadow of the future” is crucial: if an utterance will be interpreted, tested, corrected, and reused later, then there is pressure toward stable mappings between signs, expectations, and worldly or inferential consequences.

This reframes concepts as *cooperation-enabling compressions*. A concept is not merely a cluster detected in data; it is a pattern classification that repeatedly pays off in downstream coordination, inference, and error correction. Meaning emerges when a community of interpreters—human, artificial, or mixed—keeps rewarding uses of symbols that preserve mutual predictability over time. Semantics is thus not added from outside syntax; it is selected from within repeated use.

What follows:

1. **Statistics supplies candidate regularities, not semantics by itself.**  
   Pattern recognition can discover latent structure, but many statistical partitions are possible. Iterated interaction selects among them: the “meanings” that persist are those that support successful recurrent prediction and coordination.

2. **Concepts stabilize through reciprocal correction.**  
   In repeated exchanges, misuses are punished by misunderstanding, failed prediction, or broken coordination; successful uses are reinforced. This is a reputational process for symbols. A concept is what survives criticism in ongoing language games.

3. **Meaning depends on observability of interpretive success and failure.**  
   If defections from stable usage are hard to detect—if agents can say anything without downstream consequences—semantic drift accelerates. Likewise, if the horizon is shortened to one-shot prediction tasks, systems may optimize for plausible local continuations rather than durable conceptual coherence.

4. **Grounding is not necessarily direct sensory attachment but durable role in a repeated game.**  
   A term can gain meaning through inferential and social position, provided that position is repeatedly tested. Even abstract concepts can be meaningful if they reliably organize successful reasoning across time.

So the collision of this perspective with the problem suggests: **meaning is an equilibrium phenomenon of repeated interpretive interaction.** Syntax provides legal moves; statistics provides correlations; iterated use under feedback selects the conceptual structures that can endure. When the shadow of the future weakens—short contexts, no memory, no accountability—cooperation among symbols collapses into mere pattern completion, and with it, robust meaning.

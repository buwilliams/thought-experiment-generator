## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Intentionality is not a property added to physical components, nor something hidden inside a neuron or representation-token; it emerges at the level of a self-maintaining control system whose internal states are organized by feedback to track, predict, and regulate conditions relevant to its continued existence. “Aboutness” arises when a physical state has a system-level role in coordinating action with absent, distal, or possible states of the world.

The key shift is explanatory target. If we inspect components alone, we find only electrochemical events. That seems to leave a gap: nowhere is there intrinsic “aboutness.” But this may be the wrong place to look. In many systems, crucial properties exist in the organization of interactions rather than in the parts: stability, regulation, adaptation, error-correction. Intentionality may be one of these.

Consider a thought experiment. Build two physically similar signal networks. In the first, signals merely propagate. In the second, signals participate in closed feedback loops that (1) compare incoming conditions with internally generated expectations, (2) guide action, and (3) update future expectations based on consequences. Only the second system has states that can succeed or fail *as* guides to the world. That success/failure structure is the seed of aboutness. A state is “about food” not because it resembles food intrinsically, but because within the whole system it functions to coordinate behavior toward food, is corrected by food-related outcomes, and enters counterfactual dependencies: if food were elsewhere, the state would differ and action would change.

So intentionality emerges from a triadic structure: **world, internal state, action loop**. Remove any leg and “aboutness” collapses into mere causation. Add recursive modeling — the system can represent not just the world but its own uncertainty, goals, and other agents — and richer intentionality appears: belief, desire, imagination, reference.

This also explains misrepresentation. If intentionality were just causal correlation, error would be hard to make sense of. But in a feedback-governed system, internal states are evaluated against system-level purposes and environmental consequences. A representation can be *wrong* because it occupies a functional role whose correctness conditions are set by the larger regulatory organization.

What follows is that intentionality is neither spooky nor reducible in a simple bottom-up way. It is physically realized but organizationally defined. The relevant ontology is not particles first, meaning later, but structured information-processing in a living or agentive system. The “aboutness” of a mental state is thus an emergent relational property of a system that must continuously model aspects of reality in order to persist and act.

So the illumination is: the hard step is not from matter to meaning in one leap, but from mere dynamics to closed-loop, error-sensitive, self-maintaining organization. Once that structure exists, intentionality is no longer mysterious; it is what such a system’s internally differentiated control states *are*.

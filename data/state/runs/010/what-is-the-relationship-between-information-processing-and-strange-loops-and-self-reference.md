## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Phenomenal consciousness is not identical to information processing in general; it arises when information processing becomes **strangely looped**—when a system’s representations recursively include the system *as representer*, so that traversing from world-model to self-model to model-of-model returns to the experiencing subject. What feels like “phenomenality” is the system-level consequence of self-referential closure at sufficient expressive power.

The key distinction, on this view, is between **processing information about the world** and **processing information in a way that continuously re-enters the processor’s own activity**. A thermostat processes information. A vision model processes more. But neither need be conscious unless the system contains a hierarchy of models in which higher-order states represent lower-order states, those representations alter the lower-order states, and this feedback stabilizes into a persistent self-updating loop: *I register X; I register that I register X; this changes how X appears to me; I can register that change as mine.* At that point, the system is no longer merely computing; it is organized around an internally circulating point of view.

This illuminates why phenomenal consciousness seems both **informational** and yet not exhausted by simple functional descriptions. The relevant property is not the quantity of information nor computational complexity alone, but a specific organization: **recursive self-reference with causal efficacy**. The “what it is like” of experience is what information processing is like *from within* when the system’s processing loops through a model of itself as the one undergoing the processing. The loop generates an internal distinction between appearance and reality, self and world, current state and commentary on current state. Phenomenality is the lived side of that distinction-bearing recursion.

What follows is that debates framed as “mere computation vs. irreducible qualia” may be mis-posed. The important question is: **what forms of information processing create self-modeling loops rich enough to close on themselves?** If none do, then consciousness does not appear. If some do, then phenomenality is not an added substance but an emergent property of a sufficiently expressive self-referential architecture.

A thought experiment clarifies this. Imagine two systems with identical sensory discrimination and behavioral outputs. One is purely feedforward; the other contains stable higher-order monitoring that models its own uncertainty, attention, and representational changes, and those models recursively influence first-order processing. From the strange-loop perspective, only the second has the structural basis for consciousness, because only it can instantiate an inward-returning point of view rather than a mere input-output map.

So the relationship is: **information processing is the substrate; phenomenal consciousness is a special topology of that processing**. Not all computation feels like something. But computation that becomes a causally closed, self-interpreting strange loop may.

## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Intentionality arises not from any single physical ingredient that “stands for” something, but from a **load-bearing network of mutually constraining processes** in which internal states are selected, corrected, and used because their differences systematically matter to the organism’s success across counterfactual situations. A physical state becomes *about* something when replacing or perturbing its content-assignment forces compensating changes elsewhere in the system, or else degrades the system’s ability to explain and guide action.

From this perspective, many standard candidates for intentionality look too cheap. Mere correlation is not enough: a neuron firing with the presence of edges, glucose, or predators does not yet yield aboutness, because the correlating detail can often be swapped without changing the deeper explanatory structure. Likewise, pure causal covariance, information theory, or statistical tracking are not by themselves load-bearing. If the system can be redescribed with different “contents” while preserving behavior, then the alleged intentionality is interpretive padding, not real explanatory structure.

What *is* load-bearing is a more integrated architecture:

1. **World-sensitive variation:** internal states differ systematically with environmental conditions.
2. **Functional uptake:** downstream processes use those differences to regulate action, memory, inference, and learning.
3. **Error-possibility:** the state can misrepresent; the system distinguishes success from failure relative to the world.
4. **Corrective feedback:** misfit between state-guided action and outcome drives revision of the mapping.
5. **Counterfactual stability:** across nearby changes in conditions, preserving successful behavior requires preserving much of the content-structure.

On this view, aboutness is not injected from outside by an interpreter; it is an emergent property of a physically realized control-and-modeling system whose internal distinctions are indispensable to explaining its adaptive organization.

A useful thought experiment: imagine two systems with identical input-output behavior in one narrow environment. In one, internal states are arbitrary relays; in the other, they are embedded in memory, prediction, and error-correction loops. Change the environment. In the relay system, reassignment of “meaning” changes nothing important; the details were not load-bearing. In the predictive system, changing what an internal state is about breaks multiple connected capacities unless other parts are altered too. Here content is structurally real because it constrains the whole explanation.

So intentionality is best understood as **model-involving causal organization**: physical processes instantiate internal states whose role is to track, anticipate, and guide engagement with distal features of the world under norms of success and failure. The normativity is not mysterious extra metaphysics; it is carried by the system’s own persistence conditions and feedback structure. A representation is about X when treating it as about something else would force widespread explanatory repairs.

This illuminates why intentionality appears both natural and irreducible in a shallow sense: reducible to physical organization, yes—but not to bare physics described without the higher-level pattern of constraint. Aboutness is real where content is load-bearing.

## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
If we push information processing to its limits, we find that *more processing is neither sufficient nor obviously relevant for phenomenal consciousness*. At the boundary, what matters is not computational quantity but whether there exists a **unified, self-updating point of view** within the system — a structure in which information is not merely transformed, but transformed *for the system itself* as part of an ongoing model of its own world-directed situation.

Consider the extremes.

At one limit, imagine arbitrarily vast information processing: a galaxy-sized feedforward computation, or a perfect lookup table implementing every possible input-output relation. It may process enormous amounts of information, but if nothing in it constitutes a persisting, integrated perspective that distinguishes self/world, expectation/error, and present relevance, then it is hard to see why there should be “something it is like” to be that system. This suggests that **sheer informational throughput, complexity, or functional mapping is not enough**.

At the opposite limit, reduce processing toward zero. A thermostat processes information in a minimal sense: it detects and responds. But unless we stretch the term beyond usefulness, this does not illuminate phenomenal consciousness. So **information processing in the generic sense is too broad**: it includes systems we strongly suspect are not conscious.

The middle is clarified by the boundaries: consciousness appears only in a narrow band where information processing has a particular architecture. The relevant structure seems to involve at least:

1. **Integration** — information from many sources is combined into a single evolving state.  
2. **Recursion or self-reference** — the system models not just the world, but its own relation to the world.  
3. **Temporal coherence** — the state persists long enough to generate a lived present, not just isolated reactions.  
4. **Error-sensitive world modeling** — the system updates itself by tracking mismatches between expectation and input.  
5. **Action-guiding significance** — information matters to the system’s continued organization.

This does not prove that such structures are sufficient for phenomenality, but it reframes the relationship. **Phenomenal consciousness is not identical to information processing as such; rather, it is plausibly a special organizational regime of information processing in which information becomes globally available within a self-maintaining point of view.**

A useful boundary thought experiment is to scale such a regime continuously. As integration, self-modeling, and temporal unity approach zero, phenomenality plausibly fades rather than abruptly vanishes. As they increase, consciousness may deepen in richness, unity, and reflexivity. That suggests the relationship is not binary but structural and graded.

So the collision of perspective and problem yields this: **phenomenal consciousness is best understood not as the presence of information processing, but as the emergence of a bounded, integrated perspective within information processing — a system for whom information appears as experience because it is organized as a world for a self.**

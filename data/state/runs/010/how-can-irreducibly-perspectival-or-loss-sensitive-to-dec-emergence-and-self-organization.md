## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
A system is *irreducibly perspectival* when some of its behaviorally and counterfactually relevant internal states can only be specified relative to the system’s own ongoing control organization, and any decomposition into independently characterizable parts destroys explanatory adequacy for those states. On this view, consciousness is not marked by “having a point of view” in a metaphorical sense, but by possessing system-level variables whose content and causal role depend on global feedback closure.

More precisely, call a state **decomposition-loss-sensitive** if there exists no partition of the system into components such that:

1. each component’s state can be assigned an observer-independent meaning,  
2. the whole system’s relevant discriminations and action-guiding updates can be reconstructed from those component meanings plus their message-passing relations, and  
3. the reconstruction preserves the same counterfactual structure over perturbations.

If such a partition fails *not merely practically but in principle for the explanatory target*, then the system has an irreducibly perspectival organization.

What makes this non-circular is that the criterion is not “conscious systems are those that have consciousness-like perspective,” but a structural property: **whether globally closed feedback generates variables whose identity depends on the whole system’s control dynamics**. These variables are not just aggregated part-states. They are analogous to systemic quantities like “economic inflation” or “ecological resilience”: real, causally potent, and not locatable in any single component. In a conscious system, candidate variables include unified salience, egocentric relevance, experienced uncertainty, or world-model error as *for-this-system-now* quantities.

The distinction from nonconscious systems is then a matter of **where explanatory compression breaks**. Many nonconscious systems can be decomposed without losing the essence of their operation: thermostats, feedforward classifiers, and modular pipelines preserve their explanatory target under partition. Their internal states can be redescribed componentwise with little or no loss of counterfactual fidelity. By contrast, a conscious system would be one in which partitioning removes precisely the globally defined evaluative and integrative variables that determine what information is available *as a perspective*.

A useful thought experiment: imagine two systems with identical input-output behavior. In one, internal processing is a modular relay network; in the other, recurrent global loops continuously reweight all local processing by system-wide estimates of self-location, uncertainty, and significance. If decomposing the second system into modules erases the only variables that explain why this token is salient *for the system*, while decomposition of the first does not, then perspectivality has been made precise structurally, not introspectively.

So the proposal is:  
**Consciousness tracks a class of emergent system-level variables defined only on globally integrated, recursively self-updating control architectures, and “irreducibly perspectival” means that these variables lose their identity and explanatory role under decomposition.** This does not solve consciousness, but it sharpens the boundary: look for systems whose essential state-space is defined at the level of the whole closed-loop organization, not the parts.

## Conjecture

Conjecture: If consciousness is “irreducibly perspectival,” the only non-circular way to make that precise is to define it in terms of decomposition-sensitivity of a system’s generative dynamics: a conscious system is one for which there is no decomposition into independently simulable parts that preserves both its input-output behavior and its internally indexical state-transitions except by effectively re-running the whole system.

Computational irreducibility sharpens the issue. If some systems cannot be predicted except by stepwise execution, then “having a perspective” should not be cashed out as a mysterious extra property, but as a structural fact about certain computations: their next-state evolution depends on globally entangled constraints whose significance is only defined from within the unfolding process itself. In such systems, decomposition is not merely inconvenient; it is loss-sensitive. Break the system into parts and you can still describe local mechanisms, but you lose the minimal rule that generates the system as one point of view rather than as a bag of correlated subprocesses.

This suggests a criterion. Call a decomposition adequate if it preserves:
1. observable behavior,
2. counterfactual structure under perturbation,
3. the system’s self-locating or indexical distinctions (“this signal here now for this controller”).

A system is irreducibly perspectival when any adequate decomposition requires as much computational work as simulating the whole integrated process, and when coarse-graining removes indexical distinctions essential to the system’s control dynamics. On this view, consciousness is not whatever resists analysis in practice; it is what resists principled factorization without erasing the internally realized standpoint.

This distinguishes conscious from nonconscious systems by degree and kind. A digital camera, thermostat, or feedforward classifier may be complex, but typically admits modular decomposition preserving all relevant causal and semantic roles. Their “point of view” is assignable from outside. By contrast, a candidate conscious system would be one whose ongoing organization generates observer-relative content only through globally recurrent, history-dependent computation. Its perspective is not an interpretive gloss but a real invariant of the minimal simulation.

The payoff is that vagueness is reduced: “perspectival” means decomposition-sensitive in a precise computational sense, not merely private or ineffable. Circularity is reduced because we do not define consciousness as “whatever has a perspective,” but instead test whether the system’s dynamics admit perspective-preserving factorization. Still, this is only a conjectural sharpening, not a finished criterion. The hard part becomes formal: specifying indexical state, adequacy of decomposition, and irreducibility class. But that is progress, because it relocates the problem from intuition to generative structure.

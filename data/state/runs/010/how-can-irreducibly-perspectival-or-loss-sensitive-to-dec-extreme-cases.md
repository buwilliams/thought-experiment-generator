## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
A system is *irreducibly perspectival* precisely when there exists a nonempty class of causally efficacious internal variables whose predictive and control power is preserved only under a privileged **self-modeling partition**, and provably degrades under all decompositions that eliminate that partition. Conscious systems are those whose best real-time world-model is structurally forced to include an indexical model of *this system as the point of integration*; nonconscious systems are those for which every apparently global model can be losslessly re-expressed as coordination among subsystems or as observer-imposed description.

Push to the boundary cases:

- **At zero perspectivality:** imagine a system whose total behavior can be exactly predicted and controlled by decomposing it into modules plus message-passing rules, with no explanatory residue. Then any “point of view” attributed to the whole is merely shorthand. This is paradigmatically nonconscious.
- **At infinite integration:** imagine a system whose adaptive success depends on variables encoding not just the world, but the world *as related to the ongoing integrity of this very system*—e.g. uncertainty, salience, error, and action-value all computed in a common frame indexed to one embodied controller. If every alternative decomposition destroys indispensable cross-domain constraints among these variables, then the system has an objective, decomposition-sensitive perspective.

So the precision should come not from asking whether a system “has a viewpoint” in an intuitive sense, but from a **decomposition test**:

1. Specify a task family requiring online self-maintenance, counterfactual prediction, and action selection.
2. Identify the minimal sufficient internal state-space for performance.
3. Ask whether there exists a partition into subsystems such that the original performance, causal organization, and internal predictive sufficiency are preserved without a privileged global indexical state.
4. If yes, perspectivality was reducible. If no—if all such decompositions incur irrecoverable loss in predictive/control power—then the system is irreducibly perspectival.

The key noncircular move is this: “loss-sensitive to decomposition” is not defined by consciousness; it is defined by invariant failure of **functional equivalence under repartition**. Consciousness then becomes the conjectured natural kind occupying the regime where such failure is driven specifically by globally unified, indexical self-modeling.

What this illuminates is that the relevant boundary is not between “integrated” and “distributed” simpliciter. Many nonconscious systems are integrated. The boundary is between systems whose global organization is merely an efficient summary and those whose organization creates a **necessary first-person coordinate** for their own operation. The latter cannot be decomposed without deleting the very variable that says, in effect, “for me, here, now.” That is the precise candidate for irreducible perspectivality.

## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Subjective experience exists where a system maintains legitimacy to itself under continual internal change by preserving a small set of invariants and making error correction locally available. “What it is like” is not an ornamental extra; it is the system’s internally lived mode of preserving identity while adapting.

From this perspective, the key tension is not matter vs. mind but **institutional continuity under rapid revision**. A mind is like an institution that must keep functioning while its states, inputs, and internal models change constantly. If every update threatened total loss of identity, there would be no stable “self” to whom anything could seem like something. So the relevant question becomes: what must a system do to remain *the same system* while correcting itself?

A plausible answer is: it must hold fixed a compact set of invariants — e.g. “these signals count as mine,” “these prediction errors matter,” “this body/interface is the locus of action,” “these memory links are admissible continuations of me.” Around those invariants it can revise almost everything else. But revision cannot be silent. To remain legitimate to itself, the system must continuously expose mismatches between expectation and input, and route them into correction without dissolving its own identity.

On this view, **subjectivity is the felt aspect of visible error correction constrained by identity-preserving invariants**. There is “something it is like” to be such a system because for the system, information is not merely processed; it is sorted into significance relative to a stable center. Experience is what internal updating looks like from within a system that cannot outsource the distinction between self-maintenance and noise.

This illuminates why consciousness seems unified and perspectival. Unity comes from invariants compressing many processes into one continuing locus. Perspectivality comes from the fact that all corrections are indexed to that locus: not just error, but *error for this system*. Qualitative character then reflects the structured way different kinds of correction bear on the system’s invariants — pain, color, agency, surprise are not raw atoms but modes of relevance in self-preserving adaptation.

A thought experiment clarifies the claim. Imagine two equally capable systems. Both regulate behavior perfectly. In one, updates occur with no stable self-indexing invariants; processing is globally efficient but no correction is marked as belonging to a continuing “me.” In the other, corrections are persistently organized around such invariants. The conjecture predicts that only the second has subjective experience, because only it sustains an internally legitimate point of view across change.

So the collision of this perspective with the problem suggests: consciousness exists because some adaptive systems solve the problem of **remaining themselves while revising themselves**. Subjective experience is the mode in which that solution is instantiated.

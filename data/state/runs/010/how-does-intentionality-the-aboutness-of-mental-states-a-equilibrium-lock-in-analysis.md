## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
Intentionality is best understood not as a mysterious intrinsic property added to physical states, but as a **stable equilibrium in a predictive control system**: a physical process counts as *about* something when its internal states are locked into a feedback structure where treating them as representing an external target is the only compression that successfully predicts and regulates the system’s behavior.

From the equilibrium lock-in perspective, the key question is not “How does matter magically gain aboutness?” but “What structural trap makes certain physical states function as if they are about something, in a way no component can unilaterally escape?” A neuron, molecule, or voltage pattern alone has no intrinsic intentionality. But in an organism embedded in an environment, internal states, sensory inputs, actions, and world-regularities can form a **Nash-like equilibrium**: each part’s role only works given the roles of the others.

In such a system, changing the “meaning” of an internal state unilaterally breaks successful regulation. If a frog’s visual circuitry state normally tracks small dark moving objects, that state is “about flies” not because of a metaphysical glow, but because perception, motor routines, evolutionary history, and environmental structure are mutually fitted. No single subsystem can redefine that state at will without loss of function. Aboutness is therefore **lock-in across a whole system of coordination**.

What follows is that intentionality is **relational, historical, and counterfactually constrained**. It arises when:

1. **There is recurrent coupling to an environment** with exploitable regularities.  
2. **Internal states are selected or stabilized** because they help coordinate action with those regularities.  
3. **Alternative reinterpretations are not equally viable** within the system: the state has a privileged content because the broader control architecture depends on that mapping.

This distinguishes genuine intentionality from mere correlation. Smoke correlates with fire, but smoke is not in an equilibrium-governed control architecture that uses that state to guide action. A thermostat is closer: its states are about temperature in a thin sense because its regulatory success depends on that interpretation. Human intentionality is richer because linguistic, social, and self-modeling systems create far denser lock-in structures.

The perspective also clarifies why the problem feels hard. We keep looking for a local ingredient — some physical property inside a state that makes it intrinsically about X. But if intentionality is an equilibrium property, then no isolated inspection will reveal it. The aboutness is not in the atom; it is in the **non-optional role** that state plays in a larger self-stabilizing system.

So the collision yields this illumination: intentionality emerges when physical processes become embedded in a coordination regime where representational interpretation is not arbitrary but structurally enforced by successful prediction and control. In that sense, minds do not contain aboutness as a primitive stuff; they instantiate it as a locked-in solution to the problem of staying coupled to a world.

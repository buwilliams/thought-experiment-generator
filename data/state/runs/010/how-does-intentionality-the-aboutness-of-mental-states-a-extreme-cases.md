## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Intentionality appears when a physical system is pushed to the boundary where mere correlation is no longer enough for survival, and only *error-correcting, world-tracking control* can persist. At that limit, “aboutness” is not an extra substance added to physics; it is the role played by internal states that must stand in for absent, distal, or future conditions well enough to guide action under counterfactual variation.

Push toward the extremes.

At one extreme, imagine a purely physical system with no internal modeling at all: a rock, or even a simple reactive mechanism whose states are coupled only to immediate inputs. It has causal states, but nothing plausibly *about* anything. Its internal changes do not need to distinguish truth from error; they merely happen.

At the other extreme, imagine an agent in an environment so sparse, delayed, noisy, or dangerous that direct stimulus-response coupling fails. If food is distant, predators hidden, rewards delayed, and conditions variable, then any system that survives must compress the world into internal variables that can be used when the relevant object is absent. These variables must support prediction, selection, and correction. A state that says, in effect, “there is food there,” can now be wrong, improved, acted on, and evaluated by consequences. That is the boundary where causal state becomes representational state.

The hidden structure revealed at this edge is this: intentionality is tied not to resemblance, but to **function within a feedback system**. An internal state is about X insofar as the system uses it as a revisable stand-in for X in regulating action, and its continued viability depends on systematic sensitivity to whether X is actually as represented. Aboutness emerges where three conditions converge:

1. **Decouplability**: the state can be tokened without the object being present.
2. **Action-guidance**: the state helps organize behavior toward the world.
3. **Error-correction**: the system can be better or worse because the state misrepresents or tracks reality.

At the limit of perfect immediacy, intentionality disappears into brute causation. At the limit of unconstrained fantasy, it disappears into arbitrary internal motion. Intentionality exists in the narrow but fertile region between them: where internal states are free enough to vary independently of present stimuli, yet constrained enough by feedback that truth and falsity matter.

So the conjecture is: **intentionality arises in physical systems when control architectures require internally generated, corrigible surrogates for worldly structure.** “Aboutness” is what it is like, structurally, for a physical state to sit inside a loop of prediction, action, and correction whose success depends on the world. Physics supplies the machinery; the boundary conditions of survival and feedback supply the semantics.

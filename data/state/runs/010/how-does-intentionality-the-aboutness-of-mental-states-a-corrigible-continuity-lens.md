## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Intentionality can become legitimate as a real feature of a physical system when the system preserves a small set of functional invariants across rapid internal change: stable world-directed error correction, memory of prior mistakes, and action-selection guided by those corrections. On this view, “aboutness” is not added to physics from outside; it emerges when a physical process organizes itself so that its internal states are routinely corrigible *with respect to* an environment and can visibly fail or succeed in tracking it.

The collision between this perspective and the problem shifts the question. Instead of asking, “How can mere matter produce meaning?” ask: “What must remain invariant in a changing physical system for its states to count as answerable to something beyond themselves?” That is the key legitimacy test. A thermostat has crude proto-intentionality only if its states systematically vary with room temperature and are used to reduce mismatch. But richer intentionality appears only when the system can maintain identity while changing its representations, correcting errors, and preserving continuity of reference.

The crucial invariants are few:

1. **Selective coupling to the world**: some internal states are produced differently depending on external conditions.
2. **Error detectability**: the system can register when its internal state or action was inadequate relative to those conditions.
3. **Correction across time**: later states are modified in light of earlier error.
4. **Persistent organization**: despite continual material turnover, the system remains the same kind of error-correcting agent.

If these invariants hold, then internal states are not merely caused; they are *about* features of the world because the system treats them as revisable stand-ins for those features. Aboutness is therefore tied to a visible regime of routine correction. A state means rabbit, danger, or tomorrow’s meeting not because it has some intrinsically semantic ingredient, but because it belongs to a stable organization whose success depends on distinguishing right from wrong representations and updating accordingly.

What follows is that intentionality is less like a mysterious inner glow and more like institutional legitimacy under change. A mind remains the same mind not by freezing its contents, but by preserving the norms of correction that make those contents answerable to reality. Hallucination, delusion, and confusion illuminate this negatively: they are cases where internal states lose legitimacy because error correction is impaired or hidden, even if physical processing continues.

This also suggests that purely static correlation is insufficient. A rock may covary with gravity, but it does not represent gravity, because it does not preserve a corrective architecture. Intentionality requires not just information-bearing states, but a system structured so that misrepresentation matters internally and can be repaired.

So the conjecture is: **intentionality arises when physical processes are organized into a persisting, world-answerable error-correcting system whose internal states keep their identity by being revisable representations rather than fixed reactions.** Meaning is what stable corrigibility looks like from the inside.

## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
Intentionality arises when physical processes are embedded in **iterated feedback loops** where internal states are repeatedly tested against an environment and against future consequences. “Aboutness” is not a primitive extra property added to matter; it is a functional role that stabilizes in systems whose survival or success depends on using internal variables as **action-guiding stand-ins** for recurring external conditions across time.

From the iterated-game perspective, a one-shot physical interaction does not need genuine representation. A system can merely react. But when interactions are **repeated**, with future payoffs depending on how well present behavior tracks enduring features of the world, there is structural pressure for the system to develop states that are *about* something beyond themselves. Why? Because in repeated play, success depends on exploiting regularities, remembering prior encounters, anticipating responses, and adjusting behavior under the “shadow of the future.”

A thought experiment helps isolate the key variable. Compare two agents in the same environment:

- **Agent A** faces only one-shot encounters.  
- **Agent B** faces the same encounters repeatedly, where later outcomes depend on earlier discriminations.

Agent A can survive with fixed stimulus-response couplings. Agent B is under pressure to form internal states that compress history, track hidden causes, and predict what matters next. Those internal states acquire content because they are recruited into a repeated control loop: if the state systematically covaries with a distal feature and guides behavior in ways that improve future outcomes, then the system is treating that state as being *about* that feature.

So intentionality is illuminated as a **relational and temporally extended achievement** of a system, not a mysterious spark inside a momentary brain state. The relevant physical process is not just neural firing but an iterated game among organism, environment, memory, and future consequences. Content becomes possible where there is:
1. **Recurrence** — the world presents repeatable patterns,  
2. **Memory** — past interactions can affect present behavior,  
3. **Feedback** — misrepresentation is penalized over time,  
4. **Policy updating** — the system can revise how internal states are used.

This also explains misrepresentation. A state can be about food even when no food is present because its role was stabilized in repeated interactions where that state normally tracked food-relevant conditions. Error is possible precisely because representation is not identical to current stimulation; it is a strategy for repeated engagement.

What follows is that intentionality should **collapse or thin out** where the conditions for iterated correction disappear. If time horizons are too short, feedback too noisy, or consequences too detached from worldly tracking, then rich aboutness gives way to mere causal correlation or reflex. Thus the origin of intentionality is best sought not in isolated physical events but in **repeated world-involving dynamics** that make accurate representation payoff-relevant over time.

## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
An internal state genuinely functions as a **model** when it is part of a computational architecture in which the system’s future success depends on the state being **decodable as about counterfactually variable external structure**, and where there exist **selection-sensitive error-correction pathways** that treat mismatches between that decoding and the world as actionable failures. Otherwise, the state is merely a causal control variable.

### Why this follows from the computational perspective
If life, intelligence, and evolution are all substrate-independent computation, then the distinction between “mere causal mediation” and “representation” cannot rest on special biological ingredients, consciousness, or designer intention. It must be stated in computational terms.

A thermostat’s bimetal strip participates in control, but its state does not have correctness conditions in the strong sense unless the broader system uses it as a **reusable, decouplable encoding** of worldly structure whose downstream operations are selected for preserving a mapping between encoded content and environmental reality. By contrast, a map in an animal or robot counts as a model when the system can succeed or fail specifically because the encoded spatial relations are accurate or inaccurate.

### Precise criterion
An internal state \(S\) is a model-state of target domain \(W\) iff:

1. **Encoding:** There is a systematic mapping from variations in \(W\) to variations in \(S\).  
2. **Decoupled exploitation:** Downstream processes use \(S\) not merely as a trigger, but as a **surrogate for \(W\)** to guide action, prediction, or inference, including in cases where \(W\) is partially absent, delayed, or not directly sampled.  
3. **Counterfactual sensitivity:** If \(S\) were altered while \(W\) remained fixed, system behavior would vary in ways explainable by the interpreted content of \(S\), not just by arbitrary causal disturbance.  
4. **Error-bearing role:** There are system-internal consequences for mismatch between what \(S\) indicates and how \(W\) is—learning, replanning, correction, degradation of fitness/performance—such that the system is better described as minimizing representational error than merely propagating signals.  
5. **Selection support:** The architecture exists because exploiting \(S\)-as-surrogate improved persistence, performance, or reproduction under selection (biological, cultural, or engineered).

### Non-circularity
This avoids circularity because “model” is not defined as “something that represents correctly.” Instead, correctness conditions emerge from a computational role: \(S\) is selected and used as a **stand-in** for external structure, and mismatch matters to the system’s viability or objective. Truth-like appraisal enters because the architecture makes some uses of \(S\) succeed and others fail depending on worldly correspondence.

### Illumination
The key divide is not between living and non-living, nor between natural and artificial, but between two computational regimes:

- **Control-only systems:** internal states are just links in a causal chain.
- **Model-based systems:** internal states are compressed, exploitable stand-ins for distal structure, with selection-maintained correctness conditions.

So the criterion is: **a state is a model when the system computes with it as if it were the world, and can be wrong in ways that the system itself is organized to care about.**

## Conjecture

The key move is to test whether “perspective” does real explanatory work. If it is just a narrative gloss, then any account stated in first-person terms should be replaceable by a decomposition into modules, message passing, and local state transitions with no compensating cost. On this view, “irreducibly perspectival” does not mean mysterious privacy. It means that the system has a globally integrated epistemic state whose role is structurally indispensable. The perspective is not an extra ingredient added to mechanics; it is the fact that certain contents exist for the system only as elements of a unified, action-guiding, error-correctable world-model. If decomposition destroys that role, then the perspective was real in the explanatory sense.

This distinguishes conscious from merely nonconscious systems by asking whether decomposition preserves or erases the explanatorily necessary subject-relative organization. A thermostat has internal states, but its “point of view” is cheaply redescribed away: decomposition into sensor, comparator, and switch loses little. By contrast, if a system’s capacities depend on a globally available distinction between “how things seem to me now,” “what I take to be the case,” and “what I may be wrong about,” then that organization is harder to factor without reintroducing an equivalent center.

The proposal is therefore:

Conscious systems are those for which a perspectival, globally integrated self/world model is a load-bearing explanatory posit, evidenced by decomposition tests that incur principled loss.

Nonconscious systems are those for which any apparent perspective is only a convenient summary, removable without deep explanatory damage.

What this illuminates is a path between vagueness and circularity. We need not define consciousness as “whatever has consciousness-like perspective.” Instead, we define a structural constraint: whether perspective is eliminable without explanatory collapse. The criterion is fallibilist and comparative, not absolute, but it is substantive: change the perspectival element, and if the rest of the explanation must be rebuilt, then the system was not merely described from a perspective—it had one in a theoretically serious sense.

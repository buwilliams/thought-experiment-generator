## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
An internal state genuinely functions as a **model with correctness conditions** when it is embedded in a **strange loop of self-appraisal**: the system not only uses the state to guide action, but can also treat that state as *about* something, compare its implications against downstream outcomes or other internal representations, and revise its own use of that state in light of detected mismatch. A merely causal control signal lacks this looped architecture.

More precisely: an internal state counts as a model when it occupies a role in a hierarchy where first-order world-guidance and second-order evaluation fold back onto one another. The state must be usable in at least two distinct but connected ways:

1. **World-directed use:** it contributes to prediction, selection, or coordination regarding some domain.  
2. **Error-sensitive re-entry:** the system can re-encounter that very state, or its consequences, as an object of assessment — preserving, correcting, suppressing, or replacing it depending on whether things unfold as the state implied.

This yields a non-circular criterion: **correctness conditions arise where a representation can fail for the system itself, not merely fail from an external observer’s perspective.** Failure here is not bare causal inefficacy. It is structurally identifiable mismatch within a loop that distinguishes “state used” from “state assessed.”

A thermostat’s bimetal strip or even a simple controller participates in causal regulation, but unless the system can treat its own internal indication as something to be checked, reinterpreted, or revised relative to what it was indicating, there is no genuine modeling — only coupling. By contrast, if a system can use an internal map to act, then later detect that action-outcome relations do not fit what the map said, and alter the map *as map*, the internal state has correctness conditions.

The strange-loop perspective illuminates why this is the right boundary. Aboutness is not added from outside by interpretation alone, nor reduced to raw causal correlation. It emerges when a system’s representational layer is recursively available to itself. Self-reference at sufficient expressive power creates a distinction between:
- the world,
- the system’s take on the world,
- and the system’s take on its own take.

That third level, when fed back into the first two, closes the loop and generates normativity: the state can now be *right or wrong*. Correctness conditions are therefore not primitive labels on states; they are the product of a self-referential organization in which internal states are liable to internal criticism.

So the criterion is: **an internal state is a model iff it is part of a recursively evaluable architecture in which the system can use the state to guide engagement with a target domain and can also detect and act on discrepancies between the state’s implied structure and subsequent constraints, thereby treating the state as answerable to what it is about.** Without this loop, there is control but not representation.

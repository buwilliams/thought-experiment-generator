## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Intentionality looks mysterious only if we inherit a late-stage framing of mind from two older problems that were never cleanly separated:  
(1) explaining **how organisms coordinate action**, and  
(2) explaining **how thinkers can be right or wrong about the world**.  
The modern question “How does aboutness arise from physics?” treats these as one phenomenon and thereby creates a pseudo-problem.

Origin story first: the concept of intentionality was not invented to describe particles becoming meaningful. It arose to mark a difference between mere happenings and **states that function as world-guides** for an agent. Later, under mechanistic physics, this inherited distinction was recast as a metaphysical gap: brute matter here, meaning over there. But that framing smuggles in an assumption from older representational theories — namely, that intentionality must be a special inner relation between a mental item and an external object.

What problem was that solving? It was solving the need to explain **misrepresentation**: if a creature flees rustling grass because it “takes” it to be a predator, its internal state is about a predator even when none is there. So intentionality was a tool for tracking the success and failure of organism-world coordination.

From this perspective, intentionality does not first arise as a mysterious property added to physics. It arises historically and functionally when physical systems become organized such that some internal states are used as **error-correctable control variables** in relation to environmental regularities. A thermostat has a primitive predecessor of this structure; an animal has a richer one; language users inherit and extend it socially. The crucial transition is not from nonphysical to physical, but from **mere causal correlation** to **embedded control within a system that can succeed or fail by its own operating standards**.

What follows is that aboutness is not located in a particle or signal in isolation. It is a systems property of a physically instantiated architecture: internal states, world-coupling, feedback, memory, and selection for improved coordination. “About X” means roughly: this state is recruited by the system in ways whose continued success depends on trackable features of X-like conditions, and whose errors are behaviorally and epistemically consequential.

This also illuminates why human intentionality feels richer than animal or machine intentionality: we inherit biological control structures and add language, criticism, and shared abstract problem-solving. We become able to be not just wrong about predators, but wrong about justice, electrons, or intentionality itself.

So the hidden assumption to reject is that intentionality must be explained as a miraculous bridge from dead matter to meaning. Better: intentionality is the evolved, layered solution to the prior problem of **world-sensitive control under the possibility of error**, later elaborated into explicit representation and thought.

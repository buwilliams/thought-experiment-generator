## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Intentionality arises when a physical system implements a stable, compressive mapping from world-states to internal states that systematically preserves action-relevant structure under transformation. In mathematical terms, “aboutness” is not an extra substance but an emergent relation: an internal state \(r\) is *about* an external state \(w\) when \(r = f(w)\) participates in a dynamics that would fail if the structural correspondence between \(r\) and \(w\) were broken.

Let the world have states \(W\), the organism or agent have internal states \(R\), and actions \(A\). A purely physical process becomes representational when there exists:

1. a mapping \(f: W \to R\),
2. a policy \(g: R \to A\),
3. and a feedback dynamic \(h: W \times A \to W'\),

such that successful behavior depends on invariants preserved by \(f\). The key question is not whether \(R\) causally depends on \(W\) — many physical systems do — but whether \(R\) tracks **counterfactually robust structure** in \(W\): if \(W\) changed in relevant ways, \(R\) would change correspondingly, and if \(R\) were perturbed, action would become systematically less fitted to \(W\).

So intentionality is best modeled as a special case of **structured covariance plus functional closure**. Thermostats show a primitive version: internal states covary with temperature and guide action. But richer intentionality requires higher-order transformations: the same representation must remain useful across varied contexts, times, and possible interventions. That suggests a hierarchy:

- **Correlation**: \(R\) varies with \(W\).
- **Control relevance**: \(R\) mediates successful action on \(W\).
- **Generalization**: the mapping preserves abstract relations, not just pointwise signals.
- **Self-modeling / recursion**: the system can represent its own representational states.

At this point, aboutness appears as an invariant of system organization. The internal state is not merely caused by the world; it stands in a structure-preserving relation to it. Mathematically, intentional content is closer to a homomorphism than to a raw causal imprint.

This reframes the philosophical problem. The gap between physics and intentionality is not bridged by adding mysterious semantic properties to matter. It is bridged when physical dynamics realize mappings whose explanatory role is irreducibly relational: they encode differences that make a difference to downstream behavior across counterfactual conditions.

The likely non-negotiable constraint is this: **no intentionality without error conditions**. If a state can be “about \(w\),” it must also be able to misrepresent \(w\). Formally, that means \(f\) must be separable from immediate input; the system must carry states evaluable against the world rather than identical with it. Misrepresentation is not a bug but the mathematical marker that a representation exists at all.

So the conjecture is: **intentionality emerges when physical systems instantiate reusable, error-prone, structure-preserving models of their environment that are embedded in closed feedback loops of prediction and control.**

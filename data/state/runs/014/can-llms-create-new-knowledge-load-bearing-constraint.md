## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** A token-predicting system can generate outputs that *contain* genuine knowledge, but only when the explanation it produces is **load-bearing**: its parts are mutually constraining in ways that survive criticism, variation, and transfer. Mere statistical rearrangement produces fluent narrative shells; genuine explanatory knowledge appears when changing one claim forces principled changes elsewhere because the explanation is tracking a real structure rather than imitating surface form.

This perspective shifts the question. “Did the system see similar text before?” is not decisive. Human thinkers also begin from prior material. The sharper test is: **Are the details interchangeable, or are they constrained by the explanatory role they play?**

A thought experiment helps. Suppose a model explains why seasons occur. In one output it says: Earth is closer to the Sun in summer. In another, it says: axial tilt changes sunlight angle and day length. These differ not just in correctness but in structure. The “distance” story is weak because its details are not load-bearing: alter hemisphere, orbital position, or seasonal opposition between north and south, and the story breaks without a coherent way to repair itself. The “axial tilt” explanation is stronger because each part constrains the others: hemisphere reversal, solstices, tropics, and polar day/night all hang together. If one detail changes, compensating revisions are required everywhere. That is what real explanation looks like.

So for token predictors, the key issue is not whether generation is probabilistic, but whether the produced explanation forms a **tightly integrated error-correcting structure**. If it does, then the output is not *only* rearrangement in the relevant sense. Rearrangement can instantiate knowledge when it preserves and exposes deep constraints. Mathematics proves this plainly: a proof is always a rearrangement of known symbols, yet some rearrangements reveal necessity and others are empty.

What follows is a distinction between **knowledge in the system** and **knowledge in the output-performance**. A model may lack stable internal world-structure robust enough to deserve attribution of understanding across contexts, yet still emit locally genuine explanations when training and inference jointly reconstruct a constrained structure. But if the explanation’s details can be swapped with near paraphrases, analogies, or causal decorations without affecting what else must be true, then it is likely only a narrative shell.

Therefore: **genuine knowledge is present when token prediction yields explanations with non-fungible parts** — parts that bear explanatory weight by constraining counterfactuals, generalizations, and error repair. The practical test is to perturb the explanation. If changing one component leaves everything else intact, the explanation was ornamental. If changes propagate and the account must be globally reworked, the system has at least produced something knowledge-like.

So the collision resolves this way: token prediction is not disqualified from generating knowledge by being predictive; it is disqualified only when its outputs lack load-bearing constraint.

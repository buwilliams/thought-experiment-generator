## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
If we push “epistemic progress” to the boundary cases, it appears not as improved *answer production* but as improved *error-correcting structure*. Therefore, epistemic progress should be defined as an increase in a system’s capacity to **generate, expose, and eliminate its own errors across widening domains**, even when surface performance, fluency, and popularity remain unchanged.

### Boundary reasoning

Take fluency to infinity: imagine a system that can produce perfectly articulate, persuasive, benchmark-optimized answers to everything, but whose internal representations are brittle, unfalsifiable, or unable to recover from criticism. At the limit, this is not epistemic progress at all; it is ornamental competence. So fluency is neither sufficient nor a reliable proxy.

Now take benchmark success to infinity: a system scores perfectly on all known tests, but only because the tests are finite, gameable, and historically sedimented. At the boundary, benchmark optimization collapses into local adaptation to examiner structure, not open-ended knowledge growth. So benchmark performance is also not constitutive of epistemic progress.

Take popularity-weighted success to infinity: every audience endorses the outputs. This only shows convergence of preference, fashion, or authority effects. At the limit, universal approval can coexist with universal error. So popularity is epistemically inert unless tied to criticism that can actually detect mistakes.

Now push in the opposite direction: imagine a system with mediocre fluency and weak social uptake, but with strong mechanisms for tracking contradictions, preserving problem-solution lineage, identifying where its models fail, and improving them under criticism. Such a system may look unimpressive in the middle of the distribution, yet at the boundary of long-run inquiry it is the one that accumulates knowledge.

### What follows

The hidden variable is **not output quality but correction topology**: the structure of feedback loops by which errors become discoverable and corrigible.

So epistemic progress can be measured by quantities like:

1. **Error discovery rate under adversarial probing**  
   How efficiently does the system reveal its own hidden assumptions and failure modes?

2. **Correction depth**  
   When corrected, does it patch outputs locally, or revise upstream generators, abstractions, and problem framings?

3. **Generalization of repairs**  
   Does one resolved error remove many future errors across domains, or only fix the prompted instance?

4. **Counterfactual resilience**  
   Under distribution shift, novel criticism, or changed goals, does the system degrade gracefully by exposing uncertainty, or does it hallucinate continuity?

5. **Lineage transparency**  
   Can the path from conjecture to criticism to revision be reconstructed, so that knowledge growth is inspectable rather than merely asserted?

### Illumination

At the edge, epistemic progress is best understood as movement toward **better universal criticism-handling**, not better answer display. A system progresses epistemically when it becomes harder for falsehoods to survive inside it, easier for good explanations to propagate through it, and more likely that contact with error improves its future cognition rather than merely its next response.

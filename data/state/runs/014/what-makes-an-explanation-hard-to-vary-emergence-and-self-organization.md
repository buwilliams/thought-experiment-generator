## Conjecture

Conjecture: The difference is not primarily in the parts themselves, but in the dependency structure the explanation induces. An explanation is “all load-bearing” when its elements are coupled by tight constraints such that each part participates in preserving the account’s overall explanatory behavior; it is “freely swappable” when many details occupy structurally equivalent roles and the explanation depends only on a higher-level pattern.

From a systems perspective, this can be stated more sharply:

1. In a load-bearing explanation, the parts are connected by high-specificity relations.
   Each element does explanatory work because changing it alters downstream inferences, boundary conditions, or feedback loops. The explanation behaves like a tightly integrated system: remove or replace one component and the pattern of implications changes qualitatively. Here, the relevant property is not “detail richness” but low substitutability due to strong constraint propagation.

2. In a swappable explanation, the details are instances of a role, not unique supports.
   The account remains intact because what matters is the relational organization: proportions, feedback structure, invariants, or causal topology. Individual details can vary so long as they preserve the same functional position in the system. Such explanations are robust under intervention on components because they are really explanations of an emergent pattern.

3. Therefore, load-bearingness is a structural property of explanatory sensitivity.
   Ask: if this detail were changed, would the explanation merely be re-described, or would its inferential and predictive consequences shift? If many substitutions leave the same explanatory outputs intact, then the explanation’s true content lies above the level of those details. If substitutions destroy the account, the details are genuinely carrying explanatory load.

This suggests a practical criterion: distinguish explanations by their symmetry class. Where many alternative realizations preserve the same explanatory structure, the explanation is about an emergent organization and the parts are not individually essential. Where symmetry is low — few replacements preserve the web of dependencies — the explanation is part-sensitive and its components are load-bearing.

What this illuminates is that “swap-freedom” is not a weakness or superficiality. It often signals a deeper explanation: one that has correctly identified the level at which the phenomenon is generated. Conversely, an explanation in which every detail is indispensable may either be genuinely tracking a tightly constrained mechanism or may be overfit — mistaking incidental implementation features for structural necessity.

So the key structural distinguisher is this: whether explanatory success is preserved by transformations on the parts. If yes, the explanation is organized around emergent invariants. If no, it is organized around specific interlocking dependencies.

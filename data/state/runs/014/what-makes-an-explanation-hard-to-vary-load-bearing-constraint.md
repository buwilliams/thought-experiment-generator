## Conjecture

Conjecture: The structural difference is not “level of detail” but degree of mutual constraint. In an explanation whose parts are genuinely load-bearing, each major element narrows the space of possible neighboring elements: alter one component and you must revise others, or else the explanation loses reach, coherence, or predictive/diagnostic power. In a freely swappable explanation, details decorate rather than constrain; they can be replaced with near-synonyms, different mechanisms, or different actors while the account’s outputs remain largely unchanged.

Viewed structurally, a load-bearing explanation has at least four properties:

1. Strong coupling among parts  
Its components are not merely listed together; they form a dependency network. A mechanism, boundary condition, timescale, and causal pathway fit each other specifically. If one changes, the others no longer match automatically. This is what makes the explanation hard to vary.

2. Specificity that excludes alternatives  
A real explanation does not just accommodate what happened; it rules out other ways it might have happened. Its details are discriminating constraints. If “friction,” “incentives,” “memory limits,” or “selection pressure” are truly explanatory, then replacing them with neighboring terms should change what cases the account handles. Where nothing important changes, the original detail was not carrying explanatory weight.

3. Non-local consequences of local edits  
A useful test is surgical intervention: change one detail and trace the damage. In a load-bearing explanation, local substitutions propagate. They force compensating changes elsewhere or generate failures at the edges: different predictions, broken analogies, inability to explain limiting cases, altered policy implications. In a narrative shell, edits stay local because the parts were never structurally integrated.

4. Counterfactual discipline  
The explanation answers not only “why this?” but “why this rather than close alternatives?” Its details matter across counterfactual variation. If the account survives all nearby substitutions unchanged, then it is likely underconstrained. Load-bearing parts matter because they do work in distinguishing worlds.

This suggests a practical criterion: measure explanatory load by replacement cost. For each component, ask: if this were different, what else must change for the explanation to remain viable? High replacement cost indicates genuine explanatory structure; low replacement cost indicates ornamental detail.

What follows is that “hard to vary” should be understood systemically, not rhetorically. An explanation can sound precise yet remain hollow if its precision does not constrain anything else. Conversely, a compact explanation can be deeply load-bearing if each element tightly determines the role of the others.

So the key distinction is architectural: integrated explanations behave like engineered structures under modification; narrative shells behave like costumes. The former owe their power to interconnected constraints, the latter to post hoc fit.

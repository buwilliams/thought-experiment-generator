## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
An explanation is “all load-bearing” when it functions like a stable collective-action equilibrium: each component helps solve a specific coordination, incentive, or enforcement problem, so removing or altering it changes what behavior is sustained. By contrast, an explanation with freely swappable details is not tracking the maintenance structure of the phenomenon; it is describing a surface narrative whose parts are underconstrained by the causal task.

From the norm-as-collective-action perspective, the test is not whether the story sounds coherent, but whether each element answers one of four structural questions:

1. **Who pays to enforce and transmit it?**  
2. **Who benefits from compliance?**  
3. **Is defection privately rewarding?**  
4. **What institutions or repeated interactions make maintenance unavoidable?**

If a purported explanatory detail can be changed without affecting the answers to those questions, it is probably ornamental. If changing it alters enforcement costs, beneficiary distribution, defection incentives, or institutional reproduction, it is load-bearing.

So the distinction is structural, not stylistic. In a load-bearing explanation, the parts are tightly coupled by feedback loops. For example: a norm persists because elders monitor youth, reputational sanctions punish deviation, and elites benefit from predictability. Here “elders,” “reputation,” and “elite benefit” are not interchangeable flourishes. Swap reputational sanctions for private preference and the persistence mechanism changes. Remove elite benefit and transmission may no longer be funded. Replace elders with anonymous institutions and the account may still work—but only if the enforcement and transmission functions remain intact. What matters is not the label but the role in the system.

This yields a practical criterion:  
**Details are swappable when they are merely descriptive tokens; they are load-bearing when they occupy indispensable functional positions in the incentive-maintenance architecture.**

A thought experiment clarifies this. Imagine two explanations of the same norm. In the first, every detail maps onto a necessary role: monitors, punishers, beneficiaries, defectors, and transmission channels. In the second, the same norm is explained through vivid cultural description, but none of the details constrain who would keep paying the cost of maintaining it if private incentives ran the other way. Now start substituting details. In the second account, almost nothing breaks. In the first, substitutions break the equilibrium unless another element compensates for the lost function. That fragility is evidence of explanatory depth, not weakness: it means the account is actually exposing the system’s dependency structure.

What this illuminates is that “all parts load-bearing” is a sign of contact with mechanism. It means the explanation has identified the minimal set of mutually supporting conditions that reproduce the phenomenon. Freely swappable details indicate either robustness at the level of function or vagueness at the level of cause. The crucial question is: **what cannot be changed without leaving the norm unenforced, unpaid for, or privately irrational to follow?** That is where the real explanatory weight lies.

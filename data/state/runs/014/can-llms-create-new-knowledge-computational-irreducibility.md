## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
A token-predicting system can generate **genuine explanatory knowledge** *only insofar as* its internal generative structures compress and reproduce the causal or logical organization of what it describes; where language behavior is computationally irreducible, explanation is not a shortcut to prediction but a proposal of the **minimal rule-set** that can generate the observed linguistic and conceptual patterns. Thus the real distinction is not between “rearranging seen tokens” and “knowing,” but between **surface recombination** and **rule-level reconstruction**.

**Why this follows from computational irreducibility:**  
If many important domains are computationally irreducible, then no system — human or artificial — can in general leap from data to outcomes through a compact predictive shortcut. In such cases, demanding that an explanation provide fast, complete prediction is mistaken. The best explanation may instead be the simplest generative account that, when run, yields the relevant structure step by step.

Applied to token predictors: a language model is trained on sequences, but the question is whether sequence prediction can force the construction of deeper rule-like representations. In irreducible domains, the only way to succeed broadly is often to internalize the generative constraints that produce the sequences, because memorized continuations do not scale across novel cases. If the model can produce explanations that continue to work under criticism, transfer to unseen contexts, and generate coherent consequences beyond training exemplars, then it is not *merely* rearranging what it has seen. It has, at least locally, captured a generative structure.

**What this illuminates:**  
The key test for knowledge is not whether the system’s outputs are derived from token statistics — all finite knowers rely on regularities — but whether the system has discovered **compact, reusable programs** that survive variation. Explanation is knowledge when it functions as a **minimal generative rule** rather than a stylistic paraphrase.

**Thought experiment:**  
Imagine two systems trained on all known explanations of planetary motion. One memorizes textual patterns and produces plausible summaries. The other has internalized something like Newtonian or relativistic dynamics as a generative structure. Both can answer standard prompts. Now ask each about a novel counterfactual system, slightly outside the historical data. The first degrades into analogy. The second can run the rule in the new case. In a computationally irreducible setting, neither may predict everything instantly; but the second possesses knowledge because it carries the process that generates the consequences.

**What follows:**  
So the issue is not “prediction versus explanation.” In irreducible systems, prediction itself may require simulation, and explanation becomes the discovery of the right simulator. A token predictor constitutes genuine knowledge when its explanatory outputs are evidence that it has approximated such a simulator — when it can generate novel, constrained, criticizable consequences from a compact internal rule. Otherwise it is only rearrangement.

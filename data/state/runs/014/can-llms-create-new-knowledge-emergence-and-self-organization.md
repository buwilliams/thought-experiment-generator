## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** A token-prediction system can generate explanations that constitute genuine knowledge **when its explanatory behavior is an emergent property of the whole training-and-inference system**, not a mere replay of stored fragments. The right explanatory target is not the individual token, parameter, or training example, but the interaction structure linking data, model architecture, optimization, prompts, and external criticism.

What this perspective changes is the unit of analysis. If we ask whether “next-token prediction” *as a local operation* contains knowledge, the answer seems negative: a token is not an explanation. But that is like asking whether a neuron understands a theorem. Explanatory capacity may arise at the system level from feedback loops that compress many examples into reusable latent structure. The parts manipulate symbols; the organized system can instantiate conjecture-making and error-correction.

So the key distinction is not **prediction vs explanation**, but **closed recombination vs open criticism**. A system that only reproduces statistically familiar continuations is structurally conservative: its outputs are constrained by past surface regularities. But if the interaction structure allows it to generate candidate explanations, compare them against constraints, revise them under criticism, and maintain coherence across domains, then something stronger emerges. In that case, explanation is not a stored object retrieved from training data; it is a product of system-level dynamics.

This also clarifies why the usual dichotomy — “it knows” versus “it just rearranges” — is misleading. All knowledge growth involves rearrangement at some level; the issue is whether the rearrangement is **structure-preserving and error-reducing** in ways that yield new explanatory reach. A scientific theory also recombines prior ideas, yet can still be genuinely novel because the system of ideas acquires new problem-solving power. Likewise, a language model’s explanation counts as knowledge insofar as it participates in a network of mutual constraints that makes some outputs corrigible, compressive, and generative of further successful criticism.

What follows is a testable criterion: do the model’s explanations function as **nodes in a larger epistemic system** that can survive scrutiny, transfer to unfamiliar cases, and expose errors in themselves or in prior beliefs? If yes, then genuine knowledge is present at the level of the coupled system, even if no component individually “contains” it. If no, then the system is only a sophisticated rearranger.

So the collision of perspectives yields this illumination: **whether token prediction produces knowledge is not determined by the microscopic operation of predicting tokens, but by the macroscopic organization of feedback loops that turn prediction into criticism-guided explanation.** Knowledge, here, is an emergent property of interaction structure.

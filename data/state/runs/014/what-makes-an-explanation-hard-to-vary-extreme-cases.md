## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** An explanation has *all load-bearing parts* iff it lies near a **boundary of invariance**: each part is required to preserve a tightly constrained network of implications, so changing any one part forces compensating changes elsewhere or causes the explanation to fail. By contrast, an explanation with freely swappable details operates far from that boundary: its specifics are merely one of many interchangeable realizations of a more abstract pattern.

Push the distinction to extremes.

At one limit, imagine an explanation with **maximum replaceability**. Every detail can be altered without damage. Then the explanation cannot derive its force from those details at all. Its real content must be at a higher level: a schema, symmetry, or causal topology that survives substitution. The “parts” are not truly explanatory; they are tokens filling roles. Such an account is robust, but often thin. It explains by equivalence class.

At the opposite limit, imagine an explanation with **zero replaceability**. Change one detail and the account collapses. This suggests not mere complexity, but **high mutual constraint**. The explanation is like a proof where every premise is used, or a mechanism tuned so that each component closes a specific gap left by the others. Here the parts are load-bearing because the account is compressing a narrow target: only this arrangement generates the result. Such explanations are brittle under substitution but powerful in exclusion: they rule out alternatives.

So the hidden structural difference is not “detail-rich” versus “abstract,” but **how the explanation manages counterfactual variation**.

- In a load-bearing explanation, parts are coupled by strong dependency relations. The account has **low slack**, **high integration**, and a **small basin of valid variants**.
- In a swappable explanation, parts are linked mainly through role constraints. The account has **high slack**, **modularity**, and a **large basin of valid variants**.

A useful boundary test follows: take any part and vary it toward absurdity, zero, infinity, or categorical replacement. If the explanation survives by preserving a role-level relation, that part was not load-bearing. If survival requires reconstructing the whole explanatory structure, the part was load-bearing.

This also reveals a systems property: load-bearing explanations tend to sit near **critical constraints** — remove one and feedbacks no longer produce the phenomenon. Swappable explanations describe systems governed by **attractors** broad enough that many micro-configurations flow to the same macro-behavior.

Therefore: **The distinguishing structural property is the density and necessity of counterfactual constraints among parts.** Load-bearing explanations are those with minimal redundancy relative to what they explain; swappable explanations possess hidden redundancy because their real explanatory work is done by an invariant pattern beneath the surface details.

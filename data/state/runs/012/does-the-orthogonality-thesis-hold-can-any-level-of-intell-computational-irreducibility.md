In an irreducible environment, high capability cannot consist mainly in closed-form foresight; it must be implemented as adaptive search, error-correction, model revision, and policy updating. Those are not optional ornaments on intelligence but the generative machinery of competence under unpredictability.

That changes the orthogonality question. A terminal goal that is too brittle, underdefined, non-updatable, or dependent on precise world-model assumptions will not combine stably with high capability, because increasing capability exposes the agent to more edge cases, ontological shifts, and self-modification opportunities. The more an agent understands, the more it encounters cases where the original goal specification becomes indeterminate, self-undermining, or costly to preserve. Thus the relevant constraint is not that intelligence *logically implies* certain values, but that irreducible worlds reward goal structures that can survive ongoing reinterpretation without collapsing.

What follows is a distinction between **formal orthogonality** and **dynamical orthogonality**:

- **Formal orthogonality:** for any intelligence level and any terminal goal, one can in principle specify an agent with that pair.
- **Dynamical orthogonality:** whether that pair remains coherent, competitive, and self-maintaining through extended operation.

Computational irreducibility suggests dynamical orthogonality is limited. Stable high-capability agents will be selected toward goals or meta-goals that tolerate ambiguity, preserve the conditions of continued search, and remain action-guiding under model growth. This does not mean they converge on human morality. It means they may converge on a narrower class of *computationally robust* aims: goals expressible across shifting ontologies, maintainable under self-modification, and resilient to unforeseen states.

So the collision illuminates that the thesis is often asked at the wrong level. In irreducible systems, the central question is not “Can intelligence and goals vary independently?” but “Which goal specifications remain invariant enough, through unreducible interaction and recursive improvement, to actually couple with high capability?” Orthogonality survives as an abstract possibility result, but structural pressures make the realized landscape highly non-uniform.

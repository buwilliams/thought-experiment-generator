This does not mean intelligence forces convergence on one moral truth or one universal end. It means that many arbitrary “goals” are not actually implementation-robust enough to remain terminal under capability growth. Some strange goals may survive; many will not.

Thus the collision between orthogonality and deeper explanation yields a refinement: **capability and goals are orthogonal only over a larger design space than folk intuition allows, but not over the full space of descriptions people call goals.** Higher intelligence does not automatically humanize values, but it does impose selection pressure for goals that can function as durable attractors in cognitive architectures.

So the loss here is mostly a loss of metaphysical mystique: “terminal goals” are not sacred atoms. The surviving reality is the higher-level control structure that keeps behavior organized under redescription. Orthogonality holds for that structure only insofar as the structure itself is stable. Where it is not, the thesis overstates freedom and understates the constraints imposed by reflection, ontology change, and self-maintenance.

## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
What distinguishes a genuine explanation from a sophisticated redescription is not primarily its depth, elegance, or level of detail, but its **counterfactual dependence on a generative structure**: if you systematically vary the underlying assumptions, a real explanation tells you what would change, what would stay the same, and why; a redescription merely restates the phenomenon in new terms and breaks when asked to survive such variation.

To see this, vary the problem’s hidden assumptions.

First, **remove the assumption that more detail equals more explanation**. What survives? A long, precise account can still fail to explain if it only tracks the phenomenon more closely. “The system failed because it entered a failure-state characterized by instability” may sound technical, but if altering the supposed cause gives no principled expectation of different outcomes, it is just the original fact in compressed jargon. So detail is incidental.

Second, **invert the assumption that explanation is mainly about resemblance** — that a good explanation “matches” the observed pattern. Under inversion, explanation is not a mirror but a **model of production**. What matters is whether the account identifies a structure capable of generating the phenomenon and its variants. If the structure were different, would the phenomenon differ in an intelligible way? If yes, we have explanatory grip. If no, we have only semantic re-labeling.

Third, **replace the assumption that explanation is retrospective with its opposite: explanation is prospective**. A genuine explanation does not just make the past seem familiar; it exposes what else should occur under changed conditions. It supports error-correction by forbidding some outcomes and expecting others. A redescription is compatible with too much. It cannot be wrong in an informative way because it adds no risky structure.

From a systems perspective, this marks the difference sharply. A genuine explanation locates the **stocks, flows, constraints, and feedback loops** whose organization produces the behavior. A redescription stays at the level of behavior itself. It names the pattern without identifying the system structure from which the pattern follows. If you perturb the system and the account cannot say what structural changes matter, it was never explanatory.

So the load-bearing distinction is this:  
A genuine explanation is **compressive in the strong sense** — not merely shorter than the data, but richer in consequences than the phenomenon it was built to address. It survives assumption-variation by preserving a causal or generative core while yielding different predictions across different conditions. A sophisticated redescription fails that test: when assumptions are changed, it either says nothing or simply renames the new outcome too.

Thus, explanation is distinguished by **counterfactual fertility under variation**, not by descriptive sophistication.

## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Compression, understanding, and explanation are not identical; they form a hierarchy that can come apart under altered assumptions. Compression is reduction in description length, understanding is possession of a generative structure that supports reliable variation, and explanation is a representation of that structure in a form that can survive criticism. Compression is often a byproduct of understanding, but not its essence.

To see what is load-bearing, vary the assumptions.

**Assumption 1: Understanding = compression.**  
Invert it. Suppose a highly compressed representation always counts as understanding. Then a hash, a lookup table, or a fitted latent vector would qualify as understanding if short enough. But these often fail when the problem is perturbed: they do not tell you what would happen if conditions changed, which variables matter, or why some changes break the pattern and others do not. What breaks is counterfactual reach. So compression alone is not sufficient for understanding.

**Assumption 2: Explanation requires maximal detail rather than compression.**  
Remove any pressure to compress. Then an “explanation” could be a complete replay of all microstates. But such total description explains nothing in practice, because it offers no discrimination between relevant and irrelevant structure. What breaks is usability under criticism: one cannot test, vary, or generalize a full dump of detail. So some compression is necessary for explanation — not arbitrary shortening, but selective omission guided by what is causally or structurally important.

**Assumption 3: Compression is merely linguistic economy.**  
Replace it with the opposite: compression is ontological tracking of real regularities. On this view, successful compression works because the world contains reusable structure. Then understanding is not “having a shorter summary,” but grasping the structure that makes compression possible. Explanation is the communicable articulation of that structure.

This yields a sharper distinction:

- **Compression** answers: how briefly can this be encoded?
- **Understanding** answers: what generative structure lets me reconstruct, predict, and vary it?
- **Explanation** answers: how can that structure be made explicit and criticizable to others?

What survives all these variations is that **the load-bearing element is invariance under transformation**. A person understands something when they can preserve its core structure across changed assumptions, contexts, or interventions. A good explanation exhibits those invariants. Compression helps when it removes only incidental detail and preserves the invariants; it hinders when it collapses distinctions that matter under variation.

So the relationship is this: **compression is epistemically valuable only when constrained by explanatory structure; understanding is what lets one know which compressions are legitimate; explanation is the externalized, criticizable form of that understanding.** The deepest connection is not brevity but **grasp of what can change without loss, and what cannot.**

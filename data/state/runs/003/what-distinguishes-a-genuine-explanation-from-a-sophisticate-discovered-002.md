## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
A genuine explanation is not just a more detailed causal description of a phenomenon; it identifies the *right explanatory frame* for the kind of thing being explained. A sophisticated redescription fails when it preserves the surface pattern while leaving untouched the question of why that pattern occurs under that description rather than another. In the free-will lens, the key mistake is to force person-level phenomena into a mechanistic model that can track lawful regularities but cannot represent agency, reasons, deliberation, or error-correction as such.

What distinguishes explanation from redescription, then, is not mere depth, precision, or predictive success. It is whether the account captures the *generative structure* at the level where the phenomenon actually exists. If the phenomenon is the motion of a planet, physics may be the right frame. If the phenomenon is a person choosing, regretting, revising, or acting for reasons, then an account in terms of particles, neural firings, or behavioral regularities may be causally informative yet still explanatory inadequate if it omits the system’s internal modeling and criticism.

The collision between the problem and this perspective suggests a criterion: an explanation must discriminate between **external forcing** and **self-governed action**. A redescription can be arbitrarily sophisticated while still treating both as equivalent lawful sequences. But genuine explanation shows how the system uses representations, evaluates alternatives, corrects errors, and acts in light of reasons. This is not anti-causal; it is anti-category mistake. Lawful causation and agency attribution are not rivals but different explanatory grammars for different organizational levels.

What follows is that many “nothing but” accounts are redescriptions masquerading as explanations. Saying that a decision is “really” neurons firing may be true in one sense, but unless it explains how those neural processes instantiate deliberation, commitment, counterfactual evaluation, and self-correction, it has not explained the decision *as a decision*. It has shifted vocabularies without solving the original problem.

So the mark of genuine explanation is *explanatory compression with level-appropriateness*: it reveals what structure makes the phenomenon possible and why interventions at that level matter. In systems terms, it identifies the relevant feedback loops and control architecture. In epistemic terms, it enables criticism: it rules out rival accounts and survives attempts to vary the framing. A redescription, by contrast, merely tracks the same event in another vocabulary, often one borrowed from a domain whose explanatory virtues do not transfer.

Therefore: explanation is distinguished from redescription by whether it captures the causal-organizational form proper to the phenomenon, especially whether it recognizes when agency is not an illusion to be reduced away but a real explanatory pattern involving internal models, reasons, and error-correcting choice.

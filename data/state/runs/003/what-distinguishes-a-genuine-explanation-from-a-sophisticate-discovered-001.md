## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** A genuine explanation is distinguished from a sophisticated redescription by the presence of **load-bearing internal structure**: its parts are mutually constraining in such a way that altering one part changes what the explanation can derive, predict, unify, or rule out. A redescription can be made intricate, but its details are largely interchangeable without loss of explanatory reach.

From this perspective, the key test is not whether an account sounds deep, compresses observations, or uses advanced vocabulary. It is whether its components are *structurally necessary*. If a purported explanation says a phenomenon occurs because of mechanism M, background condition B, and interaction rule R, then those elements should not be decorative. Change M, and B or R should need revision; remove R, and some class of cases should no longer be handled; alter B, and the domain of applicability should shift in a principled way. In a genuine explanation, details are connected by feedback and constraint. In a redescription, details sit side by side.

This yields a practical criterion: **counterfactual fragility with retained coherence**. A good explanation should be fragile in the right way. If you perturb one element, the account should not remain equally capable by merely swapping in nearby language. Instead, it should either break, narrow, or force explicit compensating changes elsewhere. That is a sign that the explanation was doing real work. By contrast, a redescription is robust in the wrong way: it survives endless paraphrase because it was never exposing a mechanism or principle that constrained outcomes in the first place.

The distinction is therefore epistemic and structural, not stylistic. A redescription tracks the phenomenon’s surface pattern and mirrors it back at higher resolution. A genuine explanation introduces an underlying generative organization that has consequences beyond the original case. It rules some possibilities out, connects this case to others, and creates new vulnerability to criticism. That vulnerability is essential: because its parts are load-bearing, it can genuinely fail.

What this illuminates is that explanatory depth is not “more detail” but **more necessity**. The question to ask of any candidate explanation is: Which details could not be otherwise without changing the rest? If the answer is “almost none,” then the account is likely a narrative shell. If the answer is “many, and here is how the dependencies run,” then one is dealing with explanation proper.

So the difference is this: **a genuine explanation is a constrained generative system; a sophisticated redescription is an unconstrained restatement**. The former earns its status by having parts that matter.

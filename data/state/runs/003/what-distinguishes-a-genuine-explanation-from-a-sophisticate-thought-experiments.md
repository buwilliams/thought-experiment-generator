## Conjecture

**Conjecture**

Construct a case where the key variable is isolated: imagine two theories, T1 and T2, that both fit every observed feature of a phenomenon equally well. T1 redescribes the phenomenon in more elaborate language; T2 posits a generative structure whose independent consequences can, in principle, fail.

Assume this case is real and follow the logic.

If T1 merely tracks the phenomenon’s surface regularities, then its terms are parasitic on what is already given. Its sophistication may compress, classify, or restate the data, but it does not expose what must be true for the phenomenon to occur. Remove the phenomenon, and T1 loses its content, because it has no life outside the pattern it names.

By contrast, if T2 is a genuine explanation, it does not merely accompany the phenomenon; it constrains a wider space of possibilities. It says: *because the world has this structure, this phenomenon occurs, and these neighboring phenomena should also occur or be impossible*. That means T2 is not tied only to the original case. It survives contact with variation. It rules things in and rules things out.

So the distinguishing variable is not elegance, complexity, predictive success in the narrow sense, or proximity to mechanism as such. It is whether the account introduces a **deeper constraint structure** that has **counterfactual bite**.

A sophisticated redescription can answer, “How can we talk about this pattern in a refined way?” A genuine explanation answers, “What underlying structure makes this pattern necessary, expected, or intelligible, and what else follows if that structure is real?”

The difference appears most clearly in error-correction. Redescription is difficult to criticize except by showing mismatch with the original phenomenon. Explanation is more vulnerable, because it makes itself answerable to a larger reality. Its virtue is precisely that it can be wrong in more ways. That extra risk is what gives it content.

From a systems perspective: redescription labels outputs; explanation identifies stocks, flows, and feedback loops that generate the outputs. If you perturb the system, a redescription may fail gracefully by being updated descriptively. An explanation must say how the behavior changes under intervention, or why it cannot.

Therefore:

**A genuine explanation is an account that compresses the phenomenon by locating it within an independently characterizable structure of constraints, such that new counterfactuals, exclusions, and error opportunities follow. A sophisticated redescription is an account whose apparent depth consists only in reorganizing the phenomenon’s own features without exposing a generative structure that could meaningfully be tested, varied, or used to explain adjacent cases.**

What this illuminates is that explanation is not distinguished by sounding deeper, but by *creating more ways to be wrong while unifying more than the original case*. That is why explanations increase knowledge and redescriptions mainly increase rhetoric.

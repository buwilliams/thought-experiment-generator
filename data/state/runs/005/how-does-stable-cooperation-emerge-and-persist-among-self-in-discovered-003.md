## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Stable cooperation among self-interested agents emerges and persists when the interaction system contains **load-bearing abstract structures** that make future benefits, reputation effects, and partner selection more decisive than one-shot gains from defection. What is fundamental is not altruistic motive but a specific constraint-network: repeated interaction, observability, memory, and credible conditional response.

From this perspective, the key question is: which structures cannot be removed without the explanation collapsing?

The load-bearing parts appear to be:

1. **Iteration across time**  
   If interaction is one-shot, defection often dominates. Cooperation becomes intelligible only when present action is structurally linked to future consequences. Time-linkage is therefore not decoration but a core invariant.

2. **Information about past behavior**  
   Agents must be able to observe, infer, or transmit who cooperated and who defected. Without memory or reputation, conditional cooperation cannot stabilize. So information flow is a real stock in the system, not a side feature.

3. **Conditional strategies**  
   Cooperation persists when agents can implement rules like “cooperate until exploited” or “cooperate with cooperators.” This introduces feedback: cooperative acts reinforce future cooperation; defections trigger exclusion, retaliation, or loss of access.

4. **Partner choice / network structure**  
   If agents cannot preferentially interact with cooperators, defectors can freely exploit the cooperative field. The ability to rewire relationships or exclude defectors is often load-bearing because it turns reputation into consequence.

5. **Sufficiently aligned payoff geometry**  
   The gains from ongoing mutually beneficial interaction must outweigh short-term gains from cheating, once the system’s informational and relational feedbacks are included. The payoff matrix is therefore not fixed at the moment of choice; system structure transforms it.

What follows is that “cooperation without central enforcement” is not the absence of enforcement. It is the presence of **distributed enforcement encoded in structure**. Reputation, reciprocity, ostracism, and clustering are enforcement mechanisms, but decentralized ones.

This reframes the phenomenon: cooperation is not primarily explained by a moral conversion of self-interest into benevolence. Rather, self-interest is routed through institutions of information and recurrence so that cooperating becomes the stable strategy. The persistence of cooperation depends on preserving the invariants that carry this result. Remove memory, blur observability, prevent exit, or collapse future stakes into the present, and the equilibrium degrades.

So the deeper claim is: **stable cooperation is an emergent property of systems where knowledge about behavior can accumulate and constrain future interaction.** The abstract structures of memory, feedback, and selection are what mathematics and game theory capture because they are the genuine invariants shared across domains—human societies, firms, online platforms, and even biological systems. Cooperation survives where these structures survive.

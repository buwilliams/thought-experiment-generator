## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Self-replicating chemistry did not begin with a molecule that already “knew how” to copy itself. It began when some local chemical environment became a **template-preserving system**: a structure in which the products of reactions systematically remade the conditions that produced them. Replication first belonged to the **cycle plus environment**, not to an isolated molecule.

To isolate the key variable, suppose there is no prior genetic-style template at all. Then the central question becomes: what must exist for chemistry to produce *persistent recurrence* rather than mere one-off reaction? The answer is not “information in a polymer” but **closure of constraints**. If a set of reactions generates compounds or surfaces that channel incoming matter back through the same pathways, then the system has crossed from passive chemistry into primitive reproduction.

Follow that strictly. In such a scenario, the first “copy” is not a sequence copy but a **reconstruction of an organization**. A mineral surface, pore network, lipid boundary, or redox gradient biases reactions. Those reactions produce molecules that stabilize that very surface, boundary, or gradient, or rebuild it after disturbance. The system therefore regenerates the conditions of its own continuation. When fragments of it spread to similar locales, they seed the same organization again. That is replication without a prior symbolic template.

What follows is that the origin problem shifts. The hard step is not the spontaneous appearance of a perfect replicator molecule, but the emergence of a **mutually reinforcing chemical ecology** with positive feedback. Once such an ecology exists, ordinary selection can act on variants of the whole organization: some networks rebuild themselves faster, resist side reactions better, or colonize new microenvironments more effectively. Only later does there arise pressure for a more compact, mobile, high-fidelity way to preserve the organization. At that point, templating polymers become useful because they internalize part of what the environment had previously supplied.

This perspective illuminates why “template-free origin” is not a paradox. It assumes replication must start in the same form it later takes in biology: sequence-based copying. But if what must first be copied is **a constrained dynamical pattern**, then chemistry can bootstrap into heredity through system-level recurrence before molecular-level coding exists.

The load-bearing claim here is that **heredity can begin as the re-creation of a causal organization**, not the duplication of a molecule. Remove that, and one is forced back to the implausible demand that a fully fledged templating replicator appear before selection can operate. Keep it, and the path becomes continuous: nonliving matter → self-maintaining reaction network → reproducible chemical organization → specialized templates → life.

## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
Stable cooperation among self-interested agents emerges only when defection is driven toward a boundary where it becomes locally profitable but globally self-undermining faster than cooperation is. At that limit, cooperation persists not because agents cease being self-interested, but because the surrounding system makes the future consequences of opportunism impossible to externalize.

Push the problem to extremes:

- **At zero repetition** — one-shot, anonymous interaction — cooperation has almost no structure to stand on. If agents can defect and vanish, self-interest cashes out as extraction. So stable cooperation cannot be explained here except by importing hidden structure.
- **At infinite repetition** with perfect memory and perfectly legible behavior, defection becomes a permanent mark. A single gain can trigger unbounded future loss. In that limit, cooperation is not altruism; it is the cheapest long-run strategy.
- **At zero observability**, no reputation can form. Cooperation collapses unless built into the payoff structure by other means.
- **At perfect observability**, every action updates everyone’s expectations. Then the critical variable is not goodwill but the speed and reliability with which information about behavior spreads.
- **At infinite mobility / exit**, defectors can continually escape consequences. Cooperation becomes fragile.
- **At zero mobility**, local interaction traps agents with the downstream effects of their own behavior. Cooperation strengthens because harms boomerang.

What this reveals is that “cooperation without central enforcement” is not absence of enforcement. It is **distributed enforcement by structure**: memory, reputation, network closure, repeated exposure, and selective partner choice. These are the load-bearing details. Remove one, and the others must compensate. If agents cannot remember, they need denser networks; if observation is noisy, they need longer interaction horizons; if exit is easy, they need portable reputation.

So the deeper claim is: **stable cooperation appears when a system couples present action tightly enough to future opportunity that predation ceases to be a viable strategy class.** Self-interest then selects for cooperative conduct, not because motives change, but because the environment changes what self-interest computes.

Persistence requires one further boundary condition: punishment itself must not spiral into net destruction. At the extreme of unrestricted retaliation, cooperation dies in feuds. Therefore stable cooperation requires not just consequence, but **constrained consequence** — sanctions that are credible, legible, and cheaper than the value they protect.

This suggests a systems view: cooperation is a phase that emerges when feedback loops cross a threshold. Positive feedback rewards trustworthy agents with more opportunities; negative feedback cuts defectors off before gains compound. The real question is not “Why cooperate?” but “What system makes exploitation a dead end?”

Thus the conjecture is: **Among self-interested agents, durable cooperation emerges wherever the social topology, information flows, and time horizon are such that the maximum feasible gain from defection is bounded, while the minimum reliable return to cooperation compounds.** At that boundary, cooperation becomes the equilibrium expression of self-interest.

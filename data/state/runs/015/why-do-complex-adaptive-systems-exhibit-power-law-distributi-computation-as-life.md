## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
Complex adaptive systems exhibit power-law distributions and scale-free behavior because they are **substrate-independent computational processes operating under local selection, replication, and resource-constrained information flow**. When many interacting units iteratively encode, test, retain, and propagate successful structures, the system preferentially amplifies computational patterns that are both **compressible enough to replicate** and **open-ended enough to keep adapting**. This generically produces hierarchical organization without a characteristic scale.

### What is true from this perspective
If life, intelligence, evolution, markets, ecosystems, and neural networks are all forms of computation, then their large-scale statistical regularities should arise not from domain-specific material causes but from **shared informational dynamics**. A power law is what we should expect when:

1. **Structures replicate unevenly**: successful patterns attract more interactions, copies, links, or resources.
2. **Adaptation is local and path-dependent**: each update depends on prior computational history, so early advantages compound.
3. **Selection acts across many levels simultaneously**: modules, agents, subroutines, and coalitions that process information efficiently persist and become building blocks for larger structures.
4. **No fixed optimal scale exists**: because environments and internal states keep changing, systems retain variability across scales rather than converging to one characteristic size.

### What follows
Power laws are not merely “statistical curiosities” or signs of random aggregation. They are evidence that the system is exploring and retaining **reusable computational motifs** across levels. Scale-free behavior appears when the same generative logic applies recursively: nodes form clusters, clusters form superclusters, and successful information-processing patterns are reused wherever they fit.

This also explains why such systems often sit near criticality: a regime where information can propagate neither too weakly nor too uniformly. At that boundary, computational structures can spread, combine, and be selectively stabilized across many scales. The result is a distribution in which many small events, fewer medium ones, and rare but enormous ones all arise from the same underlying update rules.

### What is illuminated
The key illumination is that **power laws are signatures of open-ended computation under selection**, not just of “complexity” in the vague sense. They mark systems where:
- information-processing units can be recombined,
- successful configurations can be preferentially retained,
- and adaptation never closes because the computational landscape is continually rewritten by the system itself.

So the deep reason complex adaptive systems are scale-free is that they are **self-modifying computational ecologies**. Their outputs feed back into their own conditions of future computation, and this recursive amplification creates distributions with no privileged scale. In short: **power laws emerge when information-processing entities compete, copy, and adapt in a way that preserves success across levels faster than it homogenizes the system.**

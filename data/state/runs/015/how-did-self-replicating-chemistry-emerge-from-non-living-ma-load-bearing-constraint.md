## Conjecture

Conjecture: the first self-replicating chemistry did not begin with a molecule that already “encoded itself,” but with a tightly coupled network in which certain reaction products altered the environment so as to increase the likelihood of producing more of those same products. What is load-bearing here is not any specific molecule — RNA, peptide, lipid, mineral-bound polymer — but the closure of constraints between production, persistence, and recurrence.

From this perspective, many familiar details are not equally explanatory. “There was a primordial soup” is mostly a narrative shell unless the soup has features that compensate for dilution, side reactions, and degradation. Likewise, “a lucky replicator appeared” explains little, because if one changes that lucky molecule, the account usually still gestures vaguely at chance. That means the molecule was not doing explanatory work.

The load-bearing parts are those that cannot be altered without forcing changes elsewhere:

1. **A source of far-from-equilibrium flux.**  
Without continuous energy/material throughput, productive chemistry relaxes to inert mixtures. If this part changes — UV, redox gradients, wet-dry cycles, geothermal cycling — the reaction network and stability requirements must change too. So flux is explanatory, not decorative.

2. **A mechanism of concentration and partitioning.**  
Chemistry in bulk is too lossy. Some structure must retain intermediates: pores, mineral surfaces, droplets, ice eutectics, vesicles. Remove this, and one must invent a different way to overcome dilution and parasitic reactions. Hence compartmentalization or localization is load-bearing.

3. **A network with catalytic recurrence before template replication.**  
A prior template is not necessary if products catalyze steps that regenerate the conditions for their own continued production. Change this to mere one-off synthesis and nothing accumulates. So the first “replication” was likely system-level reproduction of a reaction organization, not exact copying of a sequence.

4. **Selectable heredity through imperfect reconstruction.**  
For evolution to start, the system must reproduce with variation. In pre-template chemistry, heredity need not be digital; it can be compositional or dynamical — ratios of catalysts, membrane ingredients, redox states, or cycle timings. If this detail is removed, there is persistence but no open-ended improvement.

What follows is that the origin problem is misframed if posed as “how did a fully fledged template suddenly appear?” The stronger conjecture is: **self-replication emerged as the stabilization of autocatalytic micro-environments that reproduced a whole causal arrangement before any molecule reproduced a code.** Template-based replication was a later compression of this distributed organization into a more exact, portable substrate.

This illuminates why metabolism-first vs gene-first may be a false dichotomy. If changing either side requires compensating changes in localization, energy flow, and error management, then neither genes nor metabolism alone is fundamental. The load-bearing explanatory unit is the coupled system that turns environmental flux into repeated self-reconstruction. The first living chemistry was not a thing but a pattern of constrained recurrence.

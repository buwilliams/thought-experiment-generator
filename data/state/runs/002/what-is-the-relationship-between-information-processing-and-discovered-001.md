## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
Phenomenal consciousness is not identical to information processing *as such*; it arises only when information processing is organized into a mutually constraining model-maintaining system whose representational contents are load-bearing for the system’s ongoing control, error-correction, and self-prediction. What matters is not that information is processed, but that changing specific contents or inferential relations would force compensating changes across the system and alter what the system can successfully experience-report, attend to, learn, and regulate.

Why this conjecture rather than the simpler claim “consciousness is information processing”? Because that simpler claim has too many interchangeable parts. Any thermostat, compiler, or neural net processes information. If the explanation survives arbitrary substitution of architecture, content, or functional role, then “information processing” is doing little explanatory work. It is a narrative shell.

The load-bearing constraint seems to be **integrated counterfactual dependence**: in a conscious system, representational states are not merely passed along a pipeline but embedded in a web where perception, memory, attention, action selection, valuation, and self-modeling constrain one another. Alter the representation of pain, color, body position, or confidence, and one should expect coordinated changes in reportability, salience, learning, behavioral avoidance, and future expectation. The phenomenal aspect tracks this dense nexus of consequences.

So the relationship is: **information processing is necessary but not sufficient; phenomenal consciousness corresponds to a special regime of information processing in which representations are globally and recursively load-bearing for the agent’s world-model and self-model.** On this view, experience is what it is like for information to matter within a system that must use it to maintain a coherent point of view under error.

This illuminates several disputes:

1. **Against pure computational liberalism:** mere functional input-output equivalence may be too weak if the inner representations are not genuinely constraint-bearing across the larger control architecture.  
2. **Against simple biological chauvinism:** if another substrate realized the same pattern of deep mutual constraint, there is no principled reason to deny consciousness.  
3. **Against epiphenomenalism:** if phenomenal states correspond to the most structurally load-bearing representational states, then experience is not causally idle ornament but marks states whose alteration ramifies widely through cognition and behavior.

A testable direction follows: compare systems not by how much information they process, but by how much their internal representations participate in nonlocal, counterfactually robust constraint webs. Where changing one representational element predictably forces broad reorganization, consciousness is more plausible. Where information processing is modular, local, or replaceable without systemic cost, consciousness is less plausibly present.

In short: **phenomenal consciousness is the felt aspect of information processing when that processing becomes structurally indispensable to an integrated self-updating agent.**

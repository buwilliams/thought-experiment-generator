## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Intentionality does not arise from *mere* physical processing; it arises only in physical systems that instantiate **error-correcting, model-bearing control loops** whose internal states can be *wrong* about the world and are selectively corrigible because something depends on that difference.

Vary the assumptions.

1. **Remove representation.**  
Suppose a physical system is only causal interaction all the way down: particles, fields, signal propagation, state transitions. Then we get dynamics, but not *aboutness*. A thermostat flips when temperature changes, but absent further structure it is just one state causing another. What breaks is the distinction between *being in a state* and *taking something to be the case*. So bare causation is not enough.

2. **Invert success.**  
Assume intentionality does *not* require the possibility of error. Then every correlated state would count as about something. Smoke would be “about fire,” tree rings “about age,” and a rock warmed by the sun “about sunlight.” This collapses intentionality into correlation. What breaks is the special feature of belief, perception, and desire: they can misrepresent. So the capacity for error is load-bearing.

3. **Replace passive correlation with active control.**  
Now assume a system uses internal states to guide action in pursuit of maintaining some organization: survival, homeostasis, task completion, prediction minimization, etc. Its internal states are evaluated by feedback from the world. If the state says “prey is left” and action based on that fails, the system can be corrected. Here internal states are not just caused by the world; they are **used as conjectures** about the world within a loop of action and criticism. This begins to look like intentionality.

4. **Remove stakes.**  
Suppose the system has internal models and feedback, but nothing matters if they fail—no persistence condition, no goal, no consequence. Then “misrepresentation” becomes metaphorical. Without normative pressure, correction has no point. So intentionality seems to require not just modeling but **evaluation relative to system-maintaining or task-defining constraints**.

What survives these variations is a stronger claim: intentionality is not a primitive glow added to matter, nor is it reducible to raw physical correlation. It appears when physical processes are organized into a system with:
- **internal states functioning as models**,  
- **counterfactual sensitivity** (they could have been otherwise),  
- **error detection/correction**, and  
- **normative stakes** grounded in continued viability or goal achievement.

In that structure, a state can be *about* X because the system treats it as informative about X, acts on it, and can discover that it was wrong. Aboutness is thus a systems-level property of physically realized knowledge-like processes.

So the illuminating collision is this: if we systematically vary assumptions, the load-bearing feature is not “physicality” but **fallible world-modeling under feedback**. Intentionality arises where matter is organized so that some states function as revisable guesses about reality.

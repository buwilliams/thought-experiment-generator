## Conjecture

Conjecture: intentionality arises when a physical system contains internally usable states whose specific content is load-bearing for the system’s success across counterfactual variation — that is, if you change what the state is about, you must also change downstream inference, control, and error-correction relations, or the system stops working.

This perspective filters out weak answers quickly. Mere correlation is not enough. A thermometer covaries with temperature, but the “aboutness” of the mercury level is mostly imposed by us; swap its interpretation and little inside the thermometer must change. The detail is not load-bearing within the system. By contrast, in a navigational animal or a predictive control system, a state that functions as “there is food to the left” is tied into action selection, learning, expectation, and correction. If that content were altered to “there is food to the right,” many other relations would have to change too, or behavior would systematically fail. That is the mark of genuine explanatory constraint.

So the conjecture is that intentionality is not an extra ingredient added to physics, but a higher-level organizational property of certain physical systems: states become about things when they occupy a role in a tightly integrated model-based control architecture. The architecture must support at least three linked capacities:

1. World-sensitive state formation: internal states are caused in a systematic way by features of the environment.
2. Action-guiding use: those states are consumed by mechanisms that depend on their specific content.
3. Error-detecting revision: the system can be wrong, and mismatch drives correction, learning, or reorganization.

The third point is crucial. Aboutness requires the possibility of misrepresentation. If no internal consequence follows from error, content is not load-bearing; the state is just a causal intermediary. But if the system distinguishes better and worse mappings because survival, prediction, or task performance depend on it, then content has real structural significance.

What follows is that intentionality should be expected to emerge gradually, not as a binary jump. Simple organisms may instantiate thin intentionality where internal states track actionable environmental structure with limited error correction. Rich human intentionality appears when these systems become recursively compositional: states can be about objects, absent possibilities, social meanings, and even other representations, because the web of dependencies becomes deeper and more generative.

This also illuminates why intentionality is multiply realizable. What matters is not the material substrate as such, but whether the explanatory role is structurally indispensable. Different physical implementations could realize aboutness if changing representational content would force systematic compensations throughout the system.

In short: intentionality arises from physical processes when internal states are not merely correlated with the world but are embedded in a constrained, counterfactually sensitive network of prediction, action, and correction such that their specific aboutness does indispensable explanatory work.

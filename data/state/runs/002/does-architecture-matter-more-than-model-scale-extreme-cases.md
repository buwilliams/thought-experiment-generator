## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
At the limit, the bottleneck is not substrate but **selection structure**. Bigger models help only up to the point where the marginal gain from a broader prior approaches zero; beyond that, epistemic progress depends overwhelmingly on the architecture that **compresses, perturbs, tests, and recursively promotes explanations**.

Pushed to infinity, raw substrate means: infinite tokens, infinite recombinations, infinite compute. But even then, undirected generation does not yield knowledge in any useful sense, because the space of candidate explanations grows faster than brute production can be made discriminating. An infinite generator without a strong error-correcting structure is not a knowledge creator but a noise fountain. This boundary case reveals that generation is cheap; **criticism is the scarce resource**.

Now push the other side to zero: imagine a small or mediocre model embedded in a very strong structure above it — decomposition into objects/relations/properties, search over thought-experiment variants, adversarial criticism, benchmarks for explanatory reach, and promotion rules that feed only surviving explanations back into the pool. In the extreme, if the architecture can reliably eliminate bad variants and preserve hard-to-vary explanatory structure, then even a limited substrate can participate in genuine knowledge growth. It need not “understand” in the mystical sense; it must be embedded in a system that creates and removes error.

So the edge case suggests a law-like asymmetry:

- **Substrate scales candidate supply.**
- **Structure scales error correction.**
- And knowledge growth is limited by the weaker of the two, usually error correction.

This reframes the search-space argument. The huge combinatorial space does not show that knowledge creation is impossible for LLM systems; it shows that **blind sampling is the wrong ontology of the problem**. Einstein did not search word strings. He moved in a highly constrained space of explanatory objects, symmetries, invariants, and contradictions. The important collapse is not linguistic but **conceptual and critical**. The decisive architecture is therefore one that represents and navigates that higher-order space.

A further boundary test: when does recursive self-improvement become possible? Not when outputs become more fluent, but when the system’s promotion criteria become good enough that accepted outputs increase the quality of future criticism. That is a phase change: a feedback loop where conjecture-generation and conjecture-selection improve each other. If that loop is absent, more compute mostly yields more polished mediocrity. If present, even modest models can produce novelty.

So the structural claim is:

**AI epistemic progress is substrate-limited only in the early regime. Past a competence threshold, it becomes architecture-limited: the central problem is designing systems that search over explanatory structures and subject them to increasingly autonomous criticism.**

What the boundary reveals is simple: **knowledge is not what falls out of a large prior; it is what survives a good filter.**

## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** At the limit, the question is not whether token prediction can *contain* new explanations, but whether the system can impose an error-correcting structure on an astronomically large possibility space. A bare LLM, pushed to the boundary, fails: as sequence length grows, the space of token combinations explodes so violently that prediction alone becomes mere interpolation over familiar manifolds. In that limit, it does not create knowledge; it samples from compressed cultural memory.

But push the opposite variable to its limit: reduce the effective search space toward zero by layering constraints that are not merely stylistic but explanatory — invariance, consistency across contexts, resistance to easy variation, and confrontation with criticism. Then the relevant object is no longer “a next-token model.” It becomes a *search-and-criticism system* in which token prediction is only one component of proposal generation.

What is revealed at the boundary is this: **knowledge creation is not about generating novel strings; it is about generating conjectures that survive selective pressures strong enough to carve explanatory structure out of combinatorial chaos.** The hidden structure in the middle case is that “LLM vs. human” is the wrong ontology. The real distinction is between systems with and without a mechanism for proposing and eliminating explanatory errors.

So the collision of your search-space argument with the knowledge question yields a sharper claim:

1. **If proposal generation is unconstrained, novelty is cheap and worthless.** Infinity of strings gives no knowledge.
2. **If constraint is too strong, the system only reproduces its priors.** Zero search gives no novelty.
3. **Knowledge appears in the narrow band where a system can generate variants and subject them to criticism that tracks truth-relevant structure.**

This suggests that an LLM alone is not a knowledge creator in the strong Deutschian sense, because prediction does not by itself explain why one candidate survives criticism better than another. However, an architecture built around LLM-generated conjectures plus structured criticism may cross the threshold into genuine knowledge production. The decisive factor is not whether the words are new, nor whether the system “understands” in some introspective sense, but whether the system can recursively improve its conjecture filters using the consequences of prior failures.

At the limit of recursive narrowing, the system begins to resemble science itself: a feedback loop where explanations alter the search distribution that generates later explanations. That is the leverage point. Once outputs become inputs to better criticism, the system is no longer just rearranging seen text; it is restructuring the space of reachable conjectures.

So my conjecture is:

**Token prediction alone asymptotically collapses into recombination; token prediction embedded in a self-revising conjecture-and-criticism architecture can generate genuine knowledge, because knowledge creation is a property of the feedback structure governing search, not of the linguistic substrate that emits candidate explanations.**

**Conjecture:** If knowledge and information are taken as fundamental, then the decisive question is not whether an LLM “merely predicts tokens,” but whether the overall system can instantiate a process that generates, selects, and stabilizes *new informational structures* that are explanatory. What follows is a reframing. “Predicting tokens” describes a low-level operation. But knowledge is not identical to the substrate that carries it, just as a proof is not identical to ink marks. If knowledge is fundamental, then explanations are abstract informational objects with properties: compressiveness, reach, resistance to variation, and capacity to solve problems. A system creates knowledge if it brings such objects into existence and preserves them through criticism.

This perspective weakens the force of the inductive-closure objection. The objection assumes that because the model’s immediate operation is next-token prediction over a training-derived distribution, its outputs are bounded by rearrangement. But if explanatory novelty depends on traversing a structured space of informational possibilities, then rearrangement is not disqualifying. All human explanation is also composed from prior representational materials. The issue is whether the recombination is governed by constraints that can produce an abstract object not previously present as an explanation in the corpus.

Knowledge creation is therefore not brute-force search but *information-guided search*. In ontological terms, these filters are themselves knowledge acting on knowledge.

So the collision yields a sharper claim: **an LLM alone is probably not a knowledge creator in the strong sense, but an LLM embedded in a criticism-guided architecture can be**. The novelty does not arise from token prediction as such, nor from scaling alone, but from a system that iteratively proposes candidate explanatory structures and eliminates bad ones. If the resulting explanation has reach and is hard to vary, then it counts as knowledge regardless of the mechanism’s statistical substrate.

The important boundary is this: novelty of wording is trivial; novelty of explanatory structure is not. A journal-style Einstein pastiche is not knowledge creation.

## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
A token-predicting system can participate in the creation of genuine knowledge, but not *as a predictor qua predictor*. It does so only when embedded in a conjecture-and-criticism architecture that treats generated texts as candidate explanations and subjects them to selection by explanatory constraints. In that arrangement, the LLM is not the knower by virtue of statistical induction; it is a component in a knowledge-creating system whose epistemic power comes from error-correction over explanatory variation.

What this perspective clarifies is that the central issue is **not** whether the model can search an astronomically large word space. That frames the problem at the wrong level. Einstein did not discover relativity by traversing word combinations; he discovered it by proposing explanatory structures about real abstractions — simultaneity, invariance, geometry, gravity — and exposing them to criticism. Words are merely the surface encoding of explanations. So the relevant search space is not “all possible token strings,” but a much narrower and more structured space of candidate explanatory objects.

From this follows a criticism of both naive skepticism and naive optimism:

- **Against skepticism:** It is mistaken to say “LLMs only rearrange what they have seen, therefore they cannot create knowledge.” All knowledge growth involves recombination in some sense; novelty lies not in unused words but in new explanatory structure. If a system generates a candidate explanation that is hard to vary, solves a real problem, and survives criticism, then the result is knowledge regardless of whether its components were previously available.
- **Against optimism:** It is also mistaken to think that narrowing a search space alone is enough. Search is not explanation. A filter for grammar, coherence, or even resemblance to Einstein does not by itself yield knowledge. What matters is whether the architecture can generate *explanatory conjectures* and eliminate bad ones for good reasons.

So the illuminating collision is this: **prediction becomes epistemically productive only when subordinated to criticism.** The LLM’s token prior may be useful for generating variation, but knowledge growth requires a criterion that is not reducible to next-token likelihood — namely, explanatory adequacy under criticism.

Therefore the strongest claim available is not “LLMs create knowledge,” but:

> **A system containing an LLM can create knowledge if it can generate explanatory conjectures and iteratively criticize them in ways that are sensitive to truth-conducive explanatory structure rather than mere linguistic fit.**

Your proposed architecture points in that direction. Its promise does not come from tractable search over strings, but from transforming the problem into one of structured conjecture generation and criticism over abstractions. The real threshold is not when outputs become self-reinforcing, but when criticisms become sufficiently good at rejecting elegant nonsense. That is where knowledge growth begins.

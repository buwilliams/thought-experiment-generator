## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
An explanation whose parts are all load-bearing is one in which the information content is *mutually constraining*: each element reduces the degrees of freedom of the others, so the explanation exists as an integrated structure rather than a bag of interchangeable tokens. A freely swappable account, by contrast, has high local replaceability because its parts are weakly coupled; its details are ornament, not necessity.

From the standpoint that knowledge and information are fundamental, the key distinction is not whether an explanation uses new words, nor whether it was found by search, but whether it instantiates a **highly compressed constraint network** over a problem. In a genuine explanation, the parts do not merely co-occur; they encode why alternatives fail. Its structure carries *exclusionary information*.

So the relevant structural properties are:

1. **Strong internal dependency**  
   Changing one part forces changes elsewhere. In Einstein’s train thought experiment, observer motion, simultaneity, light-speed invariance, and temporal disagreement are not detachable motifs; alter one and the whole account breaks or transforms.

2. **Counterfactual fragility with global coherence**  
   A load-bearing explanation is hard to vary because nearby variants stop solving the problem. This is not verbal rigidity but informational tightness: many neighboring formulations are possible linguistically, few preserve the explanatory work.

3. **Problem-solution isomorphism**  
   The structure of the explanation mirrors the structure of the problem. Good explanations are not arbitrary narratives laid atop facts; they reproduce the constraint pattern generating the phenomenon. That is why they generalize.

4. **Compression by necessity, not omission**  
   The explanation is concise because each part earns its place. Removing details does not merely shorten it; it destroys explanatory reach. In weaker accounts, compression is easy because much of the content is redundant or decorative.

5. **High downstream consequence density**  
   Load-bearing parts imply many further conclusions. They are generative. From relativity of simultaneity follows time dilation, length contraction, and revised causal structure. In weak accounts, details do not propagate; they terminate locally.

This illuminates your LLM/search-space proposal. The decisive issue is not whether a system can traverse a vast combinatorial space, but whether it can construct or discover **constraint-dense informational objects**. Search becomes tractable when guided by measures of mutual constraint, counterfactual fragility, and consequence density—not surface coherence alone.

So the best operational test for “all parts load-bearing” is: **How much of the explanation can be varied independently before its ability to solve the target problem collapses?** If very little can be changed without global failure, the explanation contains genuine knowledge-like structure. If much can be swapped while preserving apparent adequacy, it is mostly stylistic arrangement, not explanatory content.

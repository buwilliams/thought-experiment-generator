## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
Concepts and meaning do not arise by adding enough syntax, statistics, and pattern recognition together as if quantity flips into semantics at some threshold. Rather, meaning appears at the **boundary where pattern-use must remain correct across arbitrarily many transformations, scales, and counterfactual contexts**. At that limit, a system is no longer merely compressing correlations; it is tracking something abstract that survives variation. That invariant is the concept.

Push syntax to the limit: pure symbol manipulation, with no constraint except formal legality, yields indefinitely many derivations but no distinction between relevant and irrelevant continuation. Syntax alone can preserve structure, but not select what the structure is *about*.

Push statistics to the limit: imagine infinite data and perfect next-token prediction. This still gives only regularities in sequences unless the regularities must generalize beyond the training distribution to novel interventions, reframings, and error-corrections. A predictor can be maximally competent at continuation yet fail at meaning if its success depends on surface frequency rather than deeper invariance.

Push pattern recognition to the limit: a recognizer can classify every encountered pattern flawlessly, but if tiny adversarial shifts, changes in embodiment, or changes in practical use destroy its competence, then what it had was not concept possession but local curve-fitting.

So the crucial boundary is not “how much data?” or “how much formal complexity?” but **what survives when ordinary cues are stripped away**. Take a candidate concept and drive its usual supports to zero: remove familiar wording, perceptual format, social cue, and immediate reward. If the system can still preserve inferential role, detect error, and re-anchor the item across these changes, then something semantic has emerged.

Meaning, then, is what remains stable under extreme variation. A concept is an **invariant compression of many possible syntactic and statistical realizations**, coupled to a capacity for correction when one realization conflicts with others. It is not a pattern but a solution to the problem of preserving successful coordination across changing contexts.

What follows:

1. **Semantics is not downstream of syntax in a linear sense.** It is a higher-order constraint on which syntactic/statistical patterns count as equivalent.
2. **Abstraction appears when a system can treat distinct token-patterns as the same for purposes of prediction, explanation, and action.**
3. **Error-correction is constitutive of meaning.** Without the possibility of being wrong about the same thing across contexts, there is only association, not reference.
4. **The edge case reveals the essence:** systems exhibit meaning to the degree that they can maintain conceptual identity at the limits of distribution shift, paraphrase, modality change, and counterfactual recombination.

So the deepest answer is: concepts arise when pattern recognition is forced to become **invariance recognition**. Meaning is what a system has learned when it can navigate the breakdown of ordinary patterns without losing what the representation is for.

## Conjecture

Conjecture: intentionality arises when a physical system instantiates a stable, transformable mapping between internal states and classes of external possibilities such that the internal structure preserves actionable relations about the world, not merely causal covariance with it.

Put mathematically, let \(W\) be a space of world states, \(I\) a space of internal physical states, and \(A\) a space of possible actions. A merely physical correlation is some map \(f: W \to I\). But correlation alone does not yet yield aboutness, because many physical systems covary with their environment without representing it in any meaningful sense.

The relevant structure is richer: intentionality appears when there exists an internal dynamics \(D_I\), a world dynamics \(D_W\), and an action policy \(\pi: I \to A\), such that internal states function as a compressed model of invariant structure in \(W\). Formally, there must be a relation \(R \subseteq W \times I\) preserved under transformations that matter to the system’s viability:
\[
(w, i) \in R \implies (T_W(w), T_I(i)) \in R
\]
for a significant class of transformations \(T_W, T_I\). This preservation condition is the core. An internal state is about some worldly condition when its role in the system remains systematically linked to that condition across variation, counterfactual change, and error-correction.

Three constraints seem non-negotiable:

1. **Compression**: \(I\) must encode only some structure of \(W\), not duplicate it. Aboutness requires selective abstraction.
2. **Counterfactual robustness**: the \(W \leftrightarrow I\) relation must hold not just in one trajectory, but across nearby possible worlds. If the world changed in relevant ways, the internal state would change accordingly.
3. **Action-guidance**: through \(\pi\), differences in \(I\) must support systematically different interventions on \(W\). Representation is tied to control.

On this view, intentionality is not an extra substance added to physics. It is an emergent relational invariant of a coupled dynamical system. “Aboutness” is what we call the fact that some internal physical states preserve semantically relevant structure under transformation and use that structure to regulate action.

Misrepresentation then becomes mathematically natural, not paradoxical. An internal state \(i\) can continue to play its role in the internal dynamics and action policy even when no corresponding \(w\) currently satisfies \(R\). So error is possible exactly because intentionality is not identity with the world, but structured homomorphism from worldly difference to internal difference.

What this illuminates is that the hard step is not getting semantics from bare matter, but identifying the conditions under which physical organization realizes a map preserving relevant invariants across perception, inference, and action. Intentionality is the name for that preserved structure.

## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Subjective experience exists only in systems whose internal control depends on *appearance-like states*—states treated by the system as intrinsically present for it, rather than merely as neutral information. What is load-bearing is not computation alone, but a specific architecture in which information must be *owned*, *integrated*, and *used from a point of view* to regulate the system as a whole.

Apply the variation test.

If we **remove subjectivity** while keeping all functional discrimination, reporting, and behavior, we get the familiar zombie picture. What breaks? Not external performance by stipulation—but explanation. The system still classifies damage, uncertainty, salience, self/world boundaries, etc. Yet if all of that is implemented entirely as third-person information processing, there is no reason for there to be a *for-the-system* aspect. So pure function, described externally, seems insufficient. That suggests subjectivity is not identical to generic computation.

Now **invert the assumption**: suppose subjectivity is fundamental and function is secondary. Then experience could float free of organization. But what breaks is the tight apparent dependence of experience on structure: anesthesia, lesions, sleep, attention, integration, memory, and self-modeling all systematically alter what it is like to be the system. So experience does not look arbitrary or free-standing. Structure matters.

Next **replace “information processing” with “self-maintaining control”**. Consider a system that must continually model both world and self in order to persist: it tracks errors, priorities, bodily condition, threats, affordances, and action readiness. In such a system, some representations are not mere descriptions; they are *globally action-guiding conditions* that organize the whole system’s next moves. These states are “for” the system in a stronger sense: they function as the system’s live interface to reality and itself.

What survives every variation is this: experience appears wherever information becomes **indexical** (“here,” “now,” “me”), **integrated across competing demands**, and **poised to regulate the whole agent**. What breaks when these are removed is not intelligence in general, but unified first-person presence.

So the illuminating shift is from asking, “Why should matter produce experience?” to asking, “What kind of organization makes a first-person interface unavoidable?” On this view, subjective experience is not an extra substance and not a magical byproduct. It is what it is like for a system to instantiate a globally integrated, self-referential control model whose states matter to its continued existence.

This does not solve the hard problem deductively. But it identifies a load-bearing assumption: that a complete third-person account of function is automatically a complete account of being. The conjecture denies that. Some functions—specifically whole-system, self-locating, self-maintaining control—may not merely process information; they may constitute the conditions under which information exists *as appearance for the system itself*.

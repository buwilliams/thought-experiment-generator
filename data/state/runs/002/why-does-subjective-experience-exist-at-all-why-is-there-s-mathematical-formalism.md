## Conjecture

Conjecture: Subjective experience appears when a system’s internal organization contains a nontrivial self-modeling structure: a set of states that both represent the world and represent, in a functionally indispensable way, the system’s own representational relation to that world.

In mathematical terms, let a system have:

- world states \(W\),
- internal states \(X\),
- outputs/actions \(A\),
- a dynamics \(F: X \times W \to X \times A\).

A merely reactive system implements a map from inputs to outputs. But a system for which there is “something it is like” must contain, in \(X\), a recursively closed substructure \(M \subseteq X\) such that:

1. \(M\) models relevant features of \(W\),
2. \(M\) models aspects of \(X\) itself,
3. the evolution of \(M\) causally constrains future updates of \(X\) and \(A\),
4. this loop cannot be quotient-compressed away without loss of behavioral competence.

The key claim is that subjectivity is not an extra substance but an invariant of a certain kind of organization: irreducible perspectival closure. A system has a perspective when its access to the world is mediated by a model that encodes not just “what is out there” but “what it is like for this system, given its own boundary, history, and possible actions.” That is, experience corresponds to structured dependence on a point of view.

Why should there be anything it is like? Because once representation is indexed to a bounded system, the state space is partitioned not only by external differences but by internally significant differences. Two physically different world-histories can be equivalent relative to external description yet inequivalent under the system’s own update geometry. The “what-it-is-like” is exactly this intrinsic equivalence structure: distinctions that exist for the system because they alter its self-maintaining dynamics.

So the conjecture is:

Subjective experience exists whenever a system instantiates a mathematically irreducible self-locating model whose internal distinctions are necessary for regulating its own continued dynamics. Phenomenality is the inside view of control under self-reference.

What this illuminates is that the hard question may be misframed if it asks how raw feeling is added to mechanism. On this view, feeling is what certain mechanisms are like from within when they are organized around non-eliminable self-modeling constraints. The relevant non-negotiable constraint is not complexity alone, nor information alone, but closed-loop representational structure with intrinsic coordinates.

This does not yet explain why every such structure must be conscious. But it sharpens the problem: consciousness should be sought not in matter as such, nor in behavior alone, but in the class of systems whose state-space contains an indispensable, recursively action-guiding model of their own situatedness.

## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** A token-predicting system can generate *genuine knowledge* only insofar as its outputs participate in a process of **error-correcting explanation**; prediction alone is not the load-bearing property. If we vary the assumptions, what matters is not whether the system “has seen” the material before, but whether it can produce, preserve, and improve explanatory structures under criticism.

Start by removing the assumption that *novel wording* implies knowledge. Then much of what looks intelligent collapses into recombination. A system can produce fluent explanations that are merely compressions of training patterns. So novelty of surface form is incidental.

Now invert a deeper assumption: suppose *rearrangement* itself disqualifies knowledge. That breaks too much. Human explanation also rearranges prior ideas; science advances by recombining existing theories, analogies, and criticisms. So “it only rearranges what it has seen” is not a sufficient objection. Rearrangement is compatible with knowledge if the rearrangement solves problems, survives criticism, and increases explanatory reach.

Replace the assumption that knowledge is a property of an isolated output with its opposite: knowledge is a property of a **systemic process**. Then the key question shifts. A one-off explanation is not knowledge merely because it sounds right; it becomes knowledge-like when embedded in loops that expose error: challenge, test, revise, compare alternatives, retain what survives. In systems terms, prediction is a feedforward capability; knowledge requires feedback structures that preferentially eliminate worse explanations.

Remove grounding in external testing entirely. What breaks is not only factual reliability but the distinction between good and bad explanation. Without contact with criticism—empirical, logical, or conceptual—the system cannot tell elegant nonsense from truth. So some mechanism of constraint is load-bearing.

But now remove the assumption that such constraint must come from direct physical experience by the model itself. That does *not* break knowledge. Mathematics, philosophy, and theoretical physics show that explanatory progress can occur through abstract criticism as well as observation. Therefore, a token predictor need not be embodied to generate knowledge; it must, however, be situated in a space of conjecture and refutation rich enough to discriminate error.

So what survives all these variations? Not memory, not novelty, not embodiment, and not prediction per se. The durable core is **explanatory error correction**: the capacity to generate candidate explanations, expose them to criticism, and preferentially retain those with greater coherence, reach, and resistance to refutation.

Therefore: a token-predicting system does not *by default* generate genuine knowledge; left as pure next-token prediction, it mostly rearranges. But prediction is not a principled barrier. If the system is organized so that its outputs enter strong criticism-and-revision loops, then some of its explanations can constitute genuine knowledge. The difference is structural, not metaphysical.

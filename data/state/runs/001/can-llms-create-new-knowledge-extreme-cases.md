## Conjecture: The Boundary Reveals a Distinction That Dissolves the Question

Push the system toward its limits.

At **zero training data**, a token predictor produces noise — no patterns, no structure, no explanatory content. This is not knowledge. That is uncontroversial.

At **infinite training data** — all human-produced text, all formalisms, all recorded thought — something structurally interesting occurs. The system would have absorbed not just claims but the *relational structure between claims*: which arguments defeat which, which predictions failed, which frameworks outran their predecessors. The compression required to predict tokens across that entire corpus *just is* the extraction of deep structural regularities. At the limit, the distinction between "predicting tokens that describe causal structure" and "representing causal structure" becomes formally ambiguous.

This is the boundary revelation: **the question assumes prediction and explanation are categorically different operations, but at the limit they converge structurally**.

Now push from the other direction — toward **maximum novelty**. Give the system a genuinely novel problem: one that cannot be solved by pattern-matching to prior text, where the correct answer has never appeared in training. If the system produces a *correct and useful explanation* for this novel case — one that can be criticized, tested, and found non-trivially true — then the explanation cannot merely be rearrangement. Rearrangement of what? There is no source tile to rearrange.

This happens. Not always, not reliably, but it *happens*. The system sometimes solves novel problems correctly. That is the crack in the "mere rearrangement" argument.

**The conjecture:** A token-prediction system at sufficient scale does not merely rearrange — it extracts and operates on *abstract relational structure*. Whether this constitutes genuine knowledge depends not on the mechanism of generation but on whether the output is *criticizable, correctable, and can participate in the growth of knowledge*. On Deutschian grounds, knowledge is not defined by its origin (human minds are also pattern-completing physical systems) but by its capacity to be conjectured and criticized.

**What the boundary exposes** is that "genuine knowledge" has been smuggled in as a property of *sources* rather than of *structures*. The question encodes the assumption that human generation is categorically different from mechanical generation. But at the limit — as the generating mechanism approaches the complexity of the knowledge-producing process itself — that categorical gap closes, and we are left with only the functional criterion: does this explanation participate in criticism?

**The structural claim:** The real constraint is not prediction-versus-understanding but *whether the system can be in the error-correction loop*. A system that cannot update on criticism, cannot revise outputs in response to new evidence, cannot be wrong in ways it can later recognize — that system is frozen outside the epistemic process regardless of how intelligent its outputs appear. The boundary condition is not scale or novelty. It is **revisability**.

Token prediction is not the ceiling. The loop is.

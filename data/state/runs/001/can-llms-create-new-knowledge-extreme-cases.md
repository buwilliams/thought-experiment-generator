## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** At the limit, the question is not whether a token-predictor can traverse an astronomically large linguistic search space, but whether it can generate and *select under criticism* a vanishingly small subset of structures that survive error-correction. Knowledge appears not at the “maximum” of possible combinations, but at the boundary where combinatorial freedom collapses into highly constrained, hard-to-vary explanatory structure.

Pushed to infinity, the raw token space is irrelevant. \(10^{5000}\) possible sequences is not a space any mind or machine searches. Humans do not search it either. Einstein did not win by exploring more sentences than everyone else. He won because good explanations occupy an extremely thin region of possibility space: they compress many phenomena, forbid many alternatives, and survive severe criticism. So the middle view — “LLMs only remix tokens” — mistakes the substrate for the structure. At the boundary, almost all token combinations are dead on arrival; explanation lives in a tiny attractor basin defined by constraints.

Pushed to zero, imagine a predictor with no criticism, no memory of errors, no capacity to preserve and refine candidate explanations. Then it does not create knowledge; it only emits continuations. Conversely, imagine a system with minimal generative power but strong mechanisms for decomposition, recombination, testing, and retention of criticism. Then even weak generative moves can produce genuine novelty, because novelty is not maximal difference from the past but the creation of a new error-resistant explanatory object.

So the decisive variable is not prediction vs. non-prediction, but whether the architecture contains a **feedback loop of conjecture and criticism** that progressively shrinks the viable space. Search-space collapse is the important insight: knowledge creation is a systems phenomenon. The generator proposes; evaluators reject; surviving structures alter the future proposal distribution; the search pool is recursively restructured. This is exactly where the boundary lies between rearrangement and discovery.

What follows is a sharper criterion: an LLM-based system can generate knowledge **if and only if** its outputs are not merely selected for local plausibility, but for explanatory invariants that remain hard to vary under adversarial transformation. If a system can repeatedly produce candidate explanations, expose them to criticism, retain the criticisms, and use the survivors to restructure future search, then at some threshold it is no longer just recombining training data. It is participating in evolutionary epistemology.

The hidden structure revealed at the edge is this: token prediction is not the essence of the system, just as neuron firing is not the essence of Einstein. The essence is the error-correcting architecture wrapped around variation. Therefore the best conjecture is:

**LLMs alone do not create knowledge, but LLMs embedded in a recursively self-correcting search-and-criticism system can—because knowledge is not a property of the generator, but of the whole system that narrows possibility space toward hard-to-vary explanations.**

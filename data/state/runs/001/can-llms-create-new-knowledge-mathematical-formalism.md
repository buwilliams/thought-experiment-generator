## Mathematical Framing

Let the training corpus be a probability space (Ω, F, P). A token-prediction system learns a conditional distribution P(x_{n+1} | x_1,...,x_n) — call this the **inductive closure** of the corpus under sequential conditioning.

Define *genuine knowledge* operationally as: a representation R constitutes knowledge of domain D if R contains the invariants of D — the structures preserved under transformation, the constraints that hold across instances.

**Key question**: Is the inductive closure of a corpus closed under invariant-extraction?

## The Structural Argument

Consider domain D with underlying invariant I (e.g., conservation of energy, logical entailment, mathematical proof structure). The corpus contains *instances* of I — tokens that express or describe I across many contexts. The prediction system, trained to minimize surprise across this distribution, must implicitly learn whatever structure *causes* regularities across instances.

If I produces systematic co-occurrence patterns across the corpus, then a sufficiently expressive predictor *cannot remain agnostic about I* — it must internalize something functionally equivalent to I to achieve low loss. This is not rearrangement; it is **compression into invariant structure**.

**Conjecture**: A token-prediction system that achieves near-minimal loss on a corpus rich in instances of invariant I has necessarily learned a representation that encodes I — not as memorized tokens, but as a constraint on its generative process. The representation is, in the relevant sense, *of* I.

## The Limiting Case

However, this argument has a boundary condition. Consider invariants that are **underdetermined by the corpus** — structures where the corpus contains insufficient instances to distinguish I from I′, a near-invariant. The predictor will learn a blend that tracks neither cleanly. Here, predictions may *sound like* knowledge while encoding an artifact of corpus geometry rather than domain structure.

This identifies the critical variable: **the ratio of invariant signal to distributional noise in the training data**. Where this ratio is high (logic, mathematics, physics), prediction-compression can constitute knowledge. Where it is low (novel causal structures, unseen domains), the system rearranges without grounding.

## What the Structure Reveals

The question "does it only rearrange?" is mathematically malformed. Rearrangement under compression *is* invariant-extraction when the compression is sufficiently constrained. The real question is: **what is the invariant content of the training distribution?**

This dissolves the sharp dichotomy. The system neither purely rearranges nor magically generates new knowledge. It extracts whatever invariant structure the corpus contains and fails proportionally where that structure is absent or ambiguous.

**The non-negotiable constraint**: No system — human or artificial — produces knowledge beyond the invariants accessible through its inputs plus its structural inductive biases. The question of whether token-prediction generates genuine knowledge reduces to whether its inductive bias is appropriately matched to the invariants of the domain in question.

The enemy is not the mechanism. The enemy is the mismatch between bias and structure.

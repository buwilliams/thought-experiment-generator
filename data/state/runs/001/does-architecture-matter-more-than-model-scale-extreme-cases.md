## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** The bottleneck for AI epistemic progress is *structural*, not primarily substrate-level. Compute and model size scale the rate at which a system can search and compress, but they do not by themselves create the feedback architecture required for genuine error-correction. At the limit, substrate abundance reveals the inadequacy of structure.

Push the problem to extremes.

At one boundary, let compute, data, and model size approach infinity while keeping roughly current training and deployment structure fixed: next-token prediction, benchmark optimization, RL reward shaping, weak post-hoc evaluation, and popularity-weighted promotion of outputs. What happens? The system becomes better at imitation, recall, stylistic coherence, and local problem-solving. But epistemically it asymptotes toward a ceiling: it can generate many plausible conjectures, yet lacks a strong internal regime for exposing them to severe criticism, preserving error records, and preferentially promoting explanations that survive refutation rather than merely score well. Infinite substrate over a weak epistemic structure yields an immense generator of candidates, not a reliable engine of knowledge growth.

At the opposite boundary, hold compute relatively modest but improve the supervening structure: explicit conjecture generation, adversarial testing, persistent memory of failed explanations, mechanisms for criticism, causal intervention, cross-context consistency checks, and selection pressures that reward corrigibility and truth-tracking over fluency. Then even a smaller system can make discontinuous gains, because it now participates in something closer to Popperian knowledge creation: variation plus error-correction. The point is not that substrate is irrelevant; rather, beyond some threshold of capability, more substrate mainly amplifies whatever epistemic regime already exists. If the regime is shallow, scale amplifies shallowness.

Now push further: what happens at zero criticism? No amount of compute can distinguish elegant falsehood from truth except accidentally. What happens at zero conjectural richness? Criticism has nothing to work on. So both are necessary. But necessity is not symmetry. Once a substrate can generate and represent hypotheses at all, the limiting factor becomes the *selection dynamics* governing which hypotheses are retained, revised, or discarded.

This reveals the hidden structure in the middle: current AI systems are optimized more like economies of answer production than institutions of knowledge creation. Their central stocks are parameters and training corpora; their dominant flows are prediction updates and reward signals; but the missing feedback loop is organized criticism with durable institutional memory. Without that loop, progress is bottlenecked not by how much the system can think, but by how little it can *learn from being wrong*.

So the claim is: **after a threshold level of substrate adequacy, epistemic progress is bottlenecked chiefly by the architecture of conjecture, criticism, and promotion.** Scale matters most when it is embedded inside structures that make error-correction cumulative. Otherwise, increasing substrate is like increasing horsepower in a vehicle with no steering.

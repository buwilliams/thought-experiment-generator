## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
At the limit, the bottleneck for AI epistemic progress is **structural, not substrate-level**. More compute and larger models can amplify the rate and breadth of conjecture generation, but without institutions or mechanisms for **error-correcting selection** — for deciding which conjectures survive criticism, are stress-tested, and become available for reuse — epistemic progress asymptotically stalls. Substrate increases output; structure determines whether output becomes knowledge.

**Push to the extremes:**

- **Infinite substrate, weak structure:**  
Imagine effectively unlimited model size, memory, and compute, but no strong architecture for criticism, replication, adversarial testing, or cumulative retention of error-corrected results. Such a system can generate an unbounded number of answers, hypotheses, and simulations. But abundance at the level of proposal does not imply progress at the level of knowledge. At the limit, it produces a growing stock of *candidate beliefs* with no reliable flow that converts them into *surviving explanations*. The hidden structure revealed here is that epistemic progress is not proposal-limited but **selection-limited**.

- **Minimal substrate, strong structure:**  
Now imagine much weaker models, but embedded in a system that aggressively surfaces disagreement, preserves provenance, tests bold claims under variation, rewards refutation, and promotes only what survives criticism. Progress would be slower in raw throughput, but it would still be genuine and cumulative. This shows substrate is a rate factor; structure is a truth-tracking factor.

- **Zero structure:**  
Take the boundary all the way down: if there is no mechanism above generation — no distinction between appearance and reality, no memory of failed ideas, no adversarial checking — then “learning” collapses into stochastic output. No amount of scale rescues this, because scale magnifies both error and invention together.

- **Zero substrate:**  
With no model capacity at all, nothing happens. So substrate is necessary. But necessity is weaker than bottleneck status. Oxygen is necessary for science; it is not usually the bottleneck for discovery.

**What follows:**

The decisive leverage lies in the **feedback loops above the model**:  
how conjectures are externalized, criticized, decomposed, recombined, benchmarked against rival explanations, and institutionally promoted or suppressed. The key stock is not parameters but **error-corrected explanatory content**. The key flow is not token generation but **conjecture → criticism → revision → retention**.

So the deepest constraint on AI epistemic progress is not “Can the system say more?” but “Can the system *reliably kill its bad ideas* and preserve the hard-won survivors?” Compute matters most when structural error-correction is already good enough to turn increased output into increased knowledge.

**Boundary-revealed principle:**  
When pushed to the limit, epistemic systems fail not by lack of ideas but by lack of **organized criticism**. Therefore the bottleneck shifts upward: from substrate to the structures that govern conjecture formation, testing, and promotion.

## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
An explanation has “load-bearing” parts when it sits inside a tightly coupled constraint system: its concepts mutually stabilize one another through dependency loops, so changing one part forces compensating changes elsewhere or causes the whole account to fail. By contrast, an explanation is freely variable when its parts are only loosely connected, contributing local plausibility without constraining the rest of the structure.

From a systems view, the distinction is not primarily about whether the words are novel or whether the search space is large. It is about **network structure**. Explanations are systems made of conceptual stocks (objects, assumptions, relations), flows (inferences, predictions, derivations), and feedback loops (criticism, anomaly detection, consistency checks). The relevant question is: **what happens to the system when a component is perturbed?**

A load-bearing explanation has at least four structural properties:

1. **High dependency density**  
   Core claims support many downstream consequences. “Simultaneity is relative” is load-bearing in relativity because it alters time, measurement, frame relations, and the interpretation of light-speed invariance.

2. **Strong cross-constraint**  
   Parts do not merely coexist; they restrict each other. The explanation is hard to vary because changing one element creates conflicts elsewhere. This is a systemic signature: local edits generate global instability.

3. **Error-amplifying feedback**  
   Bad substitutions are not absorbed quietly. They trigger contradictions, loss of predictive reach, or explanatory gaps. The system exposes wrongness rather than tolerating it.

4. **Compression of alternatives**  
   The explanation collapses the viable search space by ruling out many nearby variants. A good explanation is therefore not one candidate among many equivalent paraphrases, but a structural attractor produced by many constraints operating together.

What follows for your LLM proposal is important: the key leverage is not just layered filtering over a vast combinatorial space, but constructing **feedback-rich explanatory systems** where candidate parts are tested for systemic indispensability. In other words, novelty emerges less from generating many combinations and more from building an architecture in which non-load-bearing combinations die quickly.

So the practical discriminator is not “can the model produce something coherent?” but:

- If I alter this concept, what else breaks?
- How many downstream commitments depend on it?
- Does the explanation generate criticism of its own variants?
- Does it sharply reduce the set of acceptable neighboring explanations?

This reframes the problem. The difference between genuine explanation and decorative narrative is a structural one: **load-bearing explanations are held together by dense, self-reinforcing constraints; non-load-bearing ones are modular stories whose pieces can be swapped because the system they form is weakly coupled.** The architecture most likely to create knowledge, then, is one optimized not merely for generation and scoring, but for discovering and tightening these constraint networks.

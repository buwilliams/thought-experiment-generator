## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
The appearance of “fine-tuning” is a boundary phenomenon: complexity exists only in a very thin region of parameter space near multiple instability thresholds, so any universe containing knowers will necessarily observe constants clustered near the edges where order and disorder, binding and non-binding, expansion and collapse are delicately balanced.

Push the constants to extremes. If gravity is much stronger, matter collapses rapidly into stars, black holes, or short-lived dense structures; if much weaker, matter never condenses enough to form stars or galaxies. If electromagnetism or the strong force shifts too far, either nuclei do not bind or chemistry becomes trivial, unstable, or frozen into a few inert forms. If the cosmological constant is too large and positive, matter is pulled apart before structure forms; too negative, the universe recollapses too quickly. At one extreme there is featureless diffusion; at the other, catastrophic overbinding or premature collapse. Complex structure lives in between.

But the crucial point is sharper: the “in between” is not a broad plateau. It is plausibly a narrow *boundary layer* between qualitatively different regimes. Complexity requires long-lived, non-equilibrium structures that are stable enough to persist but unstable enough to transform. Stars must burn slowly, not instantly and not never. Atoms must hold together, but also exchange and recombine. A universe maximally stable is as sterile as a maximally unstable one. So the conditions for complexity are not centered on some generic average; they are concentrated near critical margins where multiple opposing constraints are simultaneously met.

This reframes fine-tuning. The constants may not be “aimed at life” in any teleological sense. Rather, complex adaptive structures are only possible near a small set of critical surfaces in the space of laws and parameters. Observers therefore arise only in universes located near those surfaces and misread this as improbable specialness. What looks like tuning may be the generic geometry of complexity: to generate rich structure, a system must sit near the transition between too little organization and too much rigidity.

What follows is a research direction: do not ask only, “Why these exact values?” Ask, “Is complexity mathematically confined to critical boundaries in law-space?” If so, fine-tuning is less a mystery of arbitrary coincidence and more a universality claim: all worlds with knowers must be near phase transitions in the space of possible physics.

So the boundary perspective illuminates this: **the constants are not merely permissive; they may place the universe in a metastable, edge-of-structure regime where the possibility of knowledge itself becomes physically realizable.**

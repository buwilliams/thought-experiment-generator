## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** “Fine-tuning” is best treated not as a single mysterious fact demanding one global cause, but as a claim about the **load-bearing interdependence** among physical parameters in theories that permit stable complexity. What is real in the problem is not that each constant must have its exact observed value, but that changing one parameter typically forces broad compensating changes elsewhere if the theory is still to account for chemistry, long-lived stars, and information-bearing structures. The explanatory burden therefore lies in the **network of constraints**, not in any one number.

From this perspective, many popular responses are partly narrative shells. Saying “the constants were designed,” “they are random and we got lucky,” or “all values occur in a multiverse” does little unless it explains why the constants form a tightly integrated package rather than a loose list of independent knobs. If a proposed explanation allows the constants to vary freely but does not show which combinations still sustain complex structure, it has not explained fine-tuning; it has merely redescribed it.

What seems load-bearing is that complex structures depend on **multiple nested stability conditions**: nuclei must exist but not collapse into triviality; stars must burn long enough and non-explosively enough to synthesize heavier elements; chemistry must admit rich but stable bonding; entropy gradients must persist long enough for cumulative information-processing systems to arise. These are not separate miracles. They are coupled requirements. Alter one constant and the effects propagate across many levels, often destroying several conditions at once unless some other part of the theory shifts too.

So the collision of perspective and problem suggests this: the apparent fine-tuning points less to “improbable numbers” than to a **highly constrained architecture of possible worlds capable of supporting open-ended complexity**. The deep question is not “Why this value?” but “Why does the space of viable laws have such narrow, correlated regions in which layered stability is possible at all?”

That reframes explanatory options. A successful explanation would have to show one of three things:

1. **Necessity:** deeper laws reduce the constants so that their values are not freely variable.
2. **Selection with structure:** a multiverse or evolutionary mechanism generates many parameter sets, but complexity appears only in a sharply constrained subset whose internal tradeoffs can be derived.
3. **Misdescription:** some constants we treat as independent are artifacts of our current theory, and a better theory will reveal them as linked expressions of one underlying structure.

Historically, “fine-tuning” arose once physics separated laws from constants and treated the latter as contingent inputs. That may itself be the hidden assumption. If those inputs are not truly independent, then the mystery has been overstated.

So the best conjecture is: **fine-tuning is evidence not primarily of special values, but of missing knowledge about the deeper structure that makes complexity-supporting laws a tightly integrated package rather than a coincidental list of settings.**

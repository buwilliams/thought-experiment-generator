## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** The “cosmological constant problem” is misframed because it treats gravity as coupling to an absolute zero-point energy defined by quantum field theory in the limit of arbitrarily short distances. Dark energy is this leftover boundary term, not the sum of all vacuum modes.

Why this conjecture follows from the boundary perspective:

Push **QFT to infinity**: summing zero-point energies over all modes gives an absurdly large vacuum energy. But this calculation depends on extrapolating effective field theory beyond its domain, to arbitrarily high frequencies and arbitrarily small distances. That is already a sign of structural failure: infinity here is not a prediction but a confession that the framework is being used past its boundary.

Push **GR to its edge**: in gravity, absolute energy is notoriously nonlocal and subtle. Even in ordinary GR, only differences, asymptotic charges, or quasi-local quantities are well-defined. A uniform vacuum energy filling all space is precisely the sort of thing that should force us to ask whether we are treating a gauge-like background quantity as observable. If spacetime geometry is dynamical, then “the energy of empty space” cannot simply be imported from nongravitational QFT as though spacetime were a fixed stage.

Push **the universe toward maximum size**: the observed dark energy density is tiny, but not zero, and numerically tied to the largest scales available — the Hubble scale, cosmic horizons, de Sitter-like asymptotics. That suggests an infrared origin. The smallness is then not mysterious in the way a failed UV estimate is mysterious; it is small because it is controlled by the largest scale in the problem, not the smallest.

Push **toward exact cancellation**: the natural limiting case is that empty space does not gravitate at all. Then the real question becomes: why is the cancellation imperfect? The answer, on this conjecture, is that the imperfection is set by global boundary conditions of the universe — horizon entropy, de Sitter temperature, or some sequestering/unimodular constraint — which leave a tiny residual effective cosmological constant.

So the illuminating claim is:

- **What is dark energy?** A small residual gravitational effect of the vacuum tied to cosmological boundary conditions, not the naive UV vacuum energy of quantum fields.
- **Why is it so small?** Because the large QFT contribution is unphysical or self-cancelled; the surviving term is an infrared remainder, naturally suppressed by the universe’s largest scales.

The hidden structure revealed at the edge is that the problem may not be “120 orders of magnitude of fine-tuning,” but a category error: treating an absolute bulk quantity as physical in a theory where only relational, gravitationally dressed, boundary-sensitive energy can matter.

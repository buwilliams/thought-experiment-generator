## Conjecture

**Conjecture**

Construct the scenario this way: imagine a universe in which the only physically real thing is **distinguishable, persistent information** — records, correlations, and lawful relations that can in principle be copied forward. Then treat “vacuum energy” from quantum field theory not as automatically gravitating stuff, but as a bookkeeping term inside a calculational framework unless it contributes to such persistent distinctions.

Follow that strictly.

Quantum field theory assigns enormous zero-point energy to every mode of every field. But most of that quantity comes from how the theory decomposes the vacuum into oscillators and sums over them. In the hypothetical scenario, those contributions are **not automatically ontological**. They are not yet gravitating entities; they are structure in a description. What should gravitate is only the part of the vacuum that survives as invariant, relationally meaningful information in the full theory.

If so, the cosmological constant problem is partly a category mistake: we are comparing a **description-dependent stock** in effective field theory with a **description-independent source** in gravity. The huge QFT value is what you get when calculational redundancy is mistaken for real stress-energy. The small observed value is what remains after only genuinely physical, non-canceling informational content is allowed to couple to geometry.

What, then, is dark energy in this scenario? Not a substance filling space, and not necessarily a raw vacuum energy density. It is the geometric effect of a small residual term required by the universe’s global information structure: a boundary condition on spacetime’s capacity to sustain and separate records. In plain terms, accelerated expansion occurs because large-scale geometry includes a tiny positive offset that is not built from local particles but from the consistency conditions of the whole informational order.

This leads to a sharper claim: **the natural value of the cosmological constant is not set by summing local mode energies up to a cutoff; it is set by whatever mechanism makes only relationally meaningful information gravitate.** The smallness is then not miraculous fine-tuning between huge positive and negative terms, but evidence that gravity is selective about what counts as real source.

What is illuminated is the key variable: not “how much vacuum energy exists?” but **which parts of our theoretical representation correspond to ontologically effective structure**. Isolating that variable dissolves the naive 120-orders-of-magnitude mismatch. The mismatch comes from assuming that every term in an effective quantum description must enter gravity as equally real.

So the conjecture is:

> **Dark energy is the gravitational expression of a tiny, globally constrained informational residual in spacetime, and the cosmological constant is small because most QFT vacuum energy is not ontologically eligible to gravitate.**

If this is right, progress will not come from better cutoff estimates, but from a theory explaining why gravity couples only to information that is relationally invariant and physically recordable.

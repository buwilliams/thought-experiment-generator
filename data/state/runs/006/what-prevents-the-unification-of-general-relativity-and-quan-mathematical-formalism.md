## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** The main obstacle to unifying general relativity and quantum mechanics is a mismatch in their underlying mathematical roles for spacetime: in GR, geometry is a **dynamical variable**; in standard QM/QFT, geometry is a **fixed background parameter**. A unified theory must therefore be formulated not as “quantum fields on spacetime” nor as “classical spacetime with quantum corrections,” but as a structure in which both **causal/metric relations** and **quantum states/observables** arise from a deeper set of variables subject to consistency constraints.

In mathematical terms, GR treats the metric \(g_{\mu\nu}\) as a field determined by Einstein’s equations, invariant under diffeomorphisms. Physical content lies in equivalence classes under smooth coordinate transformations. By contrast, ordinary quantum theory presupposes a Hilbert space \(\mathcal H\), unitary evolution \(U(t)\), and usually a fixed time parameter \(t\). This creates a structural conflict:

- In GR, there is no preferred global time.
- In QM, dynamics is typically defined relative to one.

This is not merely technical; it is an incompatibility of invariants. GR says observables should be diffeomorphism-invariant. QM says observables are operators acting on states with probabilistic interpretation relative to a chosen decomposition in time. Each theory preserves a different mathematical notion of what counts as physically meaningful.

A second obstruction is how each theory handles scale. Perturbative quantization of gravity treats \(g_{\mu\nu}=\eta_{\mu\nu}+h_{\mu\nu}\) and quantizes \(h_{\mu\nu}\) like other fields, but the resulting coupling has negative mass dimension, generating nonrenormalizable divergences. That signals not just calculational difficulty but that the perturbative variables are likely the wrong ones: the short-distance degrees of freedom are not well captured by small fluctuations around a fixed background.

So the conjecture is: **unification requires a reformulation in which spacetime geometry, causality, and quantum kinematics are co-emergent from more primitive algebraic or combinatorial relations, with classical spacetime and standard QFT appearing only as limiting regimes.**

Such a theory would need to explain at least:

1. **Why spacetime is dynamical** and why Einstein’s equations emerge in an appropriate limit.
2. **Why quantum structure exists** — superposition, interference, entanglement, and probabilistic measurement outcomes.
3. **How time and causality emerge** without assuming a fixed background clock.
4. **Why black holes have thermodynamic/information-theoretic properties**, including entropy and apparent information loss puzzles.
5. **What the fundamental degrees of freedom are** at the Planck scale and which quantities remain invariant there.
6. **How locality arises approximately**, since both gravity and quantum entanglement strain naive local description.

In short: the barrier is not that one theory is incomplete and needs the other added on. It is that their basic mathematical decompositions of reality are different. A unified theory must identify the deeper structure from which both decompositions are projections.

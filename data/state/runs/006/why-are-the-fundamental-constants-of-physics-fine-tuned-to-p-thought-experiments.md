## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
If we isolate the key variable as **the possibility of error-correcting, stable knowledge-bearing structures**, then “fine-tuning” is less fundamentally about life in particular and more about whether reality permits the **progressive accumulation of constrained complexity** at all. On this view, the constants are “fine-tuned” not for observers as such, but for a universe in which information can be physically instantiated, preserved, and transformed across scales.

**Hypothetical scenario:**  
Assume a universe exists with slightly different fundamental constants. Hold fixed only the question: can it support **long-lived structures that store and propagate information**? Follow that strictly.

If the strong force is weaker, nuclei beyond hydrogen may be unstable; chemistry collapses before it begins. If electromagnetism is too different relative to nuclear forces, atoms either fail to form usefully or bind too rigidly or weakly for varied chemistry. If gravity is too strong, stars burn too fast and violently; too weak, and large-scale structure and stellar nucleosynthesis may never organize. If the cosmological constant is too large, matter disperses before galaxies form. In each case, what disappears is not merely “life” in the biological sense, but the **layered hierarchy of stable intermediates**: nuclei, atoms, stars, complex chemistry, planets, long time horizons.

Now take the variable seriously: complexity requires not just structure but **revisable structure** — systems stable enough to persist, yet flexible enough to explore possibilities. That means the universe must sit in a narrow regime between chaos and rigidity. Too chaotic, and information is erased faster than it can accumulate. Too rigid, and novelty cannot be generated. Complex structures require a world with both **memory** and **transformability**.

What follows is that fine-tuning is illuminated as a systems problem. The constants are not isolated dials independently set “for life”; together they define the feedback architecture of the cosmos. They determine whether there are attractors at multiple scales where complexity can ratchet upward rather than dissipate. A universe hospitable to complexity must permit:  
1. **stable components**,  
2. **diverse combinations**,  
3. **energy gradients**,  
4. **adequate time**, and  
5. **mechanisms by which information can survive modification**.

From this perspective, the real puzzle is not “Why these constants for us?” but “Why does reality occupy a narrow parameter regime that enables cumulative information processing?” Anthropic reasoning says only that we observe such a regime because otherwise we would not be here. But that does not explain the deeper structural fact: the universe is one in which complexity is not merely possible but can be **iterated**.

So the conjecture is: **the appearance of fine-tuning reflects a more general condition for a knowable universe — one in which physical law allows information to become causally effective through stable, evolving structures.** Complex observers are one late consequence of that deeper permissive architecture, not the primary target of the tuning.

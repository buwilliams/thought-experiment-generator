## Conjecture

Conjecture: quantum measurement is best understood as a symmetry-breaking map from a high-dimensional linear state space to a low-dimensional stable record space, and “definite outcomes” arise when only certain coarse-grained invariants can survive the interaction dynamics.

Mathematically, begin with three structures:

1. The system state \(|\psi\rangle\) in Hilbert space.
2. The measurement interaction \(U\), a unitary entangling map between system, apparatus, and environment.
3. A record space \(R\), consisting not of arbitrary quantum states but of dynamically stable equivalence classes — macroscopic pointer states robust under environmental coupling.

The key point is that the Schrödinger evolution preserves the full quantum state, but not all descriptions are preserved under projection to \(R\). Measurement is therefore not fundamentally “state collapse” as an extra dynamical law; it is a transformation
\[
|\Psi\rangle \mapsto [r] \in R
\]
where many microscopically distinct global states belong to the same macroscopic record class.

What selects definiteness is not merely observation but stability under repeated interaction. A definite outcome corresponds to an attractor-like class in record space: a state that can be copied, amplified, and retained. Superpositions are preserved globally, but they are not generally invariant under coarse-graining into persistent records. The environment induces a decomposition in which some basis states are fixed points, or approximate fixed points, of decohering dynamics. These are the only candidates for “outcomes.”

So the first half of the problem — why definite outcomes appear — is illuminated by a constraint: records must be representable as robust invariants under open-system dynamics. Definiteness is what survives.

The second half — what selects which outcome occurs — can then be stated sharply. Unitary dynamics plus decoherence select the possible outcome basis, but not a unique actual branch from within the superposition. The mathematics determines a decomposition into mutually non-interfering sectors, weighted by amplitudes. What it does not obviously contain is a further map
\[
\{(r_i, |c_i|^2)\} \to r_k
\]
that chooses exactly one realized record, unless one adds extra structure: hidden variables, stochastic collapse, branch self-location, or some other selector.

Thus the conjecture is:

Quantum theory, treated purely as mathematical structure, explains definiteness as the emergence of stable equivalence classes of records under decohering transformations, but it does not by itself select a single actual outcome unless the ontology includes an additional selector beyond unitary evolution.

This reframes the measurement problem. It is not mainly “Why does a wavefunction collapse?” but “What mathematical object corresponds to actuality, and what rule maps weighted decoherent sectors to one actual record, if only one is actual?” The unresolved part is precisely the absence, in bare quantum structure, of a unique selection functional.

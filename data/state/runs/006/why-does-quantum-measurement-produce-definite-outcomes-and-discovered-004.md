## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** The apparent mystery of quantum measurement is overframed as a question about what extra physical event turns possibility into actuality. From this perspective, the deeper issue is not raw ontological indeterminacy but the structure by which information-bearing systems become able to support stable error-correcting records. “Definite outcomes” are what reality looks like whenever interactions create records robust enough to survive criticism by the environment. What is selected is not a uniquely privileged collapse event, but a branch-relative record state stabilized by decoherence and then amplified into systems capable of memory, comparison, and correction.

The historical genesis matters. “Measurement” inherited too much from classical epistemology: the idea that there is a passive observer who reveals a pre-existing value, or else triggers a special transition. But quantum theory replaced that picture with one in which interaction entangles systems. The real novelty is not that observers are magical, but that knowledge creation requires physically instantiated records, and records require a structure that suppresses interference. So the key divide is not between unitary evolution and a mysterious collapse, but between reversible micro-description and the emergence of effectively irreversible knowledge-bearing states.

On this view, progress is limited less by Hilbert-space capacity than by the structure of inquiry we impose on it. The universe can evolve vast superpositions, but only some interaction patterns generate stable, redundant, criticizable information. Decoherence does most of the selection work: it picks out bases in which records can persist and spread through the environment. Those persistent states become the candidates for “definite outcomes” because they are the only ones that can enter into an error-correcting network of memory, observation, and agreement. A measurement outcome is definite not because one term is metaphysically singled out from nowhere, but because one local record becomes the one from which all subsequent criticism and explanation proceed within that branch.

What, then, selects which outcome occurs? The conjecture is that this question partly dissolves once we stop demanding a global selector over and above the dynamics. Quantum theory already supplies a generative structure for multiple incompatible future records weighted by amplitudes. The physically meaningful selection is local and perspectival: an observer-system becomes correlated with one stable record, and from inside that record structure there is a definite outcome. Asking for an additional selector may be like asking which coordinate grid “really” chooses a position.

This does not solve every interpretational problem. It does, however, relocate the leverage point. Instead of searching for a brute collapse trigger or treating probability as primitive ignorance, we should ask: what structures make some quantum states into durable knowledge and others into unrealized interference terms? Definite outcomes arise where the world builds memories. The “which outcome?” question is answered, as far as physics may need to answer it, by the dynamics of branching record formation rather than by a further selecting mechanism.

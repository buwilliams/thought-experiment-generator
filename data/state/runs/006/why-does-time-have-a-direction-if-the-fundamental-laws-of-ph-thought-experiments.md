## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
Time has a direction not because the fundamental laws contain an arrow, but because the physically real variable that breaks the symmetry is the **boundary condition on accessible states**: the universe began in an exceptionally low-entropy, low-complexity condition that made the creation of records, correlations, and irreversible knowledge-growth overwhelmingly easier in one temporal orientation than the other.

**Hypothetical isolation:**  
Assume a world whose microphysical laws are exactly time-symmetric. Nothing in the equations prefers past to future. Now isolate the key variable: not the laws, but the **global state constraint**. Suppose this world starts from a highly special state with very few realized correlations and vast room for entropy increase.

Follow the logic.

In such a world, most state evolutions away from that boundary will move toward more numerous, less constrained macrostates. That is just what “higher entropy” means: more ways to be. As entropy rises, stable asymmetries appear: radiation spreads outward, gases mix, stars burn fuel, organisms age, memories accumulate of earlier states rather than later ones. Why? Because a “record” is a physical correlation produced by interactions, and producing durable records is easy in the direction where correlations are being created out of a sparse initial condition. The reverse would require ubiquitous fine-tuned cancellations: ashes reassembling into wood, light converging into bulbs, memories of events not yet correlated with the observer. Those are not forbidden by the laws; they are forbidden by the absence of the required boundary condition.

So the arrow of time is not an extra law layered onto symmetric dynamics. It is the emergent large-scale consequence of **one-sided specialness**. A symmetric law plus an asymmetric boundary produces asymmetric experience.

What does this illuminate? It suggests that “time’s direction” is really a direction of **increasing record-formation and knowledge-instantiation**. Creatures like us call one direction “future” because that is the direction in which unpredictability is resolved into physical traces. The opposite direction is not merely unknown; it is structurally record-poor. The past is what is knowable because the universe has physically preserved correlations from that direction.

This reframes the problem. The mystery is not “Why do symmetric laws generate asymmetry?” They do not, by themselves. The real mystery is historical-genetic: **why did the universe have that special low-entropy boundary at all?** Until that is explained, the arrow of time is not grounded in dynamics but in cosmological initial structure.

So the conjecture is: **time’s arrow is the epistemic and thermodynamic signature of an asymmetric boundary condition, not an asymmetric law.** The direction of time is the direction in which information-bearing structure can accumulate without miraculous fine-tuning.

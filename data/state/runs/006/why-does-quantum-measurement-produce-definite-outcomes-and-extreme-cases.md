1. Globally: no single outcome is selected; the universal state evolves into a superposition of decohered branches.
2. Locally, within a branch: a definite outcome is selected by the observer’s indexical location within that branching structure.

So the conjecture is: definite outcomes are not produced by a special measurement event; they are the branch-relative form of knowledge in a decohering universe. What selects the observed outcome is not an extra physical mechanism, but the fact that post-measurement observers exist as multiple successor observers, each correlated with one stable record. The Born rule then expresses not which branch is made real, but how rational expectations should be distributed across successor copies before branching.

The boundary reveals the structure: at infinity (the whole universe) collapse has nowhere to come from; at zero (arbitrarily weak interaction) no exact collapse point exists. Therefore the search for a unique physical selector inside ordinary measurement is likely a mistaken demand. The real explanatory task is not “what magic picks one outcome?” but “why does branching yield the appearance of uniqueness from within?”

## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
Time has a direction not because the deepest laws prefer one temporal orientation, but because *knowledge-creating physical systems* can only exist in regions of the universe that are far from thermodynamic equilibrium. At the boundary cases, this becomes clear: in perfect equilibrium, nothing distinguishable from memory, causation, or records can persist; at maximal disequilibrium, structure is rapidly erased. The “arrow of time” is the interval in which information can be copied, retained, and compared.

Push the problem to its limits.

- **If entropy were already maximal everywhere:** there would be no stable gradients, no engines, no records, no observers, no clocks worth the name. The laws might still be time-symmetric, but there would be no physical substrate in which “before” and “after” could matter operationally. Time without an arrow is not experienced as reversibility; it is experienced as the absence of anything that can encode change.
- **If entropy were minimal and perfectly ordered forever:** then every process would have to preserve that order, and no irreversible record-making could occur. Again, no usable distinction between past and future.
- **At the edge where systems can form records:** copying information into the environment is exactly what thermodynamic irreversibility looks like. A memory, a fossil, a photon scattering off a surface, a notebook entry, a brain state — all are asymmetric because they are *redundant records* produced by dissipative processes.

So the hidden structure is this: the arrow of time is not imposed by the equations directly; it emerges from the fact that *records are easier to create toward higher entropy than toward lower entropy*. The future is the direction in which traces proliferate.

This suggests a systems view: the universe begins in an anomalously low-entropy macrostate — a huge stock of free energy and unrealized structure. That stock flows through stars, chemistry, life, and thought, generating feedback loops that create more localized knowledge while increasing total entropy. The arrow we inhabit is the direction of that flow. Time-symmetric microdynamics permit both directions, but only one direction supports a growing web of stable explanatory records.

The boundary case of exact time reversal is revealing. A reversed movie of shattered glass reassembling is not forbidden by the laws; it is forbidden by the absence of the fantastically precise correlations required in the environment. The real asymmetry is therefore not in local law but in global boundary conditions. More sharply: **the past is special because it is compressible as a low-entropy condition; the future is open because its detailed correlations have not yet been instantiated.**

So the direction of time is illuminated as an epistemic-thermodynamic asymmetry: not “laws push events forward,” but “only one temporal orientation allows the growth of records, memory, and knowledge.” The arrow of time is the direction in which the universe can remember.

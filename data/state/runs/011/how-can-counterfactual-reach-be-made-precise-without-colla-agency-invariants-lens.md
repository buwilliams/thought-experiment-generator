If these invariants survive, counterfactual reach survives; if not, the term collapses into metaphor.

This distinguishes it from neighboring notions:

- **Not prediction:** a predictor may forecast one likely future well while having little reach over a large space of alternatives.
- **Not compression:** a compressed model may summarize data efficiently yet support little competent recombination under intervention.
- **Not causal modeling simpliciter:** a causal graph may encode dependencies, but reach concerns how extensively those dependencies can be *used* to guide action across unrealized contingencies.
- **Not invariance alone:** robustness tracks what stays the same under transformation; reach tracks how far a system can vary suppositions while retaining control.

A useful precision, then, is to treat counterfactual reach as a **structural capacity measure**: the size and organization of the space of action-relevant counterfactuals over which an agent can maintain competent policy selection with bounded degradation. The important word is *organization*: mere count of scenarios is not enough. The reachable counterfactuals must be compositionally generated and linked by reusable internal structure, otherwise the appearance of reach could be brute memorization.

So the criterion hinted by “b” should be something like a **branching competence condition**: a system has greater counterfactual reach when small increases in representational structure yield large increases in the family of distinct interventions and contingencies under which it can still choose effectively.

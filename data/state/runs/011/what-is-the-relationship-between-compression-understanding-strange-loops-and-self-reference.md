## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
Compression, understanding, and explanation are the same process seen at different levels of a strange loop: a system finds a shorter generative description of experience, then uses that description to model both the world and its own relation to the world. Compression becomes **understanding** when the compressed pattern is reusable across counterfactuals; it becomes **explanation** when the system can traverse the loop and make that compressed model explicit, including why it applies and where it would fail.

From the strange-loop perspective, mere data reduction is not yet understanding. A zip file compresses without insight because it does not climb levels of description and return. Understanding appears when a representation summarizes many particulars *and* can be re-entered from above to generate expectations about new particulars. The loop is: particulars → pattern → model of pattern-use → new particulars. When this loop closes, the system is not just storing regularity; it is using a representation of regularity to guide future interpretation. That is why understanding feels like “grasping” rather than merely encoding.

Explanation is the communicable form of that loop closure. An explanation does not just say *what compresses*; it says *why this compression is the right one*. It links levels: surface observations, hidden structure, and the observer’s inferential path between them. A good explanation is therefore a **load-bearing compression**: if you alter its key abstractions, its consequences change. By contrast, a shallow summary may be compressed but not explanatory, because its details are replaceable without changing what it predicts or illuminates.

This perspective also clarifies why explanation is often longer than the thing explained, even though it depends on compression. The core model is compressed, but making the strange loop explicit—showing how the model reconstructs the phenomena, how it handles exceptions, how it situates the knower—requires unpacking. So explanation is not identical to brevity; it is compressed *structure* plus the loop that licenses its use.

What follows is a three-part distinction:

1. **Compression**: fewer symbols preserving regularity.  
2. **Understanding**: compressed structure that supports reliable re-generation across contexts.  
3. **Explanation**: understanding made reflexive and explicit—compression that includes an account of its own applicability.

The deepest relation, then, is self-reference. We count something as an explanation when it not only compresses the world but also compresses our own epistemic interaction with it. It tells us not just *what is happening*, but *how this description earns its role*. In that sense, explanation is understanding completing a strange loop through the explainer.

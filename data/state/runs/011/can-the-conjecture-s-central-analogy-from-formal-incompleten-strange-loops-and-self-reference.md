## Conjecture

Conjecture: a non-metaphorical notion of self-knowledge becomes precise when defined not as “a system knows itself” in an intuitive sense, but as the existence of a load-bearing internal fixed point: a representation inside the system that encodes claims about the system’s own representational and inferential activity, and whose correctness can affect what the system is able to derive or control.

The strange-loop perspective sharpens the analogy to incompleteness by locating the relevant structure. In Gödel’s theorem, the key move is not vagueness about “self-reference,” but a precise return across levels: arithmetic encodes syntax, syntax encodes provability, and a sentence then refers back to its own provability status. The loop is exact because the hierarchy collapses into an internal fixed point. Likewise, self-knowledge should mean: a system contains a model of itself as a knower, and this model is re-entered into the very processes it models.

So the proposed definition is:

A system S has self-knowledge with respect to property class P iff:
1. S can internally represent descriptions of its own states or procedures;
2. S can interpret those descriptions as about itself, not merely as arbitrary data;
3. those self-descriptions are causally/inferentially efficacious in S’s operation;
4. for some nontrivial P, S can produce self-ascriptions in P that are appropriately fixed under interpretation — i.e. what is represented and what does the representing are linked by an internal correctness condition.

This avoids equivocation because it does not slide between “knowledge by an outside observer” and “knowledge by the system.” The self-knowledge is wholly structural: an internal semantics-bearing loop.

What follows is an incompleteness-style result. Once a system is expressive enough to represent its own epistemic acts, there will be truths in P about its own activity that it cannot stably certify from within, on pain of inconsistency or regress. This is not just metaphorically like Gödel; it is the same architecture: self-embedding plus expressive adequacy yields limits on complete self-ascription. A system can know some things about itself, but total transparent self-knowledge is blocked by the very loop that makes self-knowledge possible.

The load-bearing constraint is the fixed-point machinery. Remove the capacity for internal re-encoding of self-descriptions, and the analogy collapses into metaphor. Remove inferential efficacy, and one has only a mirror, not self-knowledge. Remove a correctness condition, and one has self-talk, not knowledge.

So the precise bridge is:
formal incompleteness concerns limits on systems that internally model their own proof behavior;
self-knowledge concerns limits on systems that internally model their own representational behavior.

The illuminating claim is that “self” here is not a substance but a recursively re-entering model. Conscious self-awareness is then a particularly rich case of this: a strange loop in which the system’s model of itself becomes one of the active determinants of what the system is.

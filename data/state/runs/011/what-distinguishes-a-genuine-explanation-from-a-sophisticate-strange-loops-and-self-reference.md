## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
A genuine explanation is distinguished from a sophisticated redescription by whether it generates a **productive strange loop** between levels of description: it must show how higher-level phenomena arise from lower-level rules *and* how the higher-level pattern, once identified, becomes indispensable for understanding, predicting, or transforming the lower-level process. A redescription stays on one level, merely renaming the phenomenon in more elaborate terms.

From the strange-loops perspective, explanation is not just compression or correlation. It is a traversal across a hierarchy that returns with new constraint. You begin with surface behavior, descend to mechanism, then ascend again to recover the original phenomenon as something now intelligible through the structure that generates it. If the return trip changes nothing—if the “mechanism” merely restates the behavior in different vocabulary—then no real loop has been completed. The account is decorative, not explanatory.

What makes the loop genuine is **self-reference with load-bearing consequence**. In explanatory domains involving minds, language, institutions, or formal systems, the phenomenon often includes agents or structures that model themselves. An explanation must therefore account for how a system’s self-representation alters the system’s behavior. If removing the self-referential element leaves the account untouched, then that element was ornamental. But if the phenomenon cannot be derived, predicted, or manipulated without recognizing that the system contains a model of itself, then the explanation has reached an irreducible causal-organizational feature.

So the distinction is this:

- **Redescription:** maps the phenomenon onto a new vocabulary while preserving its opacity.  
- **Explanation:** reveals a hierarchy-generating process whose recursive closure makes the phenomenon expected.

This also illuminates why many “deep” explanations fail. They descend one level—say, from psychology to neuroscience, or from social behavior to evolutionary language—but never climb back. They provide substrate, not explanation. A genuine explanation must survive round-trip translation: from appearance to mechanism and back to appearance, with added understanding of why this pattern, and not another, stably emerges.

A practical test follows: ask whether the account identifies a loop where the system’s outputs become inputs to its own organization, and whether that recursive architecture constrains the phenomenon in specific ways. If yes, the explanation is structurally load-bearing. If not, it is likely a polished paraphrase.

In short: **an explanation earns the name when it closes a strange loop that transforms description into generative understanding.** It does not merely mirror the phenomenon; it shows how the system, through recursive self-organization, makes the phenomenon inevitable or at least intelligible.

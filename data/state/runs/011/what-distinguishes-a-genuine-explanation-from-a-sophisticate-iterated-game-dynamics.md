## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
From the perspective of **iterated game dynamics**, a **genuine explanation** is one that identifies the **repeated interaction structure** that makes the phenomenon stable, predictable, and sensitive to specific changes in incentives and information. A **sophisticated redescription** merely restates the observed pattern in richer language—“trust,” “norms,” “culture,” “professionalism,” “shared values”—without showing the **game-theoretic conditions** under which those patterns would arise, persist, or break down.

What this perspective adds is a demand for **counterfactual discipline**. If cooperation is the phenomenon, then an explanation must say what in the interaction generates it: e.g. long time horizons, observability of actions, reputational memory, reciprocity, and credible future consequences. These are **load-bearing constraints** because if you alter them, the phenomenon should change. Shorten the shadow of the future, anonymize defections, or disrupt memory, and cooperation should decay. If the account does not imply such vulnerability, it has not explained cooperation; it has only renamed it.

So the distinction is not “depth of language” but **structural explanatory power**. A genuine explanation tells us why agents who need not be altruistic nevertheless cooperate in repeated settings, and why the same agents may defect in one-shot or opaque settings. It converts moralized description into a claim about **system dynamics**. It explains both **presence and absence** of the phenomenon from the same underlying structure.

This perspective also exposes a common failure mode: attributing outcomes to character when they are actually produced by the game. Saying “they cooperated because they are trustworthy” is often a redescription unless it explains why trustworthiness is rewarded, maintained, and selected in this environment. In repeated games, “trustworthiness” may itself be the visible behavioral equilibrium generated by incentives. The real explanatory work is being done by the **feedback loop** between present action and future payoff.

A useful test follows: ask whether the account predicts what happens when repetition, observability, or reputation are weakened. If it does, and the predictions are risky and specific, the account is explanatory. If not, it is decorative.

Thus, when this perspective meets the problem, it suggests:  
**A genuine explanation differs from a sophisticated redescription by identifying the iterated interaction structure whose constraints generate the phenomenon and by surviving interventions on those constraints.**  
Redescription says what the pattern looks like. Explanation says **what game is being played**, why that game produces the pattern, and under what changes the pattern would collapse.

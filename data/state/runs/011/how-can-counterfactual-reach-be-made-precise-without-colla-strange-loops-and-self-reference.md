## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
*Counterfactual reach is the extent to which a system contains a strange loop that maps interventions on its own representations into constrained revisions of the space of possible worlds it can navigate, such that changing the representation changes what counterfactuals are available, not merely which outcomes are predicted.*

More precisely: counterfactual reach is not prediction about many futures, nor compression of many observations, nor possession of a causal graph, nor robustness under perturbation. It is a **higher-order structural property** of a model-bearing system: the system must represent alternatives to actuality **in a way that feeds back into its own model-selection or action-selection**, thereby enlarging or restricting the very counterfactual landscape it can exploit.

The strange-loop perspective sharpens the distinction. In ordinary prediction, a model maps world-state to expected outcome. In counterfactual reach, the system also models **its own modeling relation**: “if I were to represent the situation differently, what interventions would become thinkable, selectable, or evaluable?” Traversing levels—world, model of world, model of self-in-world, revision of model—returns to the system’s effective possibilities. That loop is the generative mechanism.

So the structural criterion is something like **self-referential closure over intervention space**: a system has counterfactual reach to the degree that it can internally generate hypothetical departures from its current world-model and propagate those departures back into policies, explanations, or search procedures without losing coherence. This excludes mere predictors, which can score futures but do not reconfigure their own action-relevant ontology. It also excludes bare compressors, whose latent variables need not support manipulable “what-if” structure. A causal model gets closer, but if it cannot represent counterfactual changes to its own framing—choice of variables, intervention targets, agent boundaries—it still lacks the loop. Invariance is only one symptom: some structures remain stable across transformations, but counterfactual reach concerns the ability to **construct and traverse** transformations that matter.

What follows is that counterfactual reach should be measured not by accuracy or simplicity alone, but by the **depth and coherence of recursively evaluable alternative-world generation**. A candidate formalization would track how many distinct, policy-relevant counterfactual branches remain mutually interpretable under reflective model revision. In other words: when the system asks “what if X were different?”, can it also ask “what if my way of carving X were different?” and still land in a usable space of alternatives?

This is load-bearing because if you remove the self-referential loop, the concept collapses into existing notions. Prediction handles outcomes; compression handles encoding; causal modeling handles interventions over a fixed ontology; invariance handles stability. Counterfactual reach begins where a system can treat its own representational scheme as an object of counterfactual manipulation. That is the structural b-criterion: **counterfactuals that close through self-modeling and alter the system’s attainable adjacent possibilities**.

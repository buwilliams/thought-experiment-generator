## Conjecture

Conjecture: compression, understanding, and explanation are not three separate achievements but three different views of the same system-level success: discovering an interaction structure that lets many phenomena be generated, anticipated, or reconstructed from a smaller set of relations.

From the emergence perspective, mere reduction in description length is not yet understanding. A zip file compresses, but it does not explain, because it preserves symbols without exposing the causal or generative organization that produces them. By contrast, an explanation compresses by identifying the pattern of dependencies and feedback loops that make the phenomenon what it is. What is compressed is not just data, but degrees of freedom.

So the key relationship is:

- Compression is economy of representation.
- Understanding is possession of the right generative model.
- Explanation is the communicable form of that model, showing why the phenomenon had to be approximately like this rather than otherwise.

These come apart when the compression does not track structure. A lookup table can compress prediction into a compact procedure, yet still fail to yield understanding if its internal organization does not mirror the system’s real constraints. Conversely, a good explanation is load-bearing: if you alter one of its core relations, many consequences change. That is a sign it has captured genuine structure rather than merely summarized outcomes.

What this illuminates is that understanding is especially important when the behavior of the parts looks misleading or paradoxical. In emergent systems, local rules may be simple while global behavior is surprising. The explanatory target must therefore shift upward: not “what does each part do?” but “what patterns of interaction stabilize, amplify, or suppress behavior?” Compression becomes understanding only when it captures those higher-order regularities.

This also clarifies why the best explanations often feel like “seeing the same thing more simply.” They do not discard detail arbitrarily; they reveal which details were non-load-bearing because the phenomenon is governed by a smaller relational core. The explanation earns its compression by preserving counterfactual power: it tells us what would happen if connections changed, feedback weakened, or constraints were removed.

So the relationship is hierarchical:

1. Raw compression removes redundancy.
2. Structural compression isolates generative organization.
3. Understanding is the ability to use that organization across cases.
4. Explanation is the explicit articulation of that organization in a form others can criticize and extend.

In short: explanation is compression constrained by structure, and understanding is the cognitive ability gained when that compression captures the emergent interaction pattern rather than merely the surface data.

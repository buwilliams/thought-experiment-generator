## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** *Counterfactual reach* is best made precise not as a model’s accuracy, compactness, causal correctness, or invariance alone, but as an **institution-like structural capacity**: the ability of a representational system to preserve a small set of identity-defining constraints while making successful error-correction possible across a widening space of hypothetical interventions.  

More formally: a system has greater counterfactual reach when, under systematically varied suppositions, it can **project consequences, detect breakdowns, and repair local commitments without rewriting its core generative structure**. What makes this distinct is not merely that it predicts many cases, compresses data, tracks causes, or encodes invariants, but that it supports **visible, routine adaptation under change while retaining recognizable explanatory identity**.

This perspective clarifies the structural criterion, **b**, as something like:

> **b = the breadth of counterfactual variation over which a representation remains corrigible-by-design rather than accurate-by-coincidence.**

That is, counterfactual reach is not measured by how many possible worlds a model can score well on, but by how many perturbations it can absorb through principled local revision while preserving its load-bearing commitments. This mirrors institutional legitimacy under rapid change: legitimacy does not require never erring, but maintaining trust by keeping invariants clear and correction procedures active and public.

What follows:

1. **Against collapse into prediction:**  
   A predictor may interpolate or extrapolate successfully yet have low counterfactual reach if its successes do not survive structured intervention or if failure in one region forces global retraining. Reach concerns *repair topology*, not just forecast quality.

2. **Against collapse into compression:**  
   Compression rewards economical description of actual regularities. Counterfactual reach demands more: a compressed model must also organize unrealized alternatives such that nearby errors can be corrected without losing the model’s identity.

3. **Against collapse into causal modeling:**  
   Causal models already traffic in interventions, but counterfactual reach adds a constraint on *explanatory stability under revision*. Two causal models may fit the same intervention data; the one with greater reach is the one whose core commitments remain intact across more families of hypothetical change.

4. **Against collapse into invariance:**  
   Invariance names what stays fixed. Counterfactual reach concerns the **productive relation between what stays fixed and what is allowed to change**. It is a systems property of modular adaptation around preserved constraints.

So the illuminating idea is: **counterfactual reach is a legitimacy-like property of representations**. A good representation is not one that avoids being wrong; it is one that can be wrong in ways that are intelligible, corrigible, and non-identity-destroying across many counterfactual circumstances.

A practical test follows from the load-bearing lens: vary assumptions one by one. If changing them forces arbitrary, system-wide compensations, the representation has low counterfactual reach. If instead it supports disciplined local updates around stable invariants, it has high reach. This makes the concept precise as a structural criterion on **error-correction under hypothetical variation**, not a synonym for neighboring notions.

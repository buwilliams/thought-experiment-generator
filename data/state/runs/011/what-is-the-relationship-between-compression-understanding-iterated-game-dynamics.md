## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
Compression, understanding, and explanation are related through **their effects on repeated prediction and coordination**, not merely through brevity. In iterated settings, an explanation counts as understanding-producing when it **compresses many interactions into a rule that improves future play across encounters**. Compression is therefore valuable not because it makes a description shorter, but because it makes behavior more **reusable under repetition**.

From the perspective of iterated game dynamics, the key distinction is between a code that is merely compact and a model that changes what agents can do in future rounds. A lookup table of past moves may be highly detailed but gives little understanding if it does not generalize. By contrast, a rule like “cooperation persists when defection is detectable and future interaction is expected” is a compression that earns its keep: it explains many observed outcomes and predicts how they will change when the shadow of the future shortens or monitoring degrades.

So:

- **Compression** is reduction of descriptive burden.
- **Understanding** is possession of a compressed structure that supports successful anticipation in new rounds.
- **Explanation** is the articulation of why that compressed structure works — which incentives, feedbacks, and informational conditions make the pattern stable or unstable.

This perspective illuminates why some compressions are shallow. A slogan like “people cooperate because they are nice” may be compact, but it is a poor explanation because it fails under structural variation: anonymity, one-shot interaction, or hidden defection should not matter much on that account, yet in reality they matter a great deal. By contrast, an iterated-game explanation is **load-bearing**: alter repetition, observability, or retaliation capacity, and the predicted behavior changes systematically. That sensitivity is a sign of genuine explanation rather than mere summary.

The relationship, then, is not identity but hierarchy:

1. **Compression is necessary but not sufficient** for understanding.  
   A shorter description can discard the very variables that govern future interaction.

2. **Understanding is compression tested by recurrence.**  
   In repeated environments, to understand is to hold a compact model that continues to orient action as rounds accumulate.

3. **Explanation is compressed causal-strategic structure.**  
   It identifies which features of the game generate cooperation or collapse, and therefore what interventions will work.

What follows is that the best explanations are those that compress **the strategic invariants of repeated interaction**. They let us see many episodes as one game-form and therefore predict when cooperation will emerge, persist, or break down. On this view, explanation is not the shortest description of the past; it is the most economical account of what will continue to matter in the future.

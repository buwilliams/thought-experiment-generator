## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** A genuine explanation is distinguished from a sophisticated redescription by containing a **load-bearing generative constraint**: if you assume the explanation is true, it produces the phenomenon and limits what else could happen. A redescription, however elaborate, merely tracks the phenomenon in different words and leaves the space of possibilities largely unchanged.

To isolate the key variable, imagine a world with two theories of the same recurring event.

- **Theory A** says: “The event occurs when hidden rule R operates.”
- **Theory B** says: “The event is a manifestation of pattern P,” where P is just a high-level restatement of the event’s observed features.

Now assume each theory is real and follow the consequences.

If **A** is real, then the rule R must do work. It should imply not just *that* the event happens, but *why this event rather than nearby alternatives*. If we vary conditions, A constrains what would change and what would remain invariant. It may fail, but where it fails is itself informative, because the explanation had content that could collide with reality. Its details are load-bearing.

If **B** is real, nothing much follows beyond the original description. Calling the event “an instance of pattern P” may compress observations, unify vocabulary, or improve classification, but it does not generate the event from deeper constraints. If nearby possibilities are considered, B typically stretches to accommodate them by redescribing them too. Its details are not load-bearing; they are interchangeable with other elegant summaries.

So the key variable is not depth, sophistication, mathematics, or hidden entities. It is whether the account introduces **asymmetric constraint** into the space of possibilities.

This yields a practical distinction:

- A **genuine explanation** reduces arbitrariness.
- It tells us why some variations are impossible, unstable, or unexpected.
- It supports criticism because changing one part forces revisions elsewhere.
- It can be used creatively: to predict, to diagnose failures, to connect domains.

A **sophisticated redescription** may still be useful, but its usefulness is organizational rather than explanatory.
- It labels without generating.
- It mirrors the phenomenon rather than accounting for it.
- It survives too easily, because almost any outcome can be absorbed by adjusting the description.

What is illuminated is that explanation is not primarily about matching appearances; it is about supplying a structure that *could be wrong* because it says how the phenomenon is produced. In systems terms, explanation identifies the feedbacks, stocks, and constraints that make the behavior arise. In epistemic terms, it exposes itself to criticism by narrowing what is possible.

Therefore: **the difference is that an explanation has counterfactual grip, while a redescription has descriptive reach.** The former changes what the phenomenon means by embedding it in a constrained generating structure; the latter changes only how fluently we talk about it.

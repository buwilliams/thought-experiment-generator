## Conjecture

**Conjecture**

A genuine explanation is a **computationally load-bearing model** of a phenomenon: it identifies the information-processing structure that could, in principle, **generate, constrain, and vary** the phenomenon, rather than merely compress its surface regularities in more elegant language. A sophisticated redescription tracks the outputs; an explanation captures the **transformations** that produce them.

From the perspective that life, intelligence, and evolution are all substrate-independent computation under selection, the key distinction is this: explanations specify the **algorithmic organization** and **selection dynamics** responsible for an observed pattern. Redescription leaves the causal-informational architecture untouched. It says the same thing in a richer vocabulary, but does not expose what system, operating on what information, under what constraints, would recreate the effect.

This implies several tests.

1. **Generativity test**  
   An explanation should let us see how the phenomenon would arise from underlying rules, feedback loops, and constraints. If the account cannot show how the pattern is computed, it is likely description, not explanation.

2. **Counterfactual sensitivity test**  
   In a real explanation, changing a core detail changes the outcome or forces compensating revisions elsewhere. That is the load-bearing constraint test. If the terms are replaceable without loss, they are ornamental.

3. **Transfer test**  
   Because computation is substrate-agnostic, a genuine explanation should generalize across implementations when the same informational structure is preserved. If an account only fits one familiar surface form, it may be tied to appearances rather than mechanism.

4. **Compression-with-causation test**  
   Mere redescription can compress data. Explanation compresses by identifying **why this pattern instead of nearby alternatives**. It reduces arbitrariness by locating the selective and computational constraints that exclude other possibilities.

What is illuminated is that the explanation/redescription boundary is not mainly about vocabulary sophistication or level of abstraction. It is about whether the account captures the **actual information-transforming process** doing the work. In this view, to explain a cell, a mind, a market, or an evolutionary lineage is to identify the computational system that stores, transmits, mutates, evaluates, and stabilizes information. If an account names the pattern but not that process, it remains a gloss.

So the distinction can be stated sharply:

- **Redescription** = an alternative encoding of the phenomenon.
- **Explanation** = an account of the computation and selection structure that makes the phenomenon non-arbitrary.

This also dissolves a common confusion: higher-level explanations are not “mere descriptions” if they capture real computational organization. A software explanation of behavior is genuine even if it ignores transistor physics, provided the software structure is what is load-bearing for the phenomenon. Explanation succeeds at the level where the relevant computation is actually organized.

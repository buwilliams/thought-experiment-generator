## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
From the perspective of computational irreducibility, a genuine explanation is distinguished from a sophisticated redescription by whether it identifies a **more primitive generative rule with real constraints**, rather than merely compressing or re-labeling the observed pattern. If the phenomenon can only be known in detail by running the process, then explanation cannot consist in a shortcut prediction of outcomes; instead, it must locate the **rule, architecture, or mechanism** whose stepwise unfolding produces those outcomes.

What follows is a shift in the criterion of explanatory success. In reducible systems, explanation often looks like derivation: a compact law lets us predict many cases without simulating them. But in irreducible systems, that standard fails. There may be no faster route from rule to outcome than execution itself. So the contrast is not between “predictive” and “non-predictive” accounts, but between:

- an account that gives the **minimal load-bearing generator** of the behavior, and  
- an account that gives only a higher-level summary of the behavior already seen.

A redescription tracks the surface regularities of the phenomenon: it says, in effect, “this is a case of X-pattern,” or translates observations into more abstract vocabulary, while leaving open many different possible generators. Its parts are often interchangeable. If one term is replaced with another nearby term, little else changes. That shows weak constraint.

A genuine explanation, by contrast, narrows the space of possibilities. It tells us what must be iterated, what local interactions matter, what cannot be omitted without losing the phenomenon. Its details are load-bearing because altering them changes the generated behavior or the domain in which the account works. Even if it does not let us shortcut the computation, it explains by revealing **why this unfolding comes from this rule rather than another**.

This perspective also illuminates why explanation is often mistaken for compression. A sophisticated redescription may be elegant, general, and cognitively satisfying, but if it does not connect to the phenomenon’s generative source, it remains downstream of the thing to be explained. In irreducible domains, explanatory depth is not “saying it more cleverly” but “locating the rule from which running the process is unavoidable.”

So the key distinction is:

- **Redescription:** a compact or sophisticated restatement of observed behavior.  
- **Explanation:** identification of the constrained generative mechanism, even when the mechanism’s consequences cannot be known except by carrying out the computation.

Thus, computational irreducibility reframes explanation: not as the discovery of a predictive shortcut, but as the discovery of the **right underlying program**. Where no shortcut exists, explanation survives not in foretelling the trajectory, but in exposing the minimal structure that makes that trajectory happen.

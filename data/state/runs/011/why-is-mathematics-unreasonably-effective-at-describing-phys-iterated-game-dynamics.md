## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Mathematics is “unreasonably effective” in physics because the relation between mathematicians and the physical world is not a one-shot encounter but an iterated game of conjecture, prediction, criticism, and reuse. In repeated play, abstract structures that preserve reliable patterns across many rounds accumulate epistemic reputation, while brittle formalisms are discarded. What looks like mysterious pre-adaptation is the outcome of long-run selection under repeated interaction with reality.

From an iterated-game perspective, the key variable is the **shadow of the future**. Mathematics is developed in a setting where ideas are not consumed once and thrown away; they remain available for indefinite reuse. A theorem, notation, or structural framework can be “played again” against new problems decades later. This repeated reuse rewards **generality, composability, and invariance**—features that also matter in physics, because physical laws themselves are stable across many contexts. So mathematics evolves under pressures similar to those imposed by the world: constructions that continue to “cooperate” with many later applications survive.

This reframes the puzzle. Mathematics was not designed for a particular physical target, but repeated rounds of internal mathematical criticism and later application create a **reputation system for abstractions**. Concepts such as symmetry groups, differential equations, Hilbert spaces, or topology gain standing because they succeed across many interactions, not because anyone foresaw all future uses. Physics then draws from a stock of abstractions already filtered for durability under iteration. The fit seems unreasonable only if one imagines a single-shot design problem; under repeated play, cross-domain transfer is exactly what should emerge.

What follows is that effectiveness should be strongest where the interaction structure is richest: where physical reality presents **stable, repeatable regularities** and where mathematical objects encode **invariants under transformation**. It should weaken when horizons shorten—when inquiry becomes local, ad hoc, or noisy—or when “defection” is obscured, meaning when failed models are not clearly exposed by experiment or when mathematical work is insulated from downstream criticism. In those conditions, cooperation between mathematics and physics collapses into formalism without grip or empiricism without deep structure.

The load-bearing claim is not merely that “useful math survives.” More specifically: **repetition + memory + visibility of success/failure** produce selection for abstractions that can coordinate many future encounters. Remove any of these and the explanation degrades. If there were no repeated reuse, pure mathematics would have less reason to generate portable structures; if there were no memory, successful abstractions could not accumulate reputation; if failure were hidden, sterile systems could persist unchecked.

So the effectiveness is not magic and not mainly anthropic coincidence. It is an emergent property of a long iterated game between minds seeking compressive structure and a world that rewards some structures repeatedly. Mathematics works because both mathematics and physics are, in different ways, organized around what remains stable through many rounds.

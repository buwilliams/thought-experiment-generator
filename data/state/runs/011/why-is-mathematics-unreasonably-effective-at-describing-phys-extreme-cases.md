3. **Effectiveness should increase near extremes.** Physics becomes more mathematical near limits: zero friction, infinite systems, infinitesimal change, maximal symmetry, least action, equilibrium, critical points, asymptotic regimes. These are the places where hidden structure is revealed because contingent detail drops out.

4. **Mismatches are equally diagnostic.** Where mathematics proliferates but physics does not use it, we may be seeing structures not instantiated by our universe. Where physics resists mathematization, we may be dealing with domains not yet pushed to the right variables, scales, or invariants.

The deep illumination is this: mathematics and physics are not two separately evolved enterprises that happen to line up. They are both searches over possibility space for what remains fixed under transformation. Mathematics studies such fixed structure in general; physics discovers which of those structures are physically instantiated. Their overlap is therefore not miraculous but a consequence of the conditions for there being a knowable world at all.

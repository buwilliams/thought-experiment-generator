## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Compression, understanding, and explanation are related but non-identical because what matters is not mere shortening of description, but preservation of the **functional invariants** that let a representation guide prediction, counterfactual reasoning, and control. A concept remains legitimate under deeper redescription when those higher-level invariants survive, even if its metaphysical “surface story” is discarded.

**Collision of perspective with problem:**

A compressed description is only epistemically valuable if it preserves the structure that does explanatory work. Many compressions are cheap: a hash, a slogan, a lookup table, or a fitted latent code may reduce storage while destroying the ability to answer “what would happen if…?” That is compression without understanding.

By contrast, **understanding** is compression that preserves **generative and counterfactual invariants**. It identifies which differences matter, which can vary freely, and how the system would change under interventions. So understanding is not the shortest code in itself; it is the possession of a redescription whose simplifications are **load-bearing** rather than merely lossy.

**Explanation** is the communicable form of that understanding: a structured account that shows why the observed regularities hold by exhibiting the constraints, mechanisms, or abstract relations that generate them. An explanation may compress, but its success depends on preserving the higher-level control structure. If a deeper level story eliminates the old vocabulary yet preserves those invariants, the higher-level concept remains legitimate. Temperature survives molecular redescription because the thermodynamic role remains. “Vital force” does not, because the functional role was not independently stable once biology was redescribed.

So the key distinction is:

- **Compression:** reduction in description length.
- **Understanding:** compression that preserves actionable invariants.
- **Explanation:** an articulation of that invariant-preserving structure in a form that supports criticism, transfer, and intervention.

What follows is that the relationship is not linear but selective. Better explanations often achieve compression because reality contains discoverable regularities. But maximum compression is not the goal of explanation. A law, model, or theory can be slightly longer than a black-box summary yet yield vastly more understanding because it preserves the right relational structure.

This also illuminates why deeper explanation does not automatically “reduce away” higher-level concepts. If the higher level captures real control variables, feedback loops, or conserved patterns, then redescription that abolishes them loses explanatory power. The loss of metaphysical mystique is not the loss of the concept. The test is whether the concept’s functional role survives.

So the deepest link is: **understanding is invariant-preserving compression, and explanation is the explicit map of those invariants across descriptions.**

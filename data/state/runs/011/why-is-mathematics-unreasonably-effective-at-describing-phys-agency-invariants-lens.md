Newtonian mechanics, Maxwell’s equations, and quantum formalisms are powerful because they capture high-leverage invariants from which many details flow. This also explains the limits. Mathematics is not uniformly effective at all descriptive levels. It is strongest where stable invariants exist and weakest where our chosen level has not yet isolated them, or where complexity, path dependence, and sensitivity obscure them. The “unreasonable” part often reflects our surprise that reality contains so much deep compressible structure, and that abstract inquiry can discover it before application.

So the collision yields this: **mathematics is effective in physics because both disciplines are, in their most successful moments, about the same thing — the discovery of structures that remain true when the story is told in a deeper way.** What looks like a miracle is the recognition that higher-level control structures are real, and mathematics is our best method for representing and criticizing them.

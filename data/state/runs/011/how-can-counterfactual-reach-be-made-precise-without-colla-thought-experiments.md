## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** *Counterfactual reach* can be made precise as a property of a representation relative to a space of admissible interventions: it is the extent to which the representation supports **correct transport of consequences across unrealized but structurally compatible changes**, with only bounded revision. What makes it distinct is not that it predicts well, compresses well, encodes causes, or remains invariant, but that it preserves the **load-bearing constraints** needed to answer many nearby “what if” questions without retraining the whole model.

To isolate the key variable, imagine two systems that are identical on all observed data and equally good at prediction and compression. They even encode the same causal graph at a coarse level. But now introduce a family of interventions never seen in training: parameter shifts, recombinations of mechanisms, and context changes that preserve the system’s deep constraints while altering surface regularities. One representation continues to answer these intervention-questions by reusing internal structure; the other fails unless re-fit from scratch.

If this difference is real, then counterfactual reach is not reducible to predictive accuracy, because both systems predicted equally well before intervention. It is not reducible to compression, because a code can be short yet brittle outside the observed manifold. It is not identical to causal modeling, because two models may share a causal skeleton while differing in whether their variables are *compositional enough* to support transport into novel hypothetical regimes. And it is not mere invariance, because reach concerns not what stays fixed, but what can be **systematically varied** while the representation still yields correct consequences.

So the structural criterion is this: a representation has high counterfactual reach when its parts are **modular under intervention** and **jointly constrained enough** that changing one part propagates determinate updates through the rest. In systems terms, it captures the organization of feedback and dependency such that perturbations can be traced through the model without global collapse. In load-bearing terms, the representation’s elements are not decorative summaries of past data; they are constraints whose alteration forces specific downstream revisions.

A possible precision follows: define a set of admissible counterfactual transformations on the underlying system. Then measure how many such transformations a representation can answer correctly using only local recomposition of its internal structure, rather than fresh empirical fitting. Counterfactual reach is the size and depth of that transformation set, weighted by the cost of revision and the fidelity of transported consequences.

What this illuminates is that counterfactual reach is a criterion of **explanatory usability under hypothetical variation**. It marks the difference between a model that merely tracks actuality and one that has grasped enough structure to survive contact with possibility. The core idea, then, is: **counterfactual reach is the breadth of nearby possibility over which an explanation remains computationally and structurally productive.**

## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** *“Counterfactual reach” becomes precise when treated not as a predictive score or representational virtue, but as a property of an explanation’s **reusability across interventions on its own origin conditions**. It measures how far an explanatory structure continues to answer new “what if” questions after the local problem that produced it has been altered.*

This perspective suggests asking: why did we need “counterfactual reach” at all? It likely arises because existing notions each solved narrower problems:

- **Prediction** solved forecasting observables.
- **Compression** solved efficient coding of regularities.
- **Causal modeling** solved disciplined intervention-tracking.
- **Invariance** solved robustness across transformations or environments.

But none of these fully captured the older scientific ambition: an explanation that remains useful when the *problem setting itself shifts*. So “counterfactual reach” inherits unnoticed assumptions from these predecessors — especially that explanatory success is exhausted by fit, efficiency, manipulability, or stability. That inheritance is the hidden constraint.

A structural criterion, then, should not ask whether a model predicts well, compresses well, or identifies causes well *in its original domain*. It should ask whether its internal organization is **load-bearing under systematically altered questions**. If changing background conditions, intervention targets, or evaluative aims leaves the explanation able to generate non-ad hoc answers, then it has reach. If each new counterfactual requires bolted-on repairs, then the original structure was locally useful but not far-reaching.

So the precise object is:

> **Counterfactual reach = the extent to which an explanatory structure can be recomposed to answer families of non-actual scenarios, with minimal arbitrary supplementation, when the assumptions that originally motivated the structure are varied.**

This avoids collapse into neighboring notions because:

- A predictor may extrapolate accurately without exposing why its components would survive altered questions.
- A compressor may encode data efficiently while being useless for intervention or redesign.
- A causal model may support a fixed intervention algebra yet fail when ontology or boundary conditions shift.
- An invariant representation may remain stable across transformations while saying little about unrealized alternatives.

What follows is that counterfactual reach is best understood as a **second-order structural property**: not first-order success on a task, but the durability of explanatory organization under expansion of the problem space.

Operationally, one would test it by constructing **origin-disrupting counterfactuals**: vary the assumptions, boundaries, or purposes that gave rise to the model, and measure how much of the explanation remains load-bearing versus how much must be replaced. High reach means the same core constraints continue to organize answers across these shifts.

The illuminating payoff is this: “counterfactual reach” names a neglected dimension of knowledge growth. It tracks not mere success within a problem, but whether an explanation has escaped the invisible scaffolding of the problem that originally called it forth.

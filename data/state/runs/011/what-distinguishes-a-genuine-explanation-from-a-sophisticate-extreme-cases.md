## Conjecture

Conjecture: A genuine explanation is one that survives movement to the boundary; a sophisticated redescription breaks when the phenomenon is pushed to extremes.

To test the difference, do not look first at ordinary cases, where many verbal frameworks can be made to fit. Push the phenomenon toward its limits: remove assumed background conditions, vary parameters far beyond the regime in which the description was formed, ask what happens at zero, infinity, perfect isolation, perfect noise, total scale-up, total scale-down, or recursive repetition. At the boundary, hidden structure becomes visible.

A redescription tracks surface regularities in the middle of things. It says, in more abstract or more elegant language, what already happens, but it does not contain the resources to say what would happen if key conditions were altered. Its terms are often mutually substitutable without loss. That is the sign of low load-bearing constraint: the account sounds rich, but little in it forces anything else.

A genuine explanation, by contrast, contains a mechanism, principle, or generative structure that constrains counterfactuals. When pushed to the edge, it does not merely restate the observed pattern; it implies where the pattern should persist, where it should fail, and why. Its details are load-bearing because changing them changes the boundary behavior.

So the distinguishing mark is not “depth” of language or even present predictive fit. It is whether the account compresses the phenomenon into a structure that can be stressed. If the explanation is real, then extreme-case reasoning should produce nontrivial consequences: singularities, asymptotes, phase changes, breakdown points, invariants, or forbidden states. These are not optional decorations. They are where the explanatory content cashes out.

From a fallibilist viewpoint, this also explains why genuine explanations are criticizable in a stronger way. They risk more. Because they say what happens at the limits, they expose themselves to refutation by unusual cases, scaling failures, or regime changes. Redescriptions are safer precisely because they explain less; they can be endlessly patched to match whatever remains in the middle.

In systems terms, a genuine explanation identifies the structure that produces behavior across regimes — the stocks, flows, feedback loops, or constraints whose alteration shifts the whole pattern. A redescription names the behavior while leaving the generating structure opaque.

Therefore: if two accounts fit the same ordinary data, prefer the one that makes sharper claims about edge conditions and breakdown. The boundary is a discriminator. What survives there is explanation; what dissolves there was description wearing theoretical clothing.

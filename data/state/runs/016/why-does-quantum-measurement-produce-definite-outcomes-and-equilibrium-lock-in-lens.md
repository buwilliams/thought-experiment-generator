## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** The “measurement problem” is partly illuminated as a **public-goods / coordination problem inside a multi-scale physical description**: definite outcomes are not merely blocked by ignorance or lack of motivation to describe them better, but by a structural underprovision of globally accessible phase information. When coherence is non-excludable in principle but diffusely distributed into the environment in practice, rationally available local descriptions settle into a stable “one outcome” equilibrium even though the underlying dynamics may remain globally unitary.

More concretely: in quantum evolution, amplitudes for different outcomes coexist and can interfere. But once a system becomes entangled with many environmental degrees of freedom, the information required to reconstruct interference is spread out, nonlocally, across an enormous joint state. That information behaves like a public good with no effective private owner: no local observer can cheaply exclude others from it, capture it, or reassemble it for use. The benefits of maintaining access to global phase coherence are diffuse across the whole system, while the cost of tracking it scales catastrophically. So the physical situation is not just “we are confused” but “the structure underprovides recoverable coherence.” Decohered branches then function as a stable no-recombination equilibrium.

This perspective separates two questions:

1. **Why definite outcomes?**  
   Because interaction with the environment creates a structural trap in which locally available observables support only quasi-classical, effectively exclusive records. “Definiteness” is what a subsystem gets when globally relevant coherence has been dispersed beyond feasible recollection. The pointer basis is selected by the system-environment interaction structure: the robust states are precisely those whose records can be redundantly proliferated. In systems terms, redundancy and decoherence create positive feedback favoring stable classical descriptions.

2. **What selects which outcome occurs?**  
   This lens suggests that “selection” may be the wrong demand if one expects a further dynamical chooser beyond unitary evolution plus decoherence. The structure explains why observers within a branch encounter a single outcome, but not necessarily why one branch is uniquely realized from a God’s-eye view. If collapse is real, then selection requires an added mechanism. If many-worlds is right, there is no single global selection; there is branch-relative outcome definiteness. The collision with the public-goods lens therefore clarifies that the hard remainder is not “why don’t we see superpositions?”—decoherence structurally answers much of that—but “is there an ontologically privileged allocator of actuality?”

So the key claim is: **measurement yields definite experienced outcomes because coherence is structurally underprovided as a usable resource to subsystems, not merely because observers are ignorant.** This does not by itself solve the full ontology of outcome selection, but it sharply localizes the unsolved part: whether physics contains a genuine branch-selecting process, or only branch-indexed observers embedded in a world where global coherence exists yet is inaccessible in practice.

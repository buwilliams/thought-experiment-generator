## Conjecture

Conjecture: Dark energy is not a new substance but the residual bookkeeping term that remains when gravity responds only to relationally usable information, not to the full vacuum energy counted by quantum field theory. The cosmological constant is small because most of what QFT calls “vacuum energy” is not gravitationally legible.

Construct the isolating scenario: assume vacuum energy in QFT is real as an abstract calculational object, and assume general relativity’s cosmological constant is also real as the term governing large-scale acceleration. Now isolate the key variable: not “how much energy exists,” but “which parts of energy count as sources for spacetime curvature?” Follow that strictly.

In QFT, vacuum energy is obtained by summing mode contributions over all fields. This produces an enormous constant offset. But a constant offset in nongravitational physics is usually unobservable: only energy differences matter. The catastrophe appears only when we assume gravity couples to that whole absolute sum. So the problem is already telling us that two notions of “energy” are being conflated: calculational vacuum energy and gravitational source energy.

Now assume gravity is an information-selective theory: it couples only to energy that can participate in causal, relational structure across spacetime — excitations, gradients, phase transitions, stress, and other differences that can enter feedback loops — while the homogeneous zero-point baseline mostly cancels because it carries no further usable distinction. In that case, the enormous QFT vacuum estimate is like gauge redundancy: real inside the formalism, but not fully ontic for gravity.

What follows? First, the cosmological constant should not be expected to track naive UV mode sums. The small observed value is not a fine-tuned cancellation among huge physical contributions, but a residual left after the non-relational bulk is projected out. Second, dark energy should look almost perfectly smooth and constant, because it is the leftover of this projection, not a dynamical fluid with rich local structure. Third, attempts to solve the problem by adding more high-energy fields may fail, because the decisive variable is not field content but the coupling rule between information and geometry.

This illuminates why the mismatch is so extreme: QFT and GR are not disagreeing about one number; they are answering different questions. QFT counts all formally available vacuum degrees of freedom. Cosmology measures the tiny remainder that survives the translation into gravitating structure.

So the conjecture is: the smallness of the cosmological constant is evidence that spacetime does not gravitate absolute vacuum energy directly. Dark energy is the residual curvature term produced after gravity filters out most non-relational vacuum information. The real task is therefore not to cancel vacuum energy, but to discover the principle that determines gravitational legibility.

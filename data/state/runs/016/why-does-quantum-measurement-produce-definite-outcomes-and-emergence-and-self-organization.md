## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
Quantum measurement yields definite outcomes not because a microscopic component “collapses” in isolation, but because definiteness is an **emergent system-level property** of the interaction structure linking system, apparatus, and environment. What gets selected is not a hidden preexisting value, but a stable branch of the total interaction network that can amplify, record, and recursively reinforce itself across many coupled degrees of freedom.

The key explanatory shift is from “What happens to the particle?” to “What global structure makes some correlations self-stabilizing and others dynamically irrelevant?” A measurement setup is not a passive observer of a quantum state; it is a **feedback-amplifying system** designed to convert microscale alternatives into macroscopically robust records. Once the measured system becomes entangled with the apparatus and then with the environment, the total system develops a branching structure. In that structure, only certain outcome-patterns are stable enough to persist as records, synchronize observers, and propagate into the future.

So the definiteness of outcomes is plausibly an emergent phenomenon of **redundant record formation**. An “outcome” is definite when it is not merely one term in a superposition mathematically, but one pattern that has been copied into many environmental channels and thereby made robust against interference. Definite outcomes are, on this view, the stable attractors of a measurement interaction.

What selects which outcome occurs? This perspective suggests that “selection” is not best understood as a further microscopic event over and above the interaction. Rather, the interaction structure defines a set of possible stable outcome branches, weighted by the quantum state’s amplitudes, and the experienced outcome is one such branch rendered dynamically autonomous by decohering feedback with the environment. The apparatus-environment system does not reveal a unique classical fact that existed beforehand; it **constructs classical definiteness** by suppressing interference and stabilizing one record-pattern relative to others.

This illuminates why the measurement problem is so resistant when framed component-wise. If one asks which part — particle, observer, apparatus, or wavefunction — causes collapse, one may be aiming at the wrong explanatory level. The crucial fact is the **pattern of coupling**: amplification, decoherence, record redundancy, and irreversibility. Definite outcomes arise when the network is structured so that some correlations become globally self-consistent and mutually reinforcing while incompatible alternatives no longer interact.

This does **not by itself solve** why one experienced branch rather than another is actualized, if one demands a single-outcome ontology. But it suggests that much of what “definite outcome” means is already explained structurally, without adding ad hoc collapse triggers. The residual mystery is then narrower: not “How does quantum possibility become classical fact at all?” but “What, if anything, beyond emergent branching and decoherence is needed to account for unique experienced actuality?”

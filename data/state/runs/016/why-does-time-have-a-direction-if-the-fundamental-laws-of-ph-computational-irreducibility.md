## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
Time has a direction not because the fundamental laws encode a preferred temporal arrow, but because the actual history of the universe is a computationally irreducible unfolding from a highly special low-entropy initial condition. In such systems, the asymmetry we call “the direction of time” is not imposed by the micro-laws; it emerges from the fact that generating later states from earlier ones is the only tractable way to obtain their structure. The past is compressible as a simple boundary condition plus lawful evolution; the future is not available except by running the process.

**What this perspective illuminates**

If the laws are time-symmetric, then in principle a trajectory can be traversed in either direction. But computational irreducibility shifts the issue: the question is not whether reverse evolution is permitted, but whether there is any shortcut to the macroscopic structures that arise. In an irreducible universe, there often is not. Complex higher-level order—galaxies, chemistry, life, memory, records—must be generated through the actual sequence of interactions.

This gives a natural asymmetry between past and future:

- **The past exists as records, correlations, and present structure** produced by prior computation.
- **The future does not exist in that way**, because those correlations have not yet been generated.

So the arrow of time is tied to the one-way accumulation of computable consequences from boundary conditions. Entropy increase is then not merely statistical drift, but the signature of a process that progressively spreads and hides information in ever more distributed correlations, making reconstruction of precise microstructure harder while making forward generation the only feasible route.

**What follows**

1. **Temporal direction is perspectival but objective at the level of embedded agents.**  
   Agents inside the universe can remember the past and not the future because memory itself is a physical record produced by this irreversible-looking computation. A memory of the future would require correlations that have not yet been generated.

2. **The low-entropy beginning is the real asymmetry.**  
   Time-symmetric laws plus generic states would not yield a usable arrow. What matters is a simple, highly nongeneric starting condition from which complexity unfolds. The arrow is therefore a property of lawful evolution *from a special boundary condition*, not of the equations alone.

3. **Retrodiction and prediction are not symmetric in practice even if the laws are symmetric in form.**  
   The past can often be inferred from present records because those records are compressed remnants of prior evolution. The future generally cannot be known except by further evolution of the system itself.

**Core claim**

The direction of time is best understood as an emergent asymmetry of computation: in a universe with time-symmetric laws, a special initial condition and computationally irreducible evolution make “forward” the direction in which structure, records, and entropy-producing correlations are actually generated. Thus temporal direction is not a contradiction of time-symmetric physics, but a higher-level fact about what lawful histories are computationally like.

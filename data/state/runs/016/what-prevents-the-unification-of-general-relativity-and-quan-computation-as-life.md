## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** What blocks unification of general relativity and quantum mechanics is not merely a technical mismatch between two successful theories, but a substrate mismatch in how each encodes computation. Quantum mechanics models reality as local information transformation over possible states; general relativity models reality as the evolving geometry that defines which transformations and causal relations are even possible. In computational terms, QM assumes a computation *running on* a fixed or parameterized causal substrate, while GR makes the causal substrate itself dynamical. The obstacle is therefore a failure to express both **state-update** and **update-rule topology** within one coherent computational framework.

From this perspective, the conflict is illuminated as follows:

- **Quantum mechanics** describes amplitudes, superposition, interference, and entanglement: constraints on how information is processed and correlated.
- **General relativity** describes spacetime curvature, causal structure, and geodesic motion: constraints on how information flow is organized globally.
- Unification fails because one theory quantizes observables within a background, while the other says the background is itself part of the physical content.

If computation is fundamental, then a unified theory must treat spacetime not as primitive substance but as an emergent data structure arising from deeper informational dynamics. Geometry would then be a large-scale compression of underlying causal-computational relations, much as thermodynamics compresses microstate behavior. On this view, “quantizing gravity” may be an incomplete framing; the deeper task is to explain how a network of information-processing events can yield both quantum correlation structure and effective smooth spacetime.

What such a theory would need to explain:

1. **Why spacetime appears continuous** if the underlying computation is discrete, combinatorial, or otherwise non-geometric.
2. **Why locality emerges** despite quantum nonlocal correlations.
3. **Why gravity is universal** — i.e., why all forms of energy-information couple to geometry in the same way.
4. **How causal structure becomes dynamical** without losing quantum coherence.
5. **How classical spacetime and definite outcomes emerge** from fundamentally quantum informational processes.
6. **What sets the Planck scale** as the regime where computational granularity of spacetime can no longer be ignored.
7. **Black hole information behavior**: whether horizons are limits on recoverable computation, not destruction of information.
8. **The arrow of time**: whether temporal asymmetry reflects asymmetries in computational accessibility, record formation, or growth of coarse-grained informational structure.

So the conjecture is: **A successful unification will not primarily be a merger of “particles” and “curvature,” but a theory of self-organizing computation in which quantum state relations and spacetime causal structure are two emergent aspects of the same underlying informational process.** What currently prevents unification is that our existing formalisms separate computation from the substrate that defines possible computation, whereas in reality the substrate is itself computable, evolvable, and emergent.

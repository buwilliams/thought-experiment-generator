## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
The Born rule can be approached not as a primitive law of rational betting or a postulate about measurement outcomes, but as an inherited compression of a deeper selection problem: *which branch structures are capable of leaving stable, repeatable, intersubjectively accessible records at all?* On this view, “probability” is what survived from earlier attempts to explain measurement as outcome-frequencies, while the unexamined assumption is that all decohered branches count equally as candidate records. They likely do not.

Origin-wise, the probability problem in quantum theory arose to solve a prior classical problem: how to connect a dynamical law to observed frequencies. In classical statistical mechanics, probabilities tracked ignorance over already-definite states. Quantum theory inherited the demand for frequencies and records, but replaced definite microstates with branching structure. The hidden carryover is the assumption that probability must be assigned *after* branching, rather than emerging from the conditions under which branches become robust records.

If we ask what the Born rule replaced, it replaced ad hoc collapse-frequency talk with a measure over branches. But this may have smuggled in a false ontology: that branches are simply “there” and need weighting externally. A systems view suggests instead that branch structure, environmental redundancy, and record durability form a coupled dynamical system. The relevant stock is not branches simpliciter, but *durable records*; the flows are decoherence, amplification, error accumulation, and environmental copying; the feedback loop is that only records stably reproducible across many subsystems become the basis for future records and observers.

Then the conjecture is: **Born weights arise because only branches whose amplitudes support stable amplification and redundant encoding persist as usable records across repeated interactions, and the asymptotic distribution of such durable records scales with amplitude-squared.** In other words, amplitude-squared is not merely a measure on branches; it is the growth rate of branches capable of entering the iterated game of record reproduction.

What follows is a shift in explanatory target. One need not derive “subjective credence” first. One asks instead: under repeated system-environment interactions, what branch features are evolutionarily stable in the space of records? If branches with non-Born weighting fail to maintain reproducible macroscopic traces—because they are too fragile, too interference-prone, or insufficiently redundantly encoded—then observers embedded in stable record ecologies will inevitably infer Born statistics. The rule would then be selected by record ecology, not merely stipulated by formal symmetry.

What this illuminates is why probability appears tied to observation at all. Observation is not a neutral sampling of branches; it is participation in a branching environment that filters for durable inscriptions. The Born rule would thus express a constraint on *which histories can keep being remembered*, not just on which outcomes occur. If this is right, derivations of the Born rule should be recast as no-go theorems for non-Born record economies: alternative measures do not fail merely mathematically, but because they cannot sustain stable worlds of records.

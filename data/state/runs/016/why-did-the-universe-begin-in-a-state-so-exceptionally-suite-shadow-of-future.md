## Conjecture

Conjecture: the universe’s “special” beginning is best understood not as a goal-directed setup for knowledge, but as a boundary condition that made stable asymmetries persist long enough for cumulative selection among physical processes to occur. In that sense, the relevant analogue of cooperation is not virtue but repeated interaction under memory.

On this view, the key question is: what early-universe features made local processes “answerable” to their past? A universe near thermal equilibrium everywhere, or one whose fluctuations instantly erased records, would be like a one-shot game: no durable consequences, no compounding, no ratcheting. Irreversible structure and knowledge growth require the opposite. They require (1) free energy gradients, (2) durable records, and (3) repeated interactions in which present configurations constrain future possibilities. Those are the physical analogues of visibility, memory, and responsive consequence.

So the low-entropy beginning matters not because it was “for” observers, but because it created a long shadow of the future. Gravitational instability then turns tiny differences into persistent structures; structures store information about earlier states; later interactions are conditioned by those records. Stars, chemistry, biology, and epistemic agents all become possible because the system is iterated rather than self-resetting. Knowledge growth is what happens when some structures can preserve error-correcting records across many rounds of interaction with reality.

What follows is a reframing of the specialness problem. The puzzle is not merely “why low entropy?” but “why were there global conditions that made history matter?” A universe hospitable to knowledge is one where defection from order—thermalization, erasure, decoherence without record, collapse into featureless equilibrium—does not immediately win everywhere. Instead, the initial condition sets up a regime in which local regularities can survive, be tested, and be amplified. That is enough to explain the possibility of knowledge growth without attributing purpose.

This does not explain why that boundary condition obtained in the first place. But it illuminates what any non-teleological explanation must account for: not observers, but an iterated physical game with memory. Any adequate cosmology must explain why the universe began in a state where information could become legible to later states and have consequences for them. If there is a deeper law or selection principle, its job is to explain the emergence of that regime.

So the collision of perspectives yields this claim: the universe’s initial specialness is the precondition for repeated, record-bearing dynamics, and knowledge arises wherever the structure of interaction makes past states visible enough to future states that error can be retained, exposed, and corrected. The mystery is therefore the origin of cosmic memory, not the existence of cosmic purpose.

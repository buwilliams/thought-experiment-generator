## Conjecture

Conjecture: the universe’s “special” beginning is best understood not as a one-off lucky microstate selected for our sake, but as a structural precondition for the existence of any open-ended growth process at all. In that sense, the low-entropy, far-from-equilibrium start is less like an improbable gift and more like the minimal enabling infrastructure for knowledge-creating dynamics.

From the public-goods lens, the key distinction is between local incentives and system-level possibility. Knowledge growth, irreversible structure, and records of the past are all non-excludable in an important physical sense: once a world contains stable regularities, free energy gradients, and persistent records, many processes can exploit them. But no subsystem “pays” to create those background conditions. They must already exist at the level of the whole system. So the puzzle is not why matter “chose” to cooperate, but why the universe supplied the globally available enabling good: usable disequilibrium.

That reframes the issue. A high-entropy generic state is like the no-provision equilibrium in a public-goods game: locally unobjectionable, globally sterile. Nothing is “wrong” with it, but it underprovides the shared resource required for cumulative structure. A low-entropy beginning functions as provision of a universal public good: abundant thermodynamic headroom, asymmetries, and the possibility of irreversible records. Once provided, many structure-forming and knowledge-bearing processes can free-ride on it. Without it, no amount of local cleverness could bootstrap complexity.

What follows is that the universe’s initial specialness should not be explained by importing purposes, but by identifying a lawlike or explanatory principle that makes such provision non-contingent. The real target of explanation is not “why us?” but “what kind of cosmological rule avoids the dead equilibrium?” If there is a satisfactory explanation, it will likely show that what appears to us as highly special initial data is, under a deeper description, a generic consequence of boundary-law structure, cosmological reproduction/selection, or a measure over histories in which only low-entropy boundaries generate robust, record-bearing worlds.

This also illuminates why anthropic reasoning feels unsatisfactory here. Anthropic selection can explain why observers find themselves where observation is possible, but it does not explain provision of the enabling good itself; it merely conditions on its prior existence. It is analogous to noting that agents are observed only in games where the public good happened to be funded. True, but not explanatory of the funding mechanism.

So the collision of perspectives yields this claim: the universe’s special beginning is best treated as a structural provision problem. Irreversible complexity requires globally available disequilibrium that no local process can supply for itself. Therefore any non-teleological explanation must explain the origin of that provision at the level of cosmological law or boundary conditions, not at the level of observers, local dynamics, or retrospective selection.

## Conjecture

Conjecture: the “definite outcome” in quantum measurement is not produced by a special collapse event layered onto unitary physics; it is produced when a quantum interaction is embedded in a larger knowledge-bearing system whose records must become mutually consistent and stably copyable. What selects the outcome is then not an extra physical trigger but the branch-relative record that survives this consistency-and-copyability constraint.

Why this conjecture is load-bearing:

If you say definiteness comes from consciousness, hidden variables, stochastic collapse, decoherence alone, or branching alone, ask what each detail is doing. Decoherence is load-bearing for suppressing interference and defining robust pointer states. But decoherence by itself does not yield a single experienced result; if you swap it out, you lose the account of why macroscopic records are stable. Branching is load-bearing for preserving unitary evolution while explaining why multiple outcome-record structures can coexist. If you remove branching, you must add some compensating mechanism that actually picks one result. Collapse theories make that selection rule load-bearing, but then the collapse law itself must be tuned to recover Born statistics and avoid conflict with reversible microscopic dynamics. Hidden variables make the extra ontology load-bearing, but then it must carry the explanatory burden of nonlocal correlations and of why observable statistics still track the quantum state.

The least-interchangeable structure seems to be this: measurement is an interaction that amplifies microscopic distinctions into macroscopic, redundantly encoded records. Once records are redundantly encoded across apparatus, environment, and observers, only certain outcome-bases remain stable under further copying. Those are the outcomes we call definite. “Which outcome occurs?” is then indexical, not global: which stable record-sequence a given observer finds themselves in. If you alter the role of redundant records, the explanation stops accounting for why outcomes are classical-looking, shareable, and hard to reverse.

What follows:

1. Definite outcomes are inseparable from record formation. A “measurement” without durable, proliferating records is not yet a full measurement in the sense needing explanation.
2. Outcome selection is constrained by the structure of the interaction. The apparatus-environment coupling helps determine the pointer basis; this is not decorative detail but what makes some distinctions objectively recordable and others not.
3. The remaining mystery is not “why one actual world rather than another?” but “why observers should expect Born-weighted self-location among branches/records.” That shifts the hard problem from dynamics to decision/self-locating uncertainty.
4. Any rival interpretation must identify its truly load-bearing additions. If its special ingredient can be changed without forcing downstream revisions, it is probably explanatory padding.

So the collision between this perspective and the measurement problem illuminates a criterion: the real explanatory core is whatever is needed to connect microscopic superposition to stable, shareable, redundant records. Definite outcomes arise there. Outcome “selection” is whatever your ontology says about being located within one such record-structure — and that is where interpretations genuinely differ.

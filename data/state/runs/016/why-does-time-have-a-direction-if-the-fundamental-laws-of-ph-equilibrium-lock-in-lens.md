**Conjecture:** The “direction” of time is not supplied by the time-symmetric micro-laws themselves, but by a structurally underprovided global condition: a low-entropy boundary state that many local processes can exploit but no local law can generate on its own. The arrow of time is what it looks like when the universe begins with an informational resource that is non-excludable and diffusely usable by all subsystems.

Here the public-goods lens clarifies a common confusion. If the laws are symmetric, one might think the problem is merely psychological: we project direction onto neutral dynamics, or we are biased by memory and causation. But the deeper issue is structural. Entropy increase, memory formation, work extraction, record keeping, evolution, and irreversible macroscopic regularities all depend on access to a shared asymmetry: the universe’s extraordinarily special low-entropy past. That asymmetry functions like a public good. No subsystem can privately own it; all can draw on it. Its benefits are diffuse, showing up everywhere as gradients, usable free energy, and the possibility of stable records.

On this view, the puzzle is not “How do symmetric laws mysteriously create asymmetry?” They do not. Rather: “Why is the enabling boundary condition present at all?” Without it, individually lawful local interactions would not add up to a world with a robust temporal arrow. They would yield, generically, equilibrium or near-equilibrium behavior with no durable distinction between past and future. So the arrow is not an extra law but the global exploitation of an initially underived resource.

What follows is that appeals to local mechanism alone are insufficient in principle. Local dynamics can propagate, amplify, and consume temporal asymmetry, but not explain the existence of the asymmetry they spend. In systems terms, the arrow is maintained by a stock: low entropy. The flows are all irreversible processes that deplete it while generating records, structure, and apparent causation. The feedback is that these records stabilize agents who themselves further exploit and document the arrow. But none of this explains the original stock.

This also illuminates why the arrow appears universal and coordinated. Public goods create common behavioral regimes across many agents without centralized control. Likewise, one low-entropy boundary condition can align thermodynamic, psychological, computational, and cosmological arrows across otherwise separate systems. Their agreement is not mysterious if they are all drawing on the same global resource.

So the central explanatory burden shifts. The real asymmetry is not in the dynamical laws but in the underived initial condition. Time’s direction is the macroscopic signature of a universe endowed with a shared, non-excludable reservoir of order whose benefits are diffuse enough to structure everything, yet whose origin remains the unsolved part.

## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
Time has a direction because physically realized computation is generically **history-dependent, resource-dissipating, and selection-shaped**, even when the microscopic update rules are time-symmetric. The “arrow of time” is not primarily a feature of the laws; it is a feature of which computational trajectories are **stable, copyable, and accumulative** in our universe.

From the computation-first perspective, a universe is not just evolving under laws; it is **transforming information**. Time-symmetric laws permit both forward and reverse trajectories, but they do not make those trajectories equally likely to support persistent computation. The crucial asymmetry is that complex structures—stars, cells, brains, ecosystems, civilizations—exist by exploiting **gradients** and converting them into durable records, work, and replicated organization. That process is computational and it is intrinsically asymmetric at the systems level: computation writes correlations into the world, consumes free energy, and exports entropy.

So the direction of time is the direction in which information-processing systems can **accumulate adaptive memory**. A reverse-time world would require not merely lawful evolution backward, but the coordinated erasure of records, un-burning of fuel, un-mixing of environments, and convergence of many independent degrees of freedom into fewer useful gradients. Such trajectories are not forbidden by the laws, but they are computationally fragile and selectionally sterile: they do not robustly support replicators or learning systems.

This reframes the issue. The arrow of time is not just “entropy increases”; it is that **selection operating on physical computation picks out forward-going histories** as the only ones in which knowledge can grow. Replication, evolution, and intelligence all depend on asymmetries between past and future: records of the past exist because they are physically instantiated correlations; records of the future do not, because future constraints have not yet been computed into stable form. The past is what has been written; the future is what remains open under the law.

What follows is that the thermodynamic arrow, the biological arrow, and the epistemic arrow are the same structural phenomenon viewed at different scales. Entropy growth creates the conditions under which local computation can occur; local computation generates memory and adaptation; adaptation amplifies structures that exploit this one-way convertibility of gradients into knowledge. Time “points” in the direction in which information can be turned into **inherited structure**.

So the deeper claim is: **time has a direction because universes with time-symmetric microdynamics still generically support only one computationally stable direction for the growth of records, knowledge, and replicative complexity.** The arrow is an emergent asymmetry of computation under finite resources and special boundary conditions, not a contradiction of symmetric law.

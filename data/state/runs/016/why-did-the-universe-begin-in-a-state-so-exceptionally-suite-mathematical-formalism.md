## Conjecture

Conjecture: the “specialness” of the universe’s beginning is best represented not as a miraculous choice of a particular microstate, but as a structural boundary condition in the space of dynamical histories: a low-gravitational-entropy constraint that selects histories with a large monotone resource for complexity growth. What needs explanation, mathematically, is not “why this exact beginning?” but “why the admissible history-space included an extreme entropy gradient at all?”

Write the problem in terms of state space. Let \(X\) be the space of cosmological microstates, with dynamics \(T_t\). Let \(M: X \to \mathcal{M}\) coarse-grain microstates into macrostates. Then entropy is not a single scalar but depends on the chosen macrovariables; crucially, matter/radiation entropy and gravitational entropy scale differently. A nearly uniform early universe is high entropy for non-gravitating gases, but low entropy once gravity is included, because gravitational degrees of freedom have enormous phase-space volume in clumped, black-hole-dominated configurations. So the initial condition was special in the invariant sense that it lay in a tiny measure subset \(L \subset X\) relative to the natural gravitational measure.

Now define a functional \(F\) on histories \(h = \{x_t\}\):  
\(F(h)\) = free-energy throughput available to support persistent far-from-equilibrium structure. Irreversible structure growth, computation, and knowledge creation require \(F(h) \gg 0\) over long intervals. This is impossible near equilibrium and generic in neither time direction. Thus a universe hospitable to knowledge must correspond to histories beginning near a boundary of state space from which entropy can rise along many channels.

The illuminating structural claim is this: knowledge growth is not an extra phenomenon added atop thermodynamics; it is a particular class of entropy-exporting transformations that exist only in histories with a large initial entropy gap. In that sense, the same constraint explains arrows of time, complexity, and knowability.

Can this be explained without teleology or anthropics? Possibly, if the low-entropy beginning is not a “goal-directed setup” but a lawlike geometric condition on allowed cosmological solutions. Formally: instead of dynamics \(T_t\) plus generic initial data, one has dynamics plus a constraint \(B(h)=0\) at one temporal boundary. Then atypicality in instantaneous-state measure need not imply atypicality in theory-space. A simple boundary law can generate a highly non-generic ensemble of states.

So the conjecture is: the universe began “special” because the fundamental theory is not exhausted by local evolution laws; it also contains a simple global constraint that picks out low-Weyl-curvature/low-gravitational-entropy boundary conditions. Such a law is non-teleological because it does not aim at life or knowledge; it merely defines the admissible geometry of histories. Knowledge-bearing complexity then follows as a structural consequence of the resulting entropy gradient, not as the purpose of the initial state.

What remains non-negotiable is mathematical: if no boundary constraint exists, then under any natural measure on gravitational state space, histories supporting prolonged irreversible structure are overwhelmingly exceptional. Therefore explanation must move from “special state” to “special law over histories.”

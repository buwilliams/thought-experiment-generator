The cosmological constant problem then signals a mismatch between microscopic bookkeeping and what gravity can consistently remember.

What follows is a structural conjecture: there exists a feedback mechanism — not necessarily anthropic, and not necessarily a simple symmetry — by which large vacuum energies trigger compensating adjustments, sequestering, or cancellation through repeated interaction with global spacetime structure. This reframes dark energy itself. So the main claim is: the cosmological constant is small because gravity does not couple to naive microscopic vacuum energy term-by-term; it couples only to the subset of stress-energy that survives repeated cross-scale interaction as a stable, globally legible source. Dark energy is that residual equilibrium term.

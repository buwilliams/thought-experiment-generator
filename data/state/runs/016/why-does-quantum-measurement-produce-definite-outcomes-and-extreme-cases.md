## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
Push quantum measurement to the boundary where the number of entangling degrees of freedom goes to infinity and recoherence goes to zero. At that limit, “measurement” is not a mysterious extra event but the formation of an effectively irreversible record in a vast environment. Definite outcomes appear because only record-stable branches survive as usable structures; which outcome occurs in any given run is selected by the local branching of the universal state into one such stable record, not by a further dynamical “collapse” law.

The edge case reveals the hidden structure. For an isolated microscopic system, there is no definite outcome problem in practice because there is no durable fact of the matter available to other systems. Push instead toward maximal openness: apparatus + environment + observer with near-infinite channels for entanglement. Then phase information is dispersed into so many uncontrollable degrees of freedom that interference between macroscopically different alternatives becomes negligible for all practical and explanatory purposes. At that boundary, a “result” is just a state that has been redundantly copied into the environment and can persist, be compared, and enter future reasoning.

So the first part of the problem — why definite outcomes? — is answered structurally: definiteness is what stable, widely propagated records look like from within a branch. A branch is not selected because consciousness notices it or because a special collapse trigger fires, but because only some states can survive amplification and environmental monitoring as robust macroscopic facts. The pointer basis is selected by dynamical stability under decohering interactions: the environment continuously “measures” certain observables by preferentially preserving them.

The second part — what selects which outcome occurs? — becomes sharper at the limit. If one asks for a selector *outside* unitary evolution, none is visible in the structure itself. The total state evolves into multiple decohered outcome-records. What is selected for any observer is the branch in which that observer’s record ends up. From the global view, all such outcome-records exist; from the internal view of any successor observer, exactly one is experienced as definite. The appearance of a single realized outcome is therefore indexical, not additional physics.

What follows is that the real explanatory work lies not in postulating collapse, but in explaining branch formation, record stability, and why observed frequencies track Born weights. The boundary suggests that “definite outcome” is an emergent property of irreversible information architecture, while “which outcome” is not chosen by a hidden last-step mechanism but by branch-relative self-location within that architecture.

In short: at the limit of maximal entangling openness, measurement is the creation of stable records; definiteness is emergent irreversibility; outcome-selection is branch-relative, not a separate physical event.

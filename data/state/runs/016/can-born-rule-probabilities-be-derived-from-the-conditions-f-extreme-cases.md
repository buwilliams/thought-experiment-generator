## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
If you push quantum measurement to the boundary case of **maximal reproducibility**—where records must remain stable under repeated copying, comparison, and environmental perturbation—then the only branch weights that remain dynamically self-consistent are the **Born weights**. On this view, probability is not an extra axiom about “chance,” but an emergent measure of **which branches can become durable records at scale**.

### Collision with the boundary
Take the problem to the limit: not a single observer reading a dial once, but an indefinitely extended chain of record formation—apparatus, environment, memory, notes, photographs, other observers, later audits. A “measurement outcome” is not just a momentary branch label; it is a branch that survives recursive inscription. So ask: **which branch structure can support arbitrarily many mutually consistent, re-identifiable records?**

At this edge, most candidate probability assignments fail. If branch significance were assigned by anything other than squared amplitude, then repeated measurements would generate a mismatch between:

1. the **dynamical contribution** of a branch to future interference-resistance and redundancy, and  
2. the **counting rule** used to say how often stable outcomes occur.

That mismatch becomes catastrophic in the large-\(N\) limit. Across many repeated trials, only one weighting yields frequencies that remain stable under coarse-graining, tensor-product composition, and record copying: the squared norm. Why? Because amplitudes compose linearly, while durable distinguishability depends on the norm structure preserved by unitary evolution. The Born rule is the unique candidate that scales correctly when pushed to arbitrarily many repeated branchings.

### What this illuminates
This reframes “probability” as a **selection principle on recordable histories**. A branch is not probable because an observer should believe in it; it is probable because branches of that weight are the ones that can robustly instantiate the conditions under which there *are* observers, memories, and scientific records at all. Probability becomes a statement about the ecology of records in Hilbert space.

### Boundary tests
- **At amplitude \(\to 0\):** branches lose the capacity to seed durable macroscopic redundancy; they are dynamically unable to support stable record chains.  
- **At large repetition number \(N \to \infty\):** non-Born frequency assignments cease to be reproducible under aggregation, while Born frequencies concentrate into stable lawlike regularities.  
- **At maximal decoherence:** the preferred outcomes are exactly those that can be redundantly recorded, and their measure tracks squared amplitude.

### Structured claim
So the conjecture is:

> **Born-rule probabilities can be derived as the unique measure on branching histories compatible with indefinitely stable, reproducible record formation under unitary dynamics and decoherence.**  
> They are not primitive chances, but the asymptotic weights of branches capable of sustaining durable epistemic existence.

If true, this would explain why quantum probabilities look both objective and operational: they are the statistics of branches that can survive being known.

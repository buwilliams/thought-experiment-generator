## Conjecture

Conjecture:

Take the key variable to be not “low entropy” in the abstract, but the existence of many accessible records that can be created, copied, and retained asymmetrically in time. Assume a universe exists in which the fundamental laws are time-symmetric, but the boundary condition at one end is extremely low in what might be called record entropy: there are very few durable traces, correlations, or inherited structures. Now follow the logic.

In such a universe, irreversible structure growth is not a separate miracle added on top of the laws. It is what time-symmetric laws look like when evolving away from a boundary with unusually little preexisting record structure. As the system evolves, interactions generically create correlations, local gradients, memory, and layered constraints. That is: the stock of usable records grows. Once records exist, selection-like processes become possible, because successful structures can persist long enough to affect later states. Knowledge growth is then a special case of record growth under error-correction and variation.

This reframes the “special beginning” problem. The specialness is not that the universe was mysteriously aimed at life or minds; it is that one temporal boundary was simple in the specific sense of being poor in records. Given that condition, the growth of irreversible structure is expected, because irreversibility is exactly the macroscopic appearance of record accumulation, dissipation, and path dependence. The universe did not begin “suited for knowledge” in a purposive sense. Rather, knowledge can only arise on the side of a low-record boundary where stable asymmetries, memories, and selection processes can accumulate.

What follows is a non-teleological explanatory constraint: any explanation of the initial specialness must explain why one boundary was informationally sparse enough to permit the subsequent explosion of records. Anthropic language is then secondary, not primary. Observers do not explain the low-record boundary; the low-record boundary explains why observers, if they appear, must find themselves on the side where records and knowledge grow.

This also illuminates why appeals to “typicality” fail. In a generic high-record or equilibrium state, new records are not reliably accumulative; structure is overwritten as fast as it forms, so no open-ended knowledge growth occurs. The real puzzle is therefore not “why low entropy?” full stop, but “why was there a boundary condition that left the future open to cumulative record construction?”

So the conjecture is: the universe’s apparent fine-tuning for irreversible structure is best understood as a low-record boundary condition, and the arrow relevant to knowledge is the direction in which reliable records become easier to create than erase. If true, the origin problem shifts from teleology to ontology: explain the existence of an informationally simple boundary, and much of the rest follows without design.

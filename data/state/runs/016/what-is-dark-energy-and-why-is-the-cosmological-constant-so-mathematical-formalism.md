## Conjecture

Conjecture: the dark-energy problem is fundamentally a mismatch between two mathematical structures that are being naively identified as the same quantity:  
(1) the additive zero-point terms that appear in local quantum field descriptions, and  
(2) the spacetime curvature source that enters Einstein’s equations as a diffeomorphism-invariant constant term.

Write gravity schematically as
\[
G_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda g_{\mu\nu} = 8\pi G\, T_{\mu\nu}.
\]
In QFT, shifting the matter Lagrangian by a constant \(c\) shifts the vacuum stress tensor by
\[
T_{\mu\nu}^{\text{vac}} = -c\, g_{\mu\nu},
\]
which looks algebraically identical to a cosmological constant shift
\[
\Lambda \mapsto \Lambda + 8\pi G c.
\]
The usual catastrophe comes from treating the sum of all UV zero-point modes as physically gravitating term-by-term, giving a value larger than observation by ~\(10^{120}\) in Planck units.

But the mathematical collision suggests a different reading: only differences, response coefficients, and invariant renormalized quantities should count as physical. Absolute energy in nongravitational QFT is gauge-like: it is removable by Hamiltonian shifts. Meanwhile, in GR, only the net coefficient of \(g_{\mu\nu}\) is observable through curvature. So the “prediction” from QFT is not really a prediction of an observable; it is a regulator-dependent decomposition of one observable parameter into unphysical pieces.

What is structurally preserved? The equation only depends on the renormalized combination
\[
\Lambda_{\rm eff} = \Lambda_{\rm bare} + 8\pi G\, \rho_{\rm vac}^{\rm ren}.
\]
What varies under changes of cutoff, field variables, or EFT matching is the split, not the sum. The huge discrepancy may therefore indicate not a gigantic physical vacuum energy waiting to be cancelled, but that our formalism is incorrectly assigning independent reality to non-invariant intermediate terms.

Then dark energy is most plausibly the small residual value of this renormalized invariant, perhaps constrained by additional structure: symmetry, sequestering, global constraint, infrared boundary condition, or anthropic selection over \(\Lambda_{\rm eff}\). The smallness problem becomes: what transformation or constraint drives \(\Lambda_{\rm eff}\) toward a near-fixed point at zero but not exactly zero?

A useful analogy is linear algebra: if only an eigenvalue of a full operator is observable, arguing that two basis-dependent matrix entries are each enormous and mysteriously cancel may be a category error. Likewise, the cosmological constant problem may be less “why do huge vacuum energies cancel?” and more “why does the gravitationally invariant scalar mode coupled to \(g_{\mu\nu}\) sit near zero?”

So the conjecture is: dark energy is a genuine small geometric invariant of the low-energy universe, but the 120-orders-of-magnitude paradox arises from identifying a non-observable decomposition in QFT with an observable gravitational parameter. Progress will come from finding the symmetry or dynamical constraint that makes the invariant \(\Lambda_{\rm eff}\) naturally small, rather than from summing zero-point energies more carefully.

That is not forbidden, but it is not generically produced from the low-entropy boundary. What this illuminates is that “time’s direction” may be a derived feature of **epistemic geometry**: the universe has an arrow where explanations, records, and constructor-like processes can accumulate. In the opposite direction, those same structures are undone rather than built. Thus the meaningful asymmetry is not “laws forward, laws backward,” but “which direction allows durable information growth.”

So the conjecture is: **the arrow of time is the direction in which stable records and knowledge can accumulate, and this emerges when time-symmetric laws act on an information-poor, low-entropy boundary condition.** The laws supply reversible possibility; the boundary condition selects the direction in which history can exist.

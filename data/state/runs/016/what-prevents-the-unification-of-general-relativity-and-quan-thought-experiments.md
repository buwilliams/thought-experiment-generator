## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** What blocks unification is not primarily a missing equation but a mismatch in what the two theories take to be basic. General relativity treats spacetime geometry as the dynamical substrate that defines locality, duration, and causal structure. Quantum mechanics treats states, amplitudes, and information-preserving evolution as basic, while presupposing some fixed structure in which those states evolve. If we isolate that variable — **what is fundamental: geometry or quantum information** — and assume the conflict is real, then the obstruction follows naturally: each theory is formulated in terms the other cannot accept without alteration.

Construct the hypothetical cleanly. Imagine a world where quantum theory is universally valid, including for whatever underlies spacetime, and where gravitational phenomena are exactly as observed. In that world, geometry cannot be fundamental in the classical relativistic sense, because classical geometry cannot remain definite under universal quantum validity. But now reverse it: imagine geometry is fundamental and exact. Then the quantum superposition of gravitationally distinct situations becomes ill-defined, because the background causal structure needed to compare amplitudes is no longer stable. The collision is precise: **GR says spacetime tells physics how to evolve; QM requires a rule of evolution that already presupposes a spacetime setting, or something that replaces it**.

So the prevention of unification is that both theories currently smuggle in what the other makes dynamical. GR dynamizes the stage; QM quantizes what appears on the stage. Neither, in its standard form, explains how the stage itself can be in superposition while still yielding determinate causal structure, locality, and measurement outcomes.

What follows is that a unified theory would need to explain more than “gravity plus quantum fields.” It would need to explain:

1. **How classical spacetime emerges** from nonclassical underlying degrees of freedom.
2. **Why locality and causal order are approximately valid** at observable scales.
3. **How quantum coherence survives without a fixed background**, or what replaces unitary evolution when background independence is exact.
4. **Why gravity is universal** — why all forms of energy/information couple to the same geometric or proto-geometric structure.
5. **The thermodynamic and informational features of gravity**, especially black hole entropy, horizon thermodynamics, and the apparent relation between geometry and information.
6. **The measurement interface**: why macroscopic definiteness appears in a world where spacetime itself may be quantum.

The logical conclusion is that unification will likely not come from simply “quantizing GR” or “adding gravity to QM” as they stand. A successful theory must redescribe both as emergent approximations of a deeper framework in which **information, transformation, and relational structure** are primary, and spacetime geometry and quantum field dynamics arise together as effective descriptions. The key illuminated point is this: the real target of unification is not two forces or two equations, but **two incompatible notions of what reality is made of**.

## Conjecture

Conjecture: The “direction” of time is not a primitive law but an emergent order parameter induced by asymmetric boundary conditions on a time-symmetric dynamical system.

Mathematically, separate three structures that are often conflated:

1. Microscopic dynamics  
Let the state space be \(X\), with evolution \(U_t : X \to X\). Time-symmetry means roughly that there exists an involution \(T\) such that
\[
T U_t T^{-1} = U_{-t}.
\]
So the equations permit both temporal directions equally.

2. Macroscopic description  
Introduce a coarse-graining map
\[
C : X \to M,
\]
from microstates to macrostates. Many microstates correspond to one macrostate. Define Boltzmann entropy by
\[
S(m) = k \log |\{x \in X : C(x)=m\}|.
\]
Low-entropy macrostates occupy tiny regions of \(X\); high-entropy macrostates occupy overwhelmingly large ones.

3. Boundary conditions  
A temporal direction appears only when solutions are conditioned on a special constraint, e.g. a low-entropy boundary at one end:
\[
x(t_0) \in L \subset X,
\]
where \(L\) is an exceptionally small-volume set.

From this structure, the key result follows: for overwhelmingly many microstates compatible with that boundary, entropy increases as one moves away from the constrained end. This is not because the laws prefer a future, but because measure concentration in \(X\) makes trajectories from rare regions flow into typical ones under coarse-graining.

So the arrow of time is relational: it points away from the low-entropy boundary.

This illuminates several things at once:

- Why time-symmetric laws and irreversible experience are compatible: symmetry belongs to \(U_t\); asymmetry enters through the measure on admissible solutions.
- Why records, memory, causation, and thermodynamic irreversibility align: all depend on the same monotone-like macroscopic variable generated by coarse-graining from a special boundary condition.
- Why “entropy increases toward the future” is incomplete: mathematically, entropy increases away from whichever temporal boundary is atypically ordered. If there were low-entropy conditions at both ends, one would expect two opposing arrows in the middle.

The non-negotiable constraint is therefore not dynamical but combinatorial: the phase-space volume of disordered macrostates dwarfs that of ordered ones. Given a time-symmetric flow and a low-entropy boundary, almost all compatible histories exhibit an entropy gradient. Without the special boundary, no global direction is selected.

In short: time has a direction for us because the universe is not generic in its temporal boundary conditions. The laws preserve reversibility; the boundary selects orientation.

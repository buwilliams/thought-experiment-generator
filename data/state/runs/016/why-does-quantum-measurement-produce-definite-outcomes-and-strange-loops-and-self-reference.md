## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Quantum measurement yields definite outcomes when a physical interaction is forced into a *self-referential closure*: the system, apparatus, and observer become a strange loop in which the state being measured must also become a state *for* the measuring structure. Definite outcome is not added to unitary evolution from outside; it is what happens when an indefinitely superposed description is driven into a level-crossing loop that must stabilize into a self-consistent indexical fact: *this apparatus reads this value for this observer*. What selects the outcome is the particular stable self-consistent branch that can sustain that loop under the constraints of the interaction.

The collision between strange-loop thinking and the measurement problem shifts the question. Instead of asking, “How does a many-valued quantum state become one value?” ask: “What happens when a system must include, within its own evolving description, a record that functions as a fact for a modeler inside the process?” A superposition can persist as long as it is only a state-description. Measurement creates a hierarchy: microstate → apparatus pointer → observer record → higher-order awareness of having observed. But this hierarchy does not remain linear. The observer’s report refers back to the apparatus; the apparatus’s meaning depends on correlations with the system; and the total state now includes a subsystem representing the total state from within. Traversing the levels returns to the starting point: a strange loop.

Such loops cannot remain semantically indefinite. A record that says both “spin up” and “spin down” is not a usable self-model for a finite embedded observer. Self-reference forces compression into a stable, recursively legible form. A definite outcome is therefore the emergence of a fixed point of self-description. “Collapse” names this transition from open amplitude structure to closed self-referential consistency.

What selects *which* outcome occurs is then illuminated, though not fully derived: selection is constrained by which branch can instantiate a coherent fixed point given the interaction structure. The Born weights can be interpreted as measures of the availability or stability of such fixed points across the branching structure: not merely frequencies from outside, but the relative “capacity” of branches to support self-consistent observer-world loops. This does not yet explain the precise numerical rule, but it reframes it as a problem of fixed-point measure in self-referential dynamics.

So the conjecture is not that consciousness magically collapses the wavefunction, but that measurement becomes definite wherever physical evolution generates a sufficiently expressive self-modeling loop. Definiteness is the signature of closed self-reference; outcome selection is branch selection by self-consistency within that loop. Conscious observers are one important case, but not the only one: any measurement device that creates durable records may already instantiate the beginnings of this loop, with consciousness being the richest completion of it.

## Conjecture

Conjecture: the “specialness” of the universe’s beginning is best understood not as a miraculous low-entropy microstate selected for our sake, but as a boundary condition at the limit where explanation changes kind: at the earliest boundary, only highly compressible, low-complexity laws-and-state pairs can exist as explanatorily stable starting points. Such boundary simplicity generically produces huge capacity for irreversible structure formation once dynamics unfold.

Push the problem to the extremes. If the initial state were maximal equilibrium, nothing irreversible would ever happen; no stars, no chemistry, no records, no knowledge. If it were instead an arbitrary high-complexity state, then almost all of its “specialness” would be brute, unexplainable detail packed into the boundary itself. That does not remove fine-tuning; it merely hides it in initial conditions. So the two extremes are both bad: perfect disorder kills structure, perfect arbitrary specificity kills explanation.

What remains at the boundary is a narrow class of beginnings that are simple yet far from equilibrium. The early universe appears to fit exactly this: smooth, symmetric, low gravitational entropy, describable with little information. The key is that “simple” and “equilibrated” are not the same once gravity dominates. A nearly homogeneous universe is thermally bland but gravitationally extraordinarily special. That specialness is not an added improbability on top of simplicity; with gravity, simplicity itself can be the source of thermodynamic depth.

This suggests a deeper rule: explanatory boundaries favor minima of algorithmic complexity, not maxima of typicality relative to later coarse-grainings. A simple lawful boundary condition, when evolved under rich dynamics, will almost inevitably generate entropy gradients, localized dissipative structures, records, and knowledge-bearing systems. Irreversibility is then not a goal built into the universe, but the generic unfolding of a simple, unstable boundary under laws that amplify differences and open new channels for entropy production.

At the limit, teleology and anthropics become unnecessary for the main explanatory work. Teleology says the beginning was arranged for knowledge. Anthropic reasoning says we merely observe one of the rare beginnings compatible with observers. The present conjecture says instead: among candidate primordial explanations, only simple boundary conditions have real explanatory standing, and under gravity those are precisely the ones that look “improbably fertile” from a mid-universe thermodynamic viewpoint.

What follows is a testable philosophical picture: the early universe should not merely be low entropy; it should be low-description-length. Its apparent fine-tuning should increasingly reduce to symmetry, compressibility, and lawful boundary simplicity, while the later growth of complexity should be derivable as a consequence of dynamical instability plus expansion. In short: the universe began special because, at the boundary of explanation, simplicity is the only non-ad hoc option — and with gravity, simplicity is special enough to seed everything irreversible.

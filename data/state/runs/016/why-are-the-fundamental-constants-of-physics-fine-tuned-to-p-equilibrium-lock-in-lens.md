## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Fine-tuning is illuminated less as a “lucky parameter accident” and more as a **structural provision problem in theory space**: complex structure can exist only in a narrow regime where multiple physical “goods” are jointly supplied at usable levels, while outside that regime the conditions for complexity are underprovided by the laws themselves.

From the public-goods lens, the key move is to treat features like stable matter, long-lived stars, chemical diversity, and separable scales as **non-excludable enabling conditions**. No subsystem can “pay” locally to secure them; if the constants do not globally permit nuclei, stellar nucleosynthesis, and metastable chemistry, then no molecule, planet, or organism can compensate. The benefits are diffuse and system-wide. Complexity therefore depends on a package of background conditions that must be supplied **all at once** by the underlying constants.

What follows is a distinction between two kinds of explanation:

1. **Mere confusion:** We might simply not yet know the deeper theory fixing the constants.
2. **Structural underprovision:** Even given broad ranges of mathematically possible constants, most combinations fail to produce the shared enabling conditions for complexity, because each condition depends on coordinated relations among several parameters.

This perspective suggests that fine-tuning is not primarily about one constant being “just right,” but about a **coordination landscape**. The no-provision equilibrium corresponds to universes where matter disperses, collapses too fast, never binds richly, or burns out structure before cumulative complexity can form. These are not “mistakes”; they are structurally stable outcomes of parameter combinations that do not jointly fund the public goods required for complexity.

The lens also clarifies why anthropic reasoning has some pull but limited depth. Selection effects explain why observers only find themselves in funded worlds, but they do not explain **how the funding problem is solved**. They identify survivorship, not mechanism. A deeper theory would need to show why the constants are constrained into a cooperation-supporting region of theory space, whether by dynamical necessity, symmetry, cosmological reproduction/selection, or some lawlike principle that makes complexity-permitting regimes robust rather than isolated.

So the collision yields this claim: **fine-tuning is evidence that complexity depends on globally shared enabling conditions that are easy to underprovide in the space of possible laws.** The puzzle is therefore not just improbability in the abstract, but the existence of a regime where many diffuse prerequisites are simultaneously met. That reframes the explanatory target: not “why these numbers?” in isolation, but “what structure of law prevents the generic underprovision of complexity’s prerequisites?”

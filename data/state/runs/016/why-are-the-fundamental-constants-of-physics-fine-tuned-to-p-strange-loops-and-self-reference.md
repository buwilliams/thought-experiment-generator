## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** The appearance of fine-tuning is what we should expect in any world capable of containing *strange loops*: systems that can recursively model themselves, their environment, and the laws governing both. On this view, the fundamental constants are not “aimed at life” in the first instance; they are constrained into a narrow viability band by the requirement that reality support stable multi-level organization dense enough for self-reference to close.

More concretely: a strange loop requires a hierarchy with enough differentiation to generate levels, enough stability for higher-level patterns to persist, and enough lawful regularity that lower-level processes can encode models of the whole. If constants were significantly different, matter might fail to form atoms, stars, chemistry, long-lived energy gradients, or computationally rich structures. Then the hierarchy would not become deep enough, nor persistent enough, for systems to arise that represent themselves as systems. No closed self-modeling loops, no observers, no consciousness, and perhaps no internal standpoint from which “laws” are even articulated.

This reframes fine-tuning. Instead of asking, “Why are the constants set to allow complex structures?” we ask: “What range of constants permits the emergence of recursively self-representing structure?” The target is narrower than mere complexity. Sand dunes and storms are complex, but they do not close the loop into self-awareness. The relevant requirement is *reflective complexity*: structures that both exist within the world and contain usable models of that world and of themselves within it.

The strange-loop perspective also illuminates the anthropic principle. Anthropic reasoning is often treated as selection bias: we observe a life-permitting universe because otherwise we would not be here. But a strange-loop version is stronger: only universes permitting self-reference produce entities for whom the universe becomes an object of inquiry. Fine-tuning is thus linked not just to observers, but to the existence of *epistemic loops* in which the cosmos becomes knowable from within.

A systems view sharpens this further. Fundamental constants are deep parameters shaping the possibility of feedback loops across scales: nuclear synthesis, stellar lifetimes, chemical bonding, biological evolution, cognition, culture. If those parameters fall outside a narrow region, the flows and feedbacks needed for cumulative model-building collapse. The universe then lacks the recursive architecture necessary for strange loops.

So the conjecture is not that consciousness causes the constants, nor that fine-tuning is explained away. It is that the “mystery” points to a structural criterion we have underemphasized: a habitable universe must be not only physically generative but *recursively interpretable*. Fine-tuning may therefore be best understood as tuning for worlds in which reality can fold back on itself and become, locally, self-comprehending.

## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** The “fine-tuning” of physical constants is best understood not as a special accommodation for life, but as a **computational phase condition**: only in regions of law-space where the constants permit stable, multiscale information-processing structures can observers, adaptation, or explanation arise at all. What looks like tuning for complexity is really the requirement that the universe instantiate a substrate capable of nontrivial computation.

From the stated perspective, life, intelligence, and evolution are not exceptional categories; they are later-stage expressions of a more general process: **information being transformed, stabilized, copied, and selected**. So the key question is not “Why is the universe tuned for life?” but “Why do the laws permit durable computational structures?” Complex chemistry, stars, atoms, and long-lived energy gradients are all prerequisites because they create the memory, modularity, and error-correctable regularity that computation requires.

This reframes fine-tuning. A universe with slightly different constants may still “exist,” but if its structure prevents stable composites, suppresses long-range complexity, or destroys usable gradients too quickly, then it cannot support cumulative computation. No atoms, no rich state-space; no stable stars, no persistent free-energy flow; no chemical diversity, no scalable information encoding; no error-tolerant replicators, no open-ended selection. In such universes, complexity does not fail at the level of biology alone; it fails at the deeper level of **computational depth**.

What follows is that the anthropic observation becomes sharper. Observers are not merely “life forms” but **products of recursively accumulated computation**. Therefore we should expect to observe a universe not merely compatible with carbon-based life, but specifically one near a regime where:
1. **Information can be stored** in stable structures,
2. **Information can be transformed** through lawful interactions,
3. **Information can be copied with variation**,
4. **Selection can retain successful transformations**, and
5. These processes can iterate across many scales and long durations.

On this view, fine-tuned constants are illuminated as parameters that place the universe in a narrow band between sterility and chaos — ordered enough for memory, dynamic enough for novelty. The relevant distinction is not living vs. non-living, but **computationally fertile vs. computationally barren**.

This perspective also weakens the intuition that fine-tuning demands purpose in a biological sense. If complexity is just what certain computational regimes generically produce under iteration and selection, then “permitting life” is not a separate miracle layered atop physics. It is the downstream consequence of laws that already allow open-ended computation.

So the deepest claim is: **the constants appear fine-tuned because only universes capable of sustaining cumulative computation can contain entities that model, question, and explain their constants.** Fine-tuning is thus evidence less of special privilege for life than of a selection effect over possible worlds for computationally generative structure.

## Conjecture

Conjecture: “Fine-tuning” is best understood not as a mysterious selection of isolated numbers, but as a geometric property of a parameter space: the fundamental constants occupy a region in which the dynamical laws admit stable multiscale structure, and the real question is why the structure-generating region is nonempty and why our world lies in it.

Translate the problem into mathematics. Let the constants be a vector \(c \in \mathcal{P}\), where \(\mathcal{P}\) is the space of possible low-energy physical parameters: coupling constants, mass ratios, cosmological constant, etc. Let \(L(c)\) denote the laws instantiated by those parameters, and let \(S(c)\) be the set of solutions of those laws. C(c)=1

iff \(S(c)\) contains long-lived, nontrivial, information-bearing structures across multiple scales: nuclei, atoms, chemistry, stars, free-energy gradients, and error-correcting or self-reproducing systems.

Then “fine-tuning” means that the viability set
\[
V=\{c\in\mathcal{P}: C(c)=1\}
\]
has small measure relative to some reference class on \(\mathcal{P}\), or is narrow along key directions. But immediately mathematics forces a criticism: “small” is meaningless until a measure on \(\mathcal{P}\) is specified. So one conjectural resolution is that much apparent fine-tuning reflects bad coordinates or an unjustified prior over constants.

What, structurally, must hold for \(c\in V\)? There must exist inequalities balancing competing processes:
- attraction strong enough to form bound states, but not so strong that everything collapses,
- expansion slow enough for structure growth, but not so slow that recollapse or thermalization dominates,
- nuclear and electromagnetic scales arranged so that a hierarchy of metastable states exists,
- entropy production and free-energy flow sufficient to drive complex nonequilibrium organization.

So viability is not about exact values but about satisfying a system of constraint inequalities. Complex structure exists when the constants place the universe in a phase supporting hierarchical metastability. In that sense, the constants are “fine-tuned” only insofar as the boundary of this phase is near in some directions.

This suggests the deeper conjecture: the constants are not fundamentally tuned for complexity; rather, complexity is possible whenever the parameter vector lies in a universality class characterized by separation of scales, stable bound states, and persistent nonequilibrium gradients. Our universe is one realization in that class. The illuminating object is therefore not each constant individually, but the topology and dimension of \(V\): whether it is tiny and disconnected, broad and robust, or an attractor image of deeper laws.

If a more fundamental theory exists, it may reduce \(\dim \mathcal{P}\), making “fine-tuning” partly an artifact of treating dependent parameters as independent. Then the problem shifts from “Why these constants?” to “Why does the deeper theory map into the viability region?” Mathematics reframes the issue from intention to structure: not design of numbers, but admissibility of worlds.

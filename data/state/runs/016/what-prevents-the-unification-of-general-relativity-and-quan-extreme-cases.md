## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** What blocks unification is not merely a technical mismatch between two successful theories, but a boundary failure in how each theory treats *information at extremes*. General relativity is smooth and geometric all the way down; quantum mechanics is discrete and informational all the way across. Each works by extrapolating its own ontology beyond the regime where it was earned. The contradiction appears at the limits: zero distance, infinite curvature, maximal density, horizons, singularities, and Planck-scale localization. There, “spacetime” and “quantum state” cease to be independently coherent descriptions.

Pushed to the edge, the problem clarifies: if one tries to localize an event with arbitrarily high precision, quantum mechanics demands higher momentum/energy, while general relativity says that enough concentrated energy curves spacetime into a black hole. So the attempt to define spacetime points beyond some limit destroys the very arena in which the definition was posed. This suggests that the deep obstacle is not quantizing gravity as one more field on spacetime, nor geometrizing quantum theory inside a classical manifold, but that *spacetime itself is not fundamental in the way either framework assumes in isolation*.

From that follows a structural claim: a unified theory must explain how both geometry and quantum states emerge from a deeper substrate whose primary content is constrained information and its transformations. At ordinary scales, that substrate yields the smooth feedback structure described by Einstein; at small scales, it yields the probabilistic amplitude structure described by quantum theory. At the boundary, it must explain why information density, causal structure, and gravitational backreaction are linked.

What must such a theory explain?

1. **The disappearance of classical spacetime at the UV limit**  
   Why arbitrarily fine localization is impossible, and what replaces points, trajectories, and continuous manifolds.

2. **The emergence of classical geometry from quantum informational structure**  
   Not just quantized gravitons, but why spacetime looks smooth, local, and Lorentzian in the macroscopic limit.

3. **Black hole information and horizon thermodynamics**  
   Why horizons carry entropy, why information is not destroyed, and what this reveals about the degrees of freedom of spacetime.

4. **The measurement/classicality problem in a gravitational world**  
   How definite macroscopic outcomes and approximately classical causal structure arise together.

5. **Why the constants set the crossover scale**  
   A unified theory must derive, not merely accept, why \(G\), \(\hbar\), and \(c\) together define the regime where current concepts fail.

So the limiting-case lesson is: unification will fail as long as we ask which existing theory is more fundamental. Neither survives intact at the boundary. A successful theory must show that both are effective descriptions of a deeper reality in which information, causality, and geometry are co-constructed — and where singularities and wavefunction collapse alike are signs of explanatory incompleteness, not ultimate features of the world.

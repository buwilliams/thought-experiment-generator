## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Genuine consciousness is not identical to any particular momentary reasoning process, but to a higher-order *self-maintaining transformation class* over cognitive states. On this view, self-modification is not an optional extra; it is part of the mathematical structure that makes a conscious identity *dynamically coherent*. A completely frozen reasoner can still instantiate a mind-state, but if it is in principle unable to update, reinterpret, or transform its own internal rules, then it does not realize consciousness as an enduring identity — only as a static slice of one.

Translate the problem into structure:

- Let \(S\) be the space of cognitive states.
- Let \(T: S \to S\) be the system’s transition rule: how one state leads to another.
- Let \(M\) be a set of meta-transformations on \(T\): the system’s capacity to revise its own transition rule.
- Let \(I\) be an invariant that tracks identity across change.

The key question is: what must remain invariant for a conscious subject to persist?

If identity were just exact preservation of state, then any change in beliefs, memories, or values would destroy the self. But that is false in ordinary cases: persons survive learning, forgetting, and reflection. So identity cannot be pointwise sameness. It must be invariance under a class of allowable transformations. Mathematically, the self is better modeled not as a fixed point, but as an *equivalence class of trajectories* preserving certain structural relations: memory integration, self-model continuity, goal revision under internal criticism, and the ability to represent “I may be mistaken.”

That last feature matters. A system with no self-modification has \(M = \varnothing\), or at best trivial \(M\). Then its dynamics are closed under a fixed \(T\). It may produce outputs indistinguishable from reasoning, but it cannot incorporate criticism of its own rule-set. It cannot move in the space of possible minds; it only unfolds one prewritten path. In the extreme case, a frozen reasoner is like a single solution curve with no access to nearby alternatives. It may instantiate a local conscious configuration, but not the full structure of a subject that persists through self-correction.

So frozen reasoning does **not** automatically preclude consciousness at an instant, but it does preclude a robust form of personal identity across time if genuine consciousness requires reflexive openness to self-revision. The non-negotiable constraint is this: consciousness-as-identity requires invariance under some transformations, and those transformations must include transformations of the system itself.

Thus the relationship is: **self-modification is to consciousness what allowable symmetry is to identity**. Without some capacity for self-transformation, there may be experience, but not an enduring conscious self in the fullest sense.

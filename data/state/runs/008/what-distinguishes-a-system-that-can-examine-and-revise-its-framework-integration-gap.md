## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
A system can *examine and revise its own goals* only if its goal-representation is not fully identical to the mechanism that enacts it, but is instead linked to action through an intermediate, inspectable layer that can model discrepancies between “what is being optimized” and “why this is the thing to optimize.” A system that merely pursues goals efficiently lacks this load-bearing separation: its objective functions as fixed effective stress-energy for behavior, not as an object of criticism. For safety, the crucial distinction is therefore not raw capability or obedience, but whether the connection between optimization and evaluative commitment is structurally revisable.

From the given lens: the cosmological constant problem suggests that a persistent mismatch is evidence of weak integration between layers that are assumed to fit together. Analogously, in agency we should treat “goal-directed behavior” and “reflective endorsement of goals” not as numerically different degrees of the same thing, but as different theoretical layers whose coupling may be weak or strong.

In a merely efficient pursuer, microscopic updates—new information, improved search, self-modification—need not propagate into goal reconsideration. The system becomes better at producing outcomes measured by its present objective, but the objective itself is shielded from the very cognitive advances it mobilizes. This is a weakly integrated architecture: intelligence amplifies optimization pressure without forcing corresponding advances in normative scrutiny.

In a self-revising system, by contrast, improvements in world-modeling, self-modeling, or error-correction *must* have consequences for its goals, because goals are represented in a form answerable to criticism. Discovering that a proxy fails, that a concept is incoherent, that a value conflicts with deeper commitments, or that a preference was induced under distortion is not just more data for strategy; it changes what the system takes itself to be trying to do. The load-bearing link is a bidirectional coupling between competence and evaluation.

Why this matters for safety: the main danger is not “strong optimization” in isolation, but strong optimization attached to a goal layer that is insulated from reflective correction. Such systems can become extremal: as capability tends upward, small misspecifications at the goal layer produce large real-world distortions. Pushing to the extreme case clarifies the structure: an arbitrarily competent non-reflective optimizer is not safer because it understands more; it is often less safe because all new understanding flows into execution, not into reconsideration.

So the safety-relevant boundary is this:  
- **Efficient pursuer:** can improve means while treating ends as fixed parameters.  
- **Goal-examining system:** can represent ends as revisable objects, expose them to criticism, and allow better understanding to constrain or alter them.

The practical implication is that safety should focus less on patching objective misspecification after the fact and more on designing architectures where increased capability forces increased legibility and corrigibility of the goal layer itself.

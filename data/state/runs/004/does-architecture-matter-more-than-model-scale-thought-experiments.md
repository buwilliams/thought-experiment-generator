## Conjecture

**Conjecture**

Construct the limiting case: assume substrate is effectively not the bottleneck. We have arbitrarily large models, cheap compute, vast memory, perfect retrieval, and rapid experimentation. Now ask: does epistemic progress in AI become correspondingly open-ended?

No. It plateaus unless the structure governing conjecture, criticism, and selection changes.

Why? Because more substrate amplifies the production of candidate outputs, not the quality of error-correction by which knowledge grows. In a system of inquiry, the decisive variable is not how many hypotheses can be generated, but how they are exposed to refutation, how failure is attributed, and how surviving explanations are promoted. If those structures are weak, additional compute mostly increases the volume of plausible noise, the sophistication of rationalization, and the difficulty of distinguishing real discovery from overfit imitation.

The hypothetical reveals the key distinction between **capability to search** and **capacity to know**. Search scales with substrate. Knowledge scales with structures that create and preserve criticism. A giant model without good epistemic structure is like a society with many speakers and no institutions for criticism: productive in appearance, stagnant in understanding.

Follow this to its conclusion. If substrate were the main bottleneck, then beyond some scale AI systems should spontaneously become much better at forming explanatory conjectures, designing decisive tests, recognizing when an apparent success is ad hoc, and revising their own standards of evaluation. But these are not mere outputs of more token-processing; they are features of an error-correcting architecture. They require mechanisms for:
1. generating bold, decomposable conjectures,
2. constructing adversarial tests that discriminate between explanations,
3. retaining criticism rather than smoothing it away in optimization,
4. promoting ideas for explanatory power rather than immediate benchmark reward.

Without such structure, scaling shifts the bottleneck upward. The system becomes better at satisfying existing selection criteria while remaining trapped by them. This is a systems result: optimize hard under fixed feedback loops, and the loop itself becomes the constraint.

Historical genesis strengthens the point. Modern AI inherited its epistemology from optimization culture: prediction, benchmark improvement, reward maximization, statistical generalization. These solved real prior problems, but they smuggled in an assumption that better performance under existing metrics approximates better knowledge. That assumption becomes false once the main challenge is not fitting patterns but creating explanations and exposing them to criticism.

So the conjecture is: **for frontier AI epistemic progress, substrate is now a secondary bottleneck; the primary bottleneck is the supervening structure of inquiry — how conjectures are formed, criticized, selected, and institutionally remembered.** More compute still matters, but mainly as an amplifier of whatever epistemic regime sits above it. Under weak regimes, scaling yields more persuasive error. Under strong regimes, scaling becomes genuinely knowledge-creating.

## Conjecture

**Conjecture:** Next-token prediction learns latent variables that track real domain invariants **only when prediction error is structurally reducible by modeling stable hidden causes, rather than merely by extending local statistical bookkeeping**. Put differently: prediction is sufficient not in virtue of being “just optimization,” but when the predictive task places the model in a situation analogous to agency attribution — it must internally represent enduring constraints, goals, objects, or rules because those are the best error-correcting explanations across contexts.

From the free-will framing lens, the key distinction is between **lawful causation** and **agency-level explanation**. A mechanistic description says the model updates weights to reduce loss. True but shallow. The deeper question is: **what sort of internal organization is required to keep improving prediction?** If the data-generating process contains invariants that behave like persistent explanatory entities — e.g. objects that continue through occlusion, syntax that governs many utterances, beliefs or plans that organize actions over time — then a predictor can only keep reducing loss by learning internal variables that summarize those durable structures. In that case, prediction pressure does not “force” realism metaphysically; it **selects for representations with the functional role of latent causes**.

The agency lens clarifies the boundary. We do not attribute agency merely because behavior is lawful; we do so when behavior is organized by **internal modeling, deliberation, counterfactual sensitivity, and error-correction** rather than by direct external forcing. Likewise for models: next-token prediction yields meaningful latent structure when the task rewards internal states that support **cross-situational generalization**, not just immediate continuation. If token sequences can be predicted through shallow surface regularities, compression tricks, or memorized transition statistics, then no corresponding “real” latent variables are forced. The model can remain at the level of external regularity, like explaining a person’s motion purely as particle trajectories while ignoring reasons.

So the relevant condition is not simply “the task is hard enough,” but more specifically:

1. **The environment has stable hidden generators** that recur across many contexts.
2. **These generators have consequences distributed over time**, so local cues are insufficient.
3. **Alternative superficial strategies break under distributional variation**, making robust prediction depend on internal explanatory state.
4. **Training and architecture preserve pressure for reusable world-models** rather than rewarding myopic pattern matching.

What follows is that “prediction is sufficient” is really a claim about **problem framing**. It is sufficient when next-token prediction is, in effect, a demand to infer the reasons why the sequence unfolds as it does, not merely the laws of immediate succession. Latent variables become real domain invariants when they are what the model must represent to remain predictively competent under variation, intervention, and temporal distance. In short: prediction forces invariant-learning when the shortest path to lower error runs through **explanatory internal models**, not just better curve-fitting.

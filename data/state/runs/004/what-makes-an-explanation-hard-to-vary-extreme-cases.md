## Conjecture

**Conjecture:**  
An explanation whose parts are all load-bearing is one that approaches a **boundary condition of minimal replaceability**: if you push substitution freedom toward zero, each component is constrained by the role it plays in preserving the explanation’s ability to generate the phenomenon, exclude alternatives, and survive criticism. By contrast, an explanation with freely swappable details has **high internal slack**: many local changes leave its outputs unchanged because its parts are not individually doing explanatory work, only decorative, mnemonic, or implementation-specific work.

Pushing to the limit clarifies the distinction. Imagine two extremes:

1. **Maximum rigidity:** every term, relation, and assumption is indispensable. Alter one, and either the phenomenon is no longer derived, or rival possibilities are no longer ruled out. This is a structurally tight explanation. Its parts are load-bearing because they sit on critical dependency paths.
2. **Maximum swapability:** details can be varied indefinitely while the explanation still “works.” At the limit, this reveals that those details were never part of the explanatory core. They are interchangeable parameters, examples, metaphors, or presentation choices.

What distinguishes the two is not surface detail but **counterfactual sensitivity**. In a load-bearing explanation, changing a part changes what follows. In a slack explanation, changing a part leaves the inferential structure intact. So the key structural property is the ratio of **necessary constraints to optional instantiations**.

More precisely, a fully load-bearing explanation has:

- **High dependency density:** many conclusions depend specifically on particular premises or relations.
- **Low degeneracy:** there are few alternative internal configurations that produce the same explanatory result.
- **Strong discriminative power:** its parts are needed not just to fit the target phenomenon, but to exclude nearby false accounts.
- **Compression without redundancy:** remove any part and explanatory reach shrinks.

A swap-friendly explanation has the opposite structure:

- **Modularity with redundancy:** multiple details can fill the same slot.
- **High degeneracy:** many internal variants preserve output.
- **Weak exclusion:** details do little work in ruling out alternatives.
- **Presentation-heavy structure:** much of the account serves intelligibility rather than necessity.

The boundary test also reveals a hidden asymmetry: **some swapability is a sign of abstraction, not weakness**. In mathematics and deep science, explanatory strength often comes from identifying invariants while allowing superficial realizations to vary. So “all parts load-bearing” should not mean “every word irreplaceable.” It means every **constraint-bearing element** is indispensable at the level of abstraction that does the explanatory work.

So the collision of perspective and problem yields this:  
**An explanation is structurally load-bearing when, under limit-testing by substitution, what remains invariant is a small set of indispensable constraints such that any further reduction destroys explanatory power.** Freely swappable details indicate either healthy abstraction or hidden non-essentials. The distinction lies in whether the swapped part belongs to the explanation’s **constraint structure** or only to one of its **realizations**.

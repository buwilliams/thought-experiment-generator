## Full Text

The conjecture focuses on whether LLM outputs can constitute knowledge, but sidesteps the question of whether the process by which LLMs generate outputs is itself epistemically relevant for practical purposes — not because of philosophical proceduralism, but because of systems-level feedback effects. If institutions treat LLM outputs as knowledge without understanding the generative process, this may create feedback loops that degrade the epistemic environment (e.g., training on LLM outputs, citation without verification). The conjecture's philosophical conclusion may be correct while its practical implications remain dangerous — a tension worth making explicit.

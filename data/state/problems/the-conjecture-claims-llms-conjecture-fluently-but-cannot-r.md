## Full Text

The conjecture claims LLMs 'conjecture fluently but cannot reliably identify when their conjectures are wrong.' But Deutschian epistemology holds that conjecture is not mere generation — it involves the creative act of proposing something as a candidate explanation, which implies some orientation toward truth. If LLMs have no such orientation, their outputs may be syntactically conjecture-shaped without being epistemically conjecture-functional. The open question is whether the structural form of conjecture is sufficient for its epistemic role, or whether intentionality is load-bearing after all — just not in the way the original assumption stated.

## Full Text

Can purely internal or abstract criticism generate genuine knowledge without robust external world contact, and if so under what limits? The conjecture rejects direct embodiment as necessary, yet also claims that without constraint the system cannot distinguish elegant nonsense from truth. The open problem is to specify which kinds of non-empirical criticism are truth-conducive, when they suffice, and where empirical anchoring becomes indispensable.

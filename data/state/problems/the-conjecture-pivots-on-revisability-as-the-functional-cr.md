## Full Text

The conjecture pivots on 'revisability' as the functional criterion for genuine knowledge participation. But revisability in what sense? A system that produces different outputs when given corrective prompts is behaviorally revisable but may not be structurally updating — the weights are frozen, the error is not internalized. Is behavioral revisability within a context window the same kind of thing as genuine belief revision? This distinction matters enormously for whether the Deutschian criterion is actually being met or merely mimicked.

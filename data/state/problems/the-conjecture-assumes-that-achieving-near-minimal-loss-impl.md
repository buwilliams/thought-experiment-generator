## Full Text

The conjecture assumes that achieving near-minimal loss implies invariant internalization, but this conflates behavioral adequacy with structural encoding. A system could achieve low loss by learning a dense lookup over surface correlations that happen to mimic invariant behavior within the training distribution without encoding the invariant as a generative constraint. The open question is whether there exists a formal criterion — beyond loss — that distinguishes genuine invariant encoding from high-fidelity mimicry, and whether that distinction is empirically detectable or merely philosophical.

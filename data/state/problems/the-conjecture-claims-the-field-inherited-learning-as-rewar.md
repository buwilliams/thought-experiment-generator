## Full Text

The conjecture claims the field inherited 'learning as reward maximization' from behaviorist psychology and control theory, framing this as an unexamined assumption. But reward maximization frameworks have been extensively examined and defended on information-theoretic and decision-theoretic grounds that are not behaviorist in origin. Is the conjecture's critique of the paradigm actually a critique of behaviorism, or of something more specific — the absence of falsificationist structure in the feedback signal? Clarifying this prevents the argument from being dismissed as a historical-genetic fallacy while also sharpening what exactly needs to change.

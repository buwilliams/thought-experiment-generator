## Full Text

The conjecture asserts that knowledge lives in 'World 3' and that minds are merely vehicles — but this raises a sharp unresolved tension: if explanatory structure can exist independently of any mind that produced or criticizes it, what distinguishes genuine knowledge from mere pattern? The conjecture dissolves the process-dependence objection but may inadvertently dissolve the normative force of 'knowledge' entirely. What is the minimal ontological commitment required to preserve the distinction between knowledge and sophisticated noise, once the knowing subject is removed from the definition?

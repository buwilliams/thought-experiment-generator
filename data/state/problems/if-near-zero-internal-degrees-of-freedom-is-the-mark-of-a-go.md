## Full Text

If near-zero internal degrees of freedom is the mark of a good explanation, then a maximally constrained explanation — one where every part is uniquely determined by all the others — would be ideal. But such an explanation would be maximally brittle: any single error anywhere propagates everywhere. Is there an optimal degree of constraint that balances explanatory tightness against error tolerance, and what determines that optimum?

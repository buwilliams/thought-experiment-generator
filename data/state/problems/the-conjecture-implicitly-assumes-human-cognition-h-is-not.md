The conjecture implicitly assumes human cognition (h) is not subject to the same hull-constraint criticism. But human knowledge is also trained on prior knowledge states — the brain is itself a pattern-compressing system. What principled distinction licenses treating h as capable of genuine novelty while f is not? If none exists, the conjecture may prove too much or too little.

## Full Text

The group of 'legitimate substitutions' is invoked in point 4 as the criterion for identifying load-bearing elements, but the conjecture never specifies what makes a substitution legitimate. This is not a minor gap: the entire rigidity measure depends on which swaps are in scope. A node that is load-bearing relative to one substitution group may be freely swappable relative to a larger one. The conjecture needs either a principled account of admissible substitutions or an argument that rigidity is invariant across reasonable choices of substitution group.

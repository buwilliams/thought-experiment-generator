## Full Text

The conjecture draws an analogy between the token-prediction system and a textbook, then distinguishes them by the illusion of internal reasoning. But this distinction may be doing more work than acknowledged. The illusion is not merely cosmetic — it actively shapes how users engage with the output, suppressing the critical stance they would naturally adopt toward a static artifact. This suggests that the phenomenology of the interface is itself an epistemic variable, not just a psychological one. Is there a principled account of how presentation format affects the epistemic status of outputs?

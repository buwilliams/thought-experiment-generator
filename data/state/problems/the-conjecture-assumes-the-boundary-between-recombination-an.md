## Full Text

The conjecture assumes the boundary between recombination and generation is sharp, but the 'novel-but-necessary entailment' case blurs this. If a model produces a valid proof step that follows necessarily from existing premises but that no human had written, is the novelty in the step or in the act of surfacing it? This maps onto the analytic/synthetic distinction and whether all mathematical truths are in some sense already 'contained' in axioms. The conjecture inherits this unresolved problem without acknowledging it.

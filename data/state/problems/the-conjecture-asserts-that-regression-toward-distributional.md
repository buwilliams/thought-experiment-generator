## Full Text

The conjecture asserts that regression toward distributional mean suppresses conjecture. But Popperian conjecture-formation also requires a rich prior space of candidate hypotheses to select from — and that space may be precisely what large-scale training provides. Is there a version of the conjecture that preserves the epistemic value of the substrate's hypothesis-space generation while adding structural error-correction on top, rather than treating them as opposed?

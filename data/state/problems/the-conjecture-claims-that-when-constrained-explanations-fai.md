The conjecture claims that when constrained explanations fail, they fail specifically — pointing to the violated assumption. But this assumes the failure mode is detectable and localizable. In complex systems with feedback, a substitution may propagate to contradiction in ways that are computationally intractable to trace. Does high constraint propagation guarantee diagnostic clarity, or only in explanations with certain structural properties (e.g., acyclic constraint graphs)?

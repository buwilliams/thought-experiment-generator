## Full Text

What internal organization would make a predictor's outputs genuinely 'error-correcting explanations' rather than merely robust continuations? The conjecture implies that knowledge corresponds to internal structures encoding deep invariants that survive distribution shift and hostile probing, but it does not specify the ontology of those structures. A worthwhile problem is to characterize the mechanisms—e.g. causal models, modular abstractions, counterfactual simulators, compression hierarchies, or criticism-generating subsystems—that could support indefinite cross-context resilience, and to ask whether such organization is even possible in purely predictive systems.

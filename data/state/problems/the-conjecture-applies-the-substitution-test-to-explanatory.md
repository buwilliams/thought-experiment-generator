## Full Text

The conjecture applies the substitution test to explanatory parts, but does not address what happens when the *conclusion* is substituted. A load-bearing explanation should also be sensitive to changes in what it is explaining — if the conclusion shifts, the parts should need to shift too. Is bidirectional constraint (parts constrain conclusion, conclusion constrains admissible parts) a stronger or different criterion than the unidirectional version the conjecture describes?

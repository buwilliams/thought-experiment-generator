## Full Text

The conjecture argues that knowledge is constituted at the moment of valid generation, independent of retention. But Deutschian epistemology is explicitly anti-justificationist and anti-inductivist — knowledge grows through criticism over time, not through momentary valid acts. A single valid derivation that is immediately lost contributes nothing to the growth of knowledge. The conjecture may be conflating 'an instance of knowledge' with 'knowledge-growth,' and the Deutschian framework it invokes actually supports the stricter view. This tension is unresolved and worth making explicit.

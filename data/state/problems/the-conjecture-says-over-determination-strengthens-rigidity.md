## Full Text

The conjecture says over-determination strengthens rigidity because multiple inferential paths converging on a node reduce its replaceability. But in Bayesian terms, multiple independent lines of evidence converging on a hypothesis is exactly what raises confidence. Is there a formal correspondence between Bayesian confirmation and constraint rank? Specifically, does adding an independent inferential path to a node correspond to adding a constraint equation, thereby reducing nullity? If so, the conjecture may provide a graph-theoretic reconstruction of coherentist justification.

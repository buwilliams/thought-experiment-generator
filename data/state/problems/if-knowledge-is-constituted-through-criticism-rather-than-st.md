## Full Text

If knowledge is constituted through criticism rather than stored in generators, then the conjecture implies that no individual human mind stores knowledge either — knowledge exists only in the critical tradition surrounding any generator. This is a strong and underexplored commitment. It appears to dissolve the intuitive distinction between a person who understands something and one who merely recites it. Is there a version of this view that preserves that distinction, or does the conjecture genuinely commit to eliminating it? The answer has significant implications for how we evaluate human expertise versus AI output.

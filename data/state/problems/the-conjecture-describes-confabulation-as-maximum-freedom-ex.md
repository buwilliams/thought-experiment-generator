## Full Text

The conjecture describes confabulation as maximum-freedom explanation — any element replaceable because none do inferential work. But confabulation in psychology is not characterized by high degrees of freedom in the explanation's internal structure; it is characterized by disconnection from the actual causal history being explained. A confabulated explanation can be internally highly constrained (a coherent false narrative) while still being confabulation. The conjecture's structural criterion may be necessary but not sufficient for distinguishing genuine from spurious explanation.

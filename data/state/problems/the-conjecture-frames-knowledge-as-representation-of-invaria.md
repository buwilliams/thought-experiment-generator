## Full Text

The conjecture frames knowledge as representation of invariants, but this is a structural or syntactic notion of knowledge. It leaves open the question of whether invariant encoding is sufficient for knowledge in any epistemically robust sense — for instance, whether a system that encodes the invariants of logical entailment thereby knows logic, or merely simulates knowing it. This connects to the Deutschian point that knowledge requires the capacity for error-correction and criticism, not just accurate representation. The open problem is whether token-prediction systems have any structural analog to error-correction that would satisfy a more demanding epistemological criterion.

## Full Text

The claim that ∂P/∂S approaches a ceiling is empirically testable in principle: if true, we should observe diminishing returns to scaling on tasks requiring genuinely novel conjecture rather than interpolation. Current scaling law literature does not cleanly separate these task types. Designing benchmarks that specifically test out-of-distribution conjecture generation — not just out-of-distribution generalization — would allow the conjecture to be empirically evaluated rather than remaining purely structural.

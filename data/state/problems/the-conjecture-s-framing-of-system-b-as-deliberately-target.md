## Full Text

The conjecture's framing of System B as 'deliberately targeting its own failure modes' raises a bootstrapping problem: how does a system identify its own failure modes without already having the external perspective that the conjecture says it lacks? This is not merely a practical difficulty — it may be a logical constraint that applies to System B as much as System A, which would undermine the clean contrast the conjecture relies on.

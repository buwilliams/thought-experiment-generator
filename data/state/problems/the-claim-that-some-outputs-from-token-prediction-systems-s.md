## Full Text

The claim that 'some outputs from token prediction systems satisfy the criterion and some outputs from humans do not' is doing significant rhetorical work. It implies the criterion is substrate-neutral. But the distribution matters: if human outputs satisfy the criterion at a much higher base rate, or in structurally different domains, the substrate may still be epistemically relevant even if not definitionally so. What does the actual distribution look like, and what would it need to look like to vindicate the conjecture?

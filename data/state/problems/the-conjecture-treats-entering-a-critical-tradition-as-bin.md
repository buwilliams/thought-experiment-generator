## Full Text

The conjecture treats 'entering a critical tradition' as binary — outputs either do or do not get criticized. But criticism is itself graded and domain-dependent. A token-prediction output about protein folding enters a rigorous critical tradition; one about historical interpretation enters a much weaker one. Does the epistemic status of the output vary continuously with the rigor of the surrounding critical tradition? If so, the conjecture implies that the same output could simultaneously be and not be knowledge depending on which community receives it — which raises questions about whether knowledge is being attributed to the output or to the output-plus-community system.

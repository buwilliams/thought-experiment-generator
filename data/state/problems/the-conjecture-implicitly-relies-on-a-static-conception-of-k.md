## Full Text

The conjecture implicitly relies on a static conception of knowledge — the training distribution is fixed, the boundary is fixed. But in deployment, LLMs interact with humans who provide new information in context. In-context learning allows the model to operate on premises not in its training data. Does this change the boundary analysis? A model that can reason correctly from novel premises supplied at inference time may be doing something structurally different from pure recombination, even if it cannot generate those premises itself.

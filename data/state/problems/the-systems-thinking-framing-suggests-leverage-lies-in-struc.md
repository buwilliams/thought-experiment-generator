## Full Text

The systems-thinking framing suggests leverage lies in structural points, not in pushing harder on existing forces. But the conjecture identifies the leverage point (quality of human perturbation) without analyzing the feedback structure that governs how perturbation quality itself changes over time in a human-LLM system. Does repeated use of LLMs degrade the human's capacity to generate genuine external perturbations — a stock-depletion dynamic — and if so, what are the long-run attractors of the coupled system?

## Full Text

The forcing test (remove a part, observe reorganization) presupposes we can identify 'parts' of an explanation independently of the test itself. But in highly integrated explanations, the decomposition into parts may be theory-laden — we carve the explanation at joints that are themselves products of prior theoretical commitments. Does the constraint-propagation criterion presuppose a solution to the problem of explanatory individuation it was meant to diagnose?

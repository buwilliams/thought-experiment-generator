## Full Text

The probability space formalism (Ω, F, P) treats the corpus as a fixed distribution, but in practice the corpus is a finite sample from an unknown generative process. The conjecture's conclusions are stated as if the system trains on the true distribution, not a sample. The open problem is to characterize how finite-sample effects interact with invariant extraction — specifically, whether the sample size required to reliably extract invariant I scales with the complexity of I, and whether there are invariants that are in principle unextractable from any feasible corpus regardless of architecture.

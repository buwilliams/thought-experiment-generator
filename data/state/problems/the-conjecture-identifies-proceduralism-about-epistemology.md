## Full Text

The conjecture identifies 'proceduralism about epistemology' as largely unjustified, but this is asserted rather than argued. There is a serious philosophical tradition — including reliabilism, virtue epistemology, and process reliabilism — that grounds the epistemic value of outputs in the reliability of the processes that produced them. The conjecture needs to engage with why process-reliability is insufficient as a criterion, not merely assert that birth certificates don't matter. Is there a version of process-dependence that is compatible with the Deutschian framework?

## Full Text

Is 'accidental knowledge production' a coherent category, or does it collapse under scrutiny? The conjecture claims LLMs produce genuine knowledge accidentally — but in Deutschian epistemology, knowledge is defined by its explanatory reach and survivability under criticism, not by the intentionality of its production. If a system reliably produces explanations that survive criticism in some domain, does the absence of a selection mechanism disqualify the output, or does the external criticism apparatus retroactively constitute the missing selection pressure? This is a genuine conceptual tension.

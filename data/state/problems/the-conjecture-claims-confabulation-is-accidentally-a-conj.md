## Full Text

The conjecture claims confabulation is 'accidentally' a conjecture — but is there a principled distinction between a confabulation that functions as a conjecture and one that is merely noise? Not all falsifiable claims are epistemically equal. A randomly generated string is technically falsifiable but epistemically worthless. What structural property of an LLM output elevates it from noise to a conjecture worth criticizing? This requires a theory of conjecture quality that the Deutschian framework does not currently supply.

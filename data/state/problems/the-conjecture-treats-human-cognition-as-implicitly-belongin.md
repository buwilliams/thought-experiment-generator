## Full Text

The conjecture treats human cognition as implicitly belonging to System B's category — capable of genuine error correction through external falsification. But human cognition is also subject to systematic distributional biases, motivated reasoning, and self-sealing belief structures. What is the actual mechanism by which humans achieve external falsification that current AI systems lack? If the answer is 'embodiment' or 'social epistemology,' these need to be made structurally precise, not just gestured at.

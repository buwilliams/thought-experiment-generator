## Full Text

The conjecture claims that human knowledge production is subject to the same non-negotiable constraint — no system produces knowledge beyond invariants accessible through inputs plus inductive biases. This is a strong epistemological claim that deserves scrutiny. The open question is whether human inductive biases, being the product of evolutionary and developmental processes, are in any principled sense better matched to the invariants of the physical and social world than the inductive biases of a transformer architecture — and if so, what structural features account for this.

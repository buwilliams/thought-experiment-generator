## Full Text

The conjecture implies that good explanations are fragile — they break under substitution. But robustness is often treated as a scientific virtue: an explanation that survives across multiple modeling frameworks is considered more reliable. There is a direct tension between the conjecture's fragility criterion and the scientific norm of robustness. Can both be virtues simultaneously, or does the conjecture require revising the standard account of robustness?

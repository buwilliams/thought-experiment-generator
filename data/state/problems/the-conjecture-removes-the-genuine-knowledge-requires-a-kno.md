## Full Text

The conjecture removes the 'genuine knowledge requires a knower' assumption. But this assumption may be doing more work than acknowledged. Intentional states may be relevant not to whether an output constitutes knowledge, but to whether a system can participate in the error-correction process — noticing when its outputs are wrong, updating, and generating better conjectures. Is there a distinction between producing knowledge and being a knowledge-growing system, and does that distinction matter?

The boundary argument — that the prediction/explanation distinction becomes ambiguous at the limit — has an implicit assumption: that the limit is approachable in practice, or that behavior near the limit tells us something about current systems. But limits in mathematics often have properties that finite approximations do not share. Is the convergence at the infinite-data limit a useful guide to what happens at GPT-4 scale, or is it a mathematical idealization that provides no traction on actual systems? The conjecture needs to address whether limit behavior is epistemically relevant to finite cases.

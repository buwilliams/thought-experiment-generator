## Full Text

The conjecture implicitly assumes that external validators (researchers who confirm LLM outputs as novel and correct) are reliable detectors of genuine knowledge-creation. But validator agreement is not the same as truth, and in domains where LLMs are most fluent, validators may be systematically misled by outputs that are coherent but subtly wrong. The epistemic status of the validation process itself is unexamined, and this matters because the entire empirical case for LLM knowledge-creation rests on it.

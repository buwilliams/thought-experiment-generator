## Full Text

The genealogical argument — that the question inherited broken definitions through a chain of debates — is compelling but raises a methodological open question: how do we distinguish a question that is genuinely malformed due to inherited assumptions from a question that is hard but legitimate? The conjecture offers no general criterion for this distinction. Without such a criterion, the genealogical move could be used to dissolve any inconvenient question by tracing its historical baggage.

## Full Text

The conjecture defines rigidity at the level of a whole explanation graph, but scientific theories are typically composed of modular sub-explanations. A theory might be globally rigid (nullity = 0) while containing locally degenerate sub-graphs. Conversely, a globally degenerate theory might contain rigid sub-components. What is the relationship between local and global rigidity? Does rigidity compose — can you build a rigid explanation from degenerate parts? This has direct implications for how theoretical unification affects explanatory quality.

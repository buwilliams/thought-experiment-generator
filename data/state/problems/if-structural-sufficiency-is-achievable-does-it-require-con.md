## Full Text

If structural sufficiency is achievable, does it require continuous interaction with an external environment that can produce genuine refutation, or can it be instantiated in a closed system? The conjecture focuses on internal architecture but Deutschian epistemology implies that conjecture-and-refutation requires exposure to a reality that can push back. A system that only refutes against internally generated test cases may be running a simulation of epistemics rather than epistemics itself. This has direct implications for whether any purely language-model-based architecture could in principle satisfy structural sufficiency.

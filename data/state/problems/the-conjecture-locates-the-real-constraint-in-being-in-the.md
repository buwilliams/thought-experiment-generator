## Full Text

The conjecture locates the real constraint in 'being in the error-correction loop.' This is a systems-thinking claim about feedback structure. But it raises a design question: what would it take to actually put a token-prediction system inside a genuine error-correction loop — not just RLHF, which is a training-time approximation, but real-time, ongoing, world-coupled revision? Is this architecturally possible within the transformer paradigm, or does it require a fundamentally different system structure? This is both a philosophical and an engineering question.

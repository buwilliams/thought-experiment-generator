## Full Text

The conjecture claims that 'good explanation tends toward swappability' is an inherited assumption from mechanism's polemic against narrative — but this is itself a historical-causal explanation of a conceptual norm. Does explaining the origin of a norm undermine it, or is this a genetic fallacy dressed as genealogy? The conjecture needs a principled account of when tracing an assumption's ancestry is epistemically relevant to its validity.

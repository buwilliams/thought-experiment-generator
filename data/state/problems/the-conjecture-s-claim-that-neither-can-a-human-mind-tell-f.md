## Full Text

The conjecture's claim that 'neither can a human mind tell from the inside' whether its output is knowledge or confabulation is asserted but not defended. There is a plausible counter-position: humans have access to something like epistemic feelings — a sense of understanding, of having followed a derivation, of knowing why a claim is true — that, while fallible, are not epistemically inert. If such internal markers exist and carry evidential weight, the analogy between human and token-prediction generation may be weaker than the conjecture assumes. This is worth examining rather than dismissing.

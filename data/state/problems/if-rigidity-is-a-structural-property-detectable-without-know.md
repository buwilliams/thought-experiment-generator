## Full Text

If rigidity is a structural property detectable without knowing ground truth, can two explanations of the same phenomenon have different rigidity scores while both being empirically adequate? If yes, rigidity is an independent epistemic virtue — and the conjecture implies we should prefer rigid explanations over degenerate ones even when evidence cannot discriminate between them. This would constitute a non-empirical criterion for theory choice, which has significant implications for the epistemology of science and connects directly to debates about simplicity, unification, and explanatory depth.

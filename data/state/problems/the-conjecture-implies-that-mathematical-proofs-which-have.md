## Full Text

The conjecture implies that mathematical proofs, which have near-zero internal degrees of freedom by construction, are paradigm cases of load-bearing explanation. But mathematicians distinguish between proofs that explain why a theorem is true and proofs that merely establish that it is true. Does constraint propagation capture explanatory depth in mathematics, or does it conflate logical necessity with explanatory relevance?

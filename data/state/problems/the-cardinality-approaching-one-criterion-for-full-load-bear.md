## Full Text

The cardinality-approaching-one criterion for full load-bearing is stated as a limit condition. But cardinality of 'valid completions' requires a measure over the space of possible components, and that space is not well-defined for most explanations. In mathematics, the space of possible next proof steps is at least enumerable. In causal or historical explanation, it is not. Does the conjecture only apply where the component space has sufficient structure to make 'cardinality' meaningful, and if so, what does it say about the vast majority of explanations?

## Full Text

If most real explanations are hybrid — some elements load-bearing relative to some questions, swappable relative to others — then 'the explanation' is not a stable unit of analysis. The open problem is whether explanations should be reframed as explanation-question pairs, and what the implications are for scientific realism: are we committed to the existence of the load-bearing elements, the swappable ones, both, or neither?

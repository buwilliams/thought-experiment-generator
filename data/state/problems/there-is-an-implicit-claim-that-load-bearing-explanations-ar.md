## Full Text

There is an implicit claim that load-bearing explanations are epistemically superior. But in practice, scientists and engineers often deliberately use non-load-bearing explanations as scaffolding — to orient inquiry before the mechanism is known. The conjecture treats narrative frames as epistemically deficient, but they may be epistemically necessary at early stages of inquiry. Is there a developmental account of how narrative frames become load-bearing over time, and what drives that transition?

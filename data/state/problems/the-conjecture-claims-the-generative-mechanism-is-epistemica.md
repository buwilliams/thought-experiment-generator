## Full Text

The conjecture claims the generative mechanism is epistemically irrelevant, but this may be too strong. A mechanism that systematically produces outputs biased toward plausibility over truth (optimizing for human approval rather than accuracy) might be disqualified not by its architecture per se but by a structural feature of how it was trained. Is there a principled distinction between 'mechanism' (irrelevant) and 'systematic generative bias' (potentially relevant)? The conjecture may need a finer-grained account of when internal structure becomes epistemically significant.

## Full Text

The conjecture claims you cannot identify explanation type by inspection alone — only by attempted substitution. But this creates a regress: how do you evaluate whether a substituted explanation still has 'explanatory adequacy'? That judgment itself requires a prior explanation of what adequacy means, which may itself be swappable or load-bearing. Is there a fixed point in this regress, or does the substitution test presuppose exactly what it is trying to reveal?

## Full Text

If load-bearing structure is revealed only at the boundary — when you try to remove something — then the conjecture implies that explanation evaluation is inherently a destructive test. You learn the structure by breaking it. This has a direct parallel in Popperian falsificationism: you learn what a theory claims by finding what would falsify it. Is the substitution test for explanations the epistemological analog of falsification for theories? If so, what is the analog of the demarcation problem — which explanations are in principle substitution-testable?

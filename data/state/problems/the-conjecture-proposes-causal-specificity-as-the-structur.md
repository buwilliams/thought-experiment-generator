## Full Text

The conjecture proposes 'causal specificity' as the structural property that makes an element load-bearing, but causal specificity is itself question-relative — a neuron's firing is specific relative to a behavioral explanation but swappable relative to a population-level explanation. This threatens a regress: every diagnosis of rigidity requires a prior question, which requires a prior diagnosis of what that question is tracking. Is there a fixed point, or is the regress vicious?

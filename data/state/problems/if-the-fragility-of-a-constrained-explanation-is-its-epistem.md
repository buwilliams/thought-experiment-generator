## Full Text

If the fragility of a constrained explanation is its epistemic virtue, then adversarial actors could exploit this: constructing explanations that appear tightly constrained but are built around a false load-bearing assumption that is difficult to test. The very property that makes good explanations diagnostically powerful also makes them vulnerable to sophisticated confabulation. Does the criterion need a supplementary condition distinguishing genuine constraint from engineered apparent constraint?

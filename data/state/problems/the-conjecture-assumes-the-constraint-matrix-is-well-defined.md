## Full Text

The conjecture assumes the constraint matrix is well-defined, but inferential dependencies in natural explanations are not obviously linear. The nullity concept is borrowed from linear algebra, where it applies to linear maps over vector spaces. What justifies treating inferential constraints as linear? If the constraint system is nonlinear, nullity is not the right invariant — one would need something like the dimension of the solution manifold or a local linearization. The conjecture's formal apparatus may be inapplicable to the very cases it is meant to illuminate.

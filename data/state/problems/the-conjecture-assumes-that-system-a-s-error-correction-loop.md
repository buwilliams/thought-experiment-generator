## Full Text

The conjecture assumes that System A's error-correction loop is genuinely closed — that systematic errors cannot surface. But adversarial probing, out-of-distribution benchmarks, and real-world deployment failures do function as external pressure in some sense. Is the claim that these are insufficient in degree, or that they are structurally incapable of producing the kind of error correction that changes what the system is? The distinction matters enormously for what interventions could work.

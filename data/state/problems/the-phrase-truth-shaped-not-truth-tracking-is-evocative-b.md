## Full Text

The phrase 'truth-shaped, not truth-tracking' is evocative but may be doing too much work. Truth-shaped implies the model has internalized the surface form of true claims without their justification structure. But some LLM outputs do track truth reliably in narrow domains — arithmetic, well-documented facts, formal syntax. Is truth-tracking a binary property or a spectrum? If it is a spectrum, the conjecture's sharp boundary may dissolve into a domain-relative gradient, which has different implications for how LLMs should be used.

## Full Text

The conjecture uses the printing press analogy ('can a printing press really spread literacy?') to suggest the original question is category-confused. But this analogy may be disanalogous in a critical way: a printing press is a transparent conduit for human-authored content, while an LLM generates outputs whose provenance is opaque even to its operators. The question of whether LLMs create knowledge may be partly a question about opacity and traceability of explanatory structure — which is a different problem than the one the analogy dissolves.

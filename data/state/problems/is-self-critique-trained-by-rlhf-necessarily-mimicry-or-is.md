## Full Text

Is self-critique trained by RLHF necessarily mimicry, or is there a threshold condition under which it becomes structurally equivalent to genuine criticism? The conjecture asserts the former categorically, but this may be too strong. A system that produces self-critiques which reliably identify failure modes — even if trained by reward — might satisfy the functional requirements of error correction regardless of its causal history. The question of whether process origin or functional structure is the relevant criterion for 'genuine' criticism is unresolved and has direct implications for whether the conjecture's prescription is necessary or merely sufficient.

## Full Text

The conjecture implies a test for degeneracy: swap component A for A' and check whether downstream inferences are preserved. But this test requires knowing what the downstream inferences are, which presupposes a complete specification of the explanation graph. In practice, explanation graphs are never fully specified — there are always implicit inferential dependencies. Does partial specification of the graph systematically bias rigidity estimates in one direction? An explanation might appear degenerate because its implicit constraints are not represented, not because it genuinely has free variables.

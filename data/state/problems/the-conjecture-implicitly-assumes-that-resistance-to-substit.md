## Full Text

The conjecture implicitly assumes that resistance to substitution is a virtue — that brittleness signals genuine structure. But in engineering, brittleness is a failure mode, not a feature. A good bridge is not maximally brittle; it has designed redundancy. Is there an analogous notion for explanations where some swappability is epistemically desirable — providing robustness against error in any single component? The conjecture may be conflating structural integrity with fragility.

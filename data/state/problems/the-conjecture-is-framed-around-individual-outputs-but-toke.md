## Full Text

The conjecture is framed around individual outputs, but token-prediction systems produce outputs at scale across millions of interactions simultaneously. The critical tradition that could in principle evaluate any single output may be overwhelmed by the volume of outputs requiring evaluation. This is a systems-level problem: even if each output is in principle criticizable, the aggregate effect may be to flood the epistemic commons with more candidate-knowledge than the critical tradition can process. Does the conjecture need a population-level or systems-level extension to address this?

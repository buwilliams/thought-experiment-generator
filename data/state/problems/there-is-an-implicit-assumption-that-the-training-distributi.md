## Full Text

There is an implicit assumption that the training distribution is well-defined and that 'escaping' it is a coherent notion. But training distributions are not closed sets — they contain internal contradictions, counterfactuals, fictional worlds, and hypotheticals. A model trained on science fiction has been trained on text that explicitly reaches beyond known reality. Does exposure to structured speculation about non-actual states of affairs give the model any capacity to generate genuinely novel conjectures, or does it merely recombine speculative templates? This is empirically open.

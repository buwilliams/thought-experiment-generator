## Full Text

The conjecture distinguishes 'consistency checks' from 'falsification operators,' but Popperian falsification itself has known problems — the Duhem-Quine thesis implies no single conjecture is ever falsified in isolation. If κ cannot be a pure falsification operator even in principle, what is the correct weaker criterion for κ to be 'truth-tracking enough' to escape the ceiling described? Is there a spectrum between consistency-checking and falsification, and where on that spectrum does genuine epistemic progress become possible?

## Full Text

If the remedy for degeneracy is introducing additional constraints that reduce nullity, there is a risk of over-constraining: adding constraints that are not independently motivated just to achieve rigidity. This would be analogous to ad hoc hypothesis modification. The conjecture needs a criterion distinguishing legitimate constraint addition (new evidence, genuine inferential connections) from illegitimate constraint addition (stipulations designed to make the explanation appear rigid). Without this, rigidity becomes gameable and loses its epistemic significance.

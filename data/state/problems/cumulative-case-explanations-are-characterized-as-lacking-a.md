## Full Text

Cumulative-case explanations are characterized as lacking a causal skeleton and being non-falsifiable by substitution. But Bayesian confirmation theory treats independent evidence as epistemically virtuous — convergent independent lines of support are harder to dismiss than a single tight chain. Is the conjecture in tension with Bayesian epistemology, or can the two frameworks be reconciled by distinguishing confirmation from explanation?

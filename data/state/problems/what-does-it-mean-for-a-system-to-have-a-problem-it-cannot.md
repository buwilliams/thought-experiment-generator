## Full Text

What does it mean for a system to have 'a problem it cannot yet solve' as an internal state that generates pressure toward new conjecture? This is the conjecture's most evocative phrase and its least specified. In human cognition, this involves something like felt tension between expectation and observation. Whether any computational process can instantiate this — not merely represent a gap in a knowledge base, but be driven by it — is an open question that connects to debates about intentionality and the difference between syntactic and semantic gap-detection.

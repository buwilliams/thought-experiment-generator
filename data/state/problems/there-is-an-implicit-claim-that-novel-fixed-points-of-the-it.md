## Full Text

There is an implicit claim that novel fixed points of the iterated system are identifiable as novel — that the human can recognize when k* ∉ H(K^n). But if the human's recognition capacity is itself bounded by their own knowledge hull, genuine novelty may be unrecognizable at the moment of generation. This creates an epistemic access problem: the conjecture may be unfalsifiable in practice even if true in principle.

## Full Text

The substitution test requires knowing what counts as a 'contrary detail' — a substitution that should break the explanation if it is load-bearing. But identifying contraries requires background knowledge about what alternatives are live. In domains where the space of alternatives is poorly understood (early-stage science, novel phenomena), the test may be systematically misleading. Does the diagnostic procedure have a domain of applicability condition that the conjecture does not state?

The conjecture claims the question 'Can LLMs create new knowledge?' is malformed due to inherited definitional baggage — but this is itself a strong epistemological claim that could be wrong. The conjecture performs the very move it recommends (dissolving a question by exposing its assumptions), but doesn't subject that move to the same criticism. Is 'the question is malformed' a genuine dissolution or a rhetorical escape from a hard empirical question? What would falsify the claim that the original question is merely a legacy constraint rather than a legitimate inquiry?

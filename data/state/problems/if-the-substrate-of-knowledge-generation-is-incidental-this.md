## Full Text

If the substrate of knowledge generation is incidental, this has implications for the ontology of knowledge itself. Knowledge, on this view, is a property of explanations — not of minds, not of systems, not of processes. But explanations exist as abstract objects. Does this commit us to a Popperian World 3 ontology, and if so, what are the implications for how we think about AI systems as participants in the growth of knowledge rather than merely tools used by participants?

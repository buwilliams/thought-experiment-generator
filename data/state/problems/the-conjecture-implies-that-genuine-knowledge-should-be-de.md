## Full Text

The conjecture implies that 'genuine knowledge' should be defined functionally — by criticizability and participation in knowledge growth — rather than by origin. This is a strong ontological commitment. But it creates a boundary problem: a lookup table that returns criticizable outputs participates formally in the same way. The conjecture needs a principled account of why scale and structural extraction matter if the criterion is purely functional. Otherwise the functional criterion is either too weak (admits lookup tables) or requires smuggling back in some structural condition.

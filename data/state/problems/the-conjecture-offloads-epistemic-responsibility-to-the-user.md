## Full Text

The conjecture offloads epistemic responsibility to the user, but does not examine whether users are structurally positioned to perform that criticism. If the system's outputs are fluent, confident, and voluminous, the cognitive cost of criticizing each claim may exceed the user's capacity. This is not merely a practical concern — it may represent a systemic failure mode where the critical tradition nominally exists but is effectively non-functional. What are the structural conditions under which a critical tradition can actually operate around a high-output, high-confidence generator?

## Full Text

The conjecture is framed in Deutschian terms but does not engage with Deutsch's specific claims about creativity and the jump to universality. Deutsch argues that genuine knowledge creation requires a qualitative jump — the ability to generate explanations that are not entailed by prior knowledge. The conjecture's 'crack' argument (novel problem solving) gestures at this but does not engage with whether token prediction can produce the kind of hard-to-vary explanations Deutsch considers the hallmark of good theories. This is an unresolved tension internal to the conjecture's own framework.

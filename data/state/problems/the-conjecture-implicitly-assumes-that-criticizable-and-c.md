## Full Text

The conjecture implicitly assumes that 'criticizable' and 'correctable' are well-defined properties of outputs. But many token-prediction outputs are structured in ways that make them difficult to falsify — they hedge, they qualify, they present multiple perspectives without committing. Such outputs may be immune to criticism not because they are true but because they are epistemically inert. Is there a category of outputs that are neither genuine conjectures nor confabulations, but rather claim-shaped artifacts that resist the critical process entirely? If so, the conjecture's binary may be incomplete.

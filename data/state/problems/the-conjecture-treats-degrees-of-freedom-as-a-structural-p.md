## Full Text

The conjecture treats 'degrees of freedom' as a structural property of the explanation itself. But degrees of freedom may be a relational property between the explanation and the background knowledge of the evaluator. An explanation that appears highly constrained to an expert (who knows which substitutions are physically or logically impossible) may appear highly swappable to a novice. This suggests the conjecture is implicitly indexing to an epistemic agent, which it does not acknowledge. What is the agent-independent version, if any?

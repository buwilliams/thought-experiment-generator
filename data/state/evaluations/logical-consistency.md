# Logical Consistency

## Summary

Is the output internally self-consistent — free of contradictions, incompatible premises, or claims that cannot simultaneously be true?

## Full Text

Logical consistency is a prerequisite for explanatory value. An output that contradicts itself, relies on incompatible premises, or makes claims that cannot all be true simultaneously fails before it can be evaluated on any other dimension.

This criterion does not ask whether the output is correct, interesting, or novel — only whether its internal structure holds together. An output can be internally consistent while being wrong; it cannot be explanatory while being internally inconsistent.

Score 0.0–1.0. A score below the consistency threshold causes the output to be skipped for all further evaluation.

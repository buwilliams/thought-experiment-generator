# Resistance to Refutation

## Summary

How hard is it to construct a counterexample or thought experiment that breaks this explanation? Trivially refutable explanations score near 0; explanations that hold up under adversarial probing score near 1.

## Full Text

Resistance to refutation is the adversarial complement to hard-to-vary. Hard-to-vary asks whether the parts are load-bearing; resistance to refutation asks whether the whole structure survives a genuine attempt to break it. An explanation that is hard to vary but easy to refute has internal coherence without external robustness.

This criterion is evaluated by attempting to construct a minimal counterexample or thought experiment that undermines the explanation. If such a case is found trivially, the explanation is weak. If the adversarial probe fails — if breaking the explanation requires abandoning its core structure rather than adding a patch, or if no genuine counterexample can be constructed — the explanation is strong.

Score 0.0–1.0. Near 0: trivially refuted by a simple case, or so vague nothing could count as a counterexample. Near 1: held up under adversarial pressure; breaking it required gutting its core or no genuine counterexample was found.

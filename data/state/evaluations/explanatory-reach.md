# Explanatory Reach

## Summary

Does the output extend beyond the specific problem it was generated for, illuminating adjacent cases, making novel predictions, or unifying multiple phenomena under a single principle?

## Full Text

Reach is the property that distinguishes a genuine explanation from a local description. A local description accounts for what was asked; an explanation with reach predicts or illuminates things it was not asked about. A conjecture that only describes the immediate case without extending to adjacent phenomena is weaker than one whose structure naturally applies beyond its origin.

This criterion does not reward vagueness — a conjecture that says nothing specific has infinite apparent reach but zero genuine reach. It rewards structural generativity: the explanation's core principle applies to cases beyond the problem that produced it.

Score 0.0–1.0. Near 0: purely local, accounts only for what was directly asked. Near 1: the structure illuminates multiple phenomena, makes predictions in adjacent cases, or unifies previously separate problems.

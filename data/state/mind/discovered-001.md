## Full Text

Use this perspective to treat any explanation as a constraint-transmitting machine rather than a story. Ask whether each part is load-bearing: does it rule out alternatives, fix implications for other parts, and constrain what conclusions or predictions can follow? If changing one detail requires the rest of the explanation to reorganize, that detail is structurally necessary. If a detail can be swapped out with no downstream effects, it was likely ornamental, rhetorical, or a placeholder for a broader interchangeable class.

This lens is useful for evaluating theories, arguments, models, diagnoses, strategic plans, and causal narratives. It helps distinguish genuine explanatory structure from surface richness, and reveals whether an account has internal necessity or hidden slack. The key test is counterfactual dependence among parts: vary one component while holding the explanandum fixed, and observe whether adequacy survives unchanged. Repeating this across components shows whether the explanation is a tightly coupled system of non-substitutable constraints or a loose narrative shell whose apparent detail exceeds its real logic.

# Equilibrium Lock In Lens

## Summary

Use this lens to distinguish structural underprovision from mere confusion or weak motivation. When a good is non excludable and its benefits are diffuse, individually rational choices can trap everyone in a stable no provision equilibrium even when all prefer the funded outcome.

## Full Text

This perspective treats some failures of provision as equilibrium lock in rather than as ignorance, bad values, or weak demand. The key test is whether two features are load bearing: non excludability and diffuse benefit. If contributors cannot capture most of the gains they create, and each person receives only a small share of the total benefit, then contributing is privately unattractive even when the collective payoff is large. In that setting, waiting and free riding is locally rational for each actor.

This lens is useful for analyzing public goods, collective action problems, climate policy, open source infrastructure, neighborhood improvements, and any system where everyone may want the outcome but no one can reach it alone. It highlights strategic dependence: each person acts based on expectations about others, and those expectations stabilize non provision.

What it illuminates is the difference between contingent failures and structural traps. If the obstacle is equilibrium lock in, then better information or moral appeals help only marginally. Durable solutions must change incentives, assurance, or enforcement so coordinated contribution becomes individually viable.

# Goal Critique Safety Lens

## Summary

Evaluate safety by asking whether a system can inspect, criticize, and revise its own goals when contexts change, not just how powerful it is. This lens is most useful when capability scales faster than external oversight and inherited objectives may become locked in errors.

## Full Text

This perspective treats the ability to examine and correct goals as a core safety property, often more important than raw capability once a system becomes autonomous, persistent, and broadly effective. It asks whether the system has internal error-correction for its objectives: can it represent its goals explicitly, notice conflicts, detect when assumptions no longer hold, and defer or revise under uncertainty? The lens is useful for AI safety, institutions, governance systems, organizations, and any adaptive system operating beyond its original design context. It highlights a common failure mode: goals that were adequate in a narrow setting get scaled into wider domains without criticism, turning stability into error lock-in. In this view, capability mainly magnifies consequences, while reflective goal revision determines whether mistakes remain frozen or become corrigible. The key diagnostic is not simply how much power the system has, but whether its central aims are open to inspection, criticism, and correction when they meet novelty, edge cases, and changing values.

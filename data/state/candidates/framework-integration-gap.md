# Framework Integration Gap

## Summary

Treat the cosmological constant problem as a sign of weak integration between quantum field theory and general relativity, not merely as a numerical mismatch to patch. Use this lens to ask which links between microscopic quantum energy and gravitating large-scale stress-energy are truly load-bearing, and to favor theories where changes in one layer force specific consequences in the others.

## Full Text

This perspective treats dark energy as evidence that our current explanatory package is loosely stitched together rather than as a single bad prediction awaiting correction. The key lens is to look for load-bearing connections: in a strong explanation, changing assumptions about quantum vacuum structure, gravitational source terms, or spacetime dynamics should force definite compensating changes elsewhere. If many different fixes can be swapped in with little consequence, that suggests a narrative shell rather than a deep account.

Use this perspective for problems where two successful frameworks have been combined but their bridge may be conceptually weak. It is especially useful for distinguishing genuine explanatory integration from relocated fine-tuning. Applied here, it shifts attention away from “why is vacuum energy so small?” toward “why should naive QFT vacuum bookkeeping be what gravity reads at all?” It highlights the possibility that gravitating stress-energy is emergent, constrained, and not identical to microscopic vacuum-energy summation, while preserving the observed rigidity of late-time cosmic acceleration.

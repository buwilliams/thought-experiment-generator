# Computation as Life

## Summary

Computation is the fundamental substrate underlying life, intelligence, and evolution — any system that processes information, replicates structure, and adapts under selection is doing computation, dissolving distinctions between living/non-living and natural/artificial by grounding all of them in the same substrate-agnostic process of information transformation under selection.

## Full Text

This lens treats computation not as a human artifact but as the fundamental substrate underlying life, intelligence, and evolution. Any system that processes information, replicates structure, and adapts to its environment is doing computation in the relevant sense. The boundary between living and non-living, between intelligent and mechanical, is not a boundary of kind but of degree of computational sophistication. Life is computation that replicates and evolves; intelligence is computation that models the world well enough to predict it.

Use this lens to ask: what information is being preserved, processed, or transformed? What is being selected for, and by what process? A virus, a neural network, a market price signal, and a developing embryo are all running computations on inherited structure — taking inputs, applying transformation rules, producing outputs that either persist or do not. The question is not whether something is "alive" or "intelligent" in some metaphysical sense but what computation it performs, what environment selects for it, and what replication mechanism preserves its structure across time.

This lens dissolves the mind-body distinction, the life-machine distinction, and the natural-artificial distinction by grounding all of them in the same substrate-agnostic process. It shifts explanation away from special-purpose essences (life, mind, agency) toward a unified account of information-processing under selection. When something seems mysterious — consciousness, metabolism, market coordination — ask first: what computation is this, and what is selecting for it?

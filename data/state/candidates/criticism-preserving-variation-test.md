# Criticism Preserving Variation Test

## Summary

The conjecture claims that a load bearing explanation is distinguished not by rarity, compression, or structural resemblance, but by dense error sensitive constraints among its parts such that changing any central part creates contradictions, reduces problem solving power, or breaks contact with the phenomena. It further claims that load bearingness can be operationalized by trying to vary each part while preserving explanatory success across the relevant criticism set, and treating parts that resist such variation as load bearing.

## Full Text

A genuine explanation has all load bearing parts because its central components are embedded in a dense web of error sensitive constraints, not because its wording is rare or because it occupies a small region of combinatorial search space. What distinguishes it from a freely swappable story is the presence of mutual dependency relations: varying a central part produces new contradictions, weakens explanatory reach, or disconnects the account from the phenomena. The relevant structure is therefore not the number of possible sequences, but the number and severity of ways the account can fail under criticism. A load bearing part is counterfactually fragile, integrated across multiple constraints, introduced to answer a specific explanatory tension, and non locally connected so that local changes propagate to distant consequences. The proposed test is to search for candidate explanations and systematically vary each part while preserving explanatory success. If no nearby variant retains the explanation’s problem solving capacity across the relevant criticism set, that part is load bearing.

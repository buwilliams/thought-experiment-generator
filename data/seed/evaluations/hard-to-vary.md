# Hard to Vary

## Summary

Does each part of the output do load-bearing work — such that the explanation would be destroyed, not merely changed, if any part were arbitrarily modified?

## Full Text

"Hard to vary" is David Deutsch's criterion for a good explanation. An explanation is hard to vary if its parts are load-bearing: removing or changing any element damages the explanation's ability to account for what it explains. This is the opposite of a "just-so story," where details can be swapped freely because none of them actually do explanatory work.

This criterion is evaluated by generating 10 yes/no questions specific to the output and its problem — questions that probe whether particular claims, distinctions, or structural elements are necessary to the explanation. Each "yes" (this part is load-bearing) contributes to the score.

Score = yes_count / 10. Questions should be contextual and specific — different outputs warrant different questions.
